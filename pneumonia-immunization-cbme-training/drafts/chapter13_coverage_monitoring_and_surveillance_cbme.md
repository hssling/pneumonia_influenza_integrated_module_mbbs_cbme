## 13.1 IMMUNIZATION SURVEILLANCE SYSTEMS

### Purpose and Components
- **Disease Surveillance:** Track vaccine-preventable diseases (pneumonia, influenza)
- **Coverage Monitoring:** Population immunity assessment through vaccination records
- **AEFI Surveillance:** Adverse events tracking and analysis
- **Program Evaluation:** Continuous quality improvement and performance measurement

### Surveillance Systems in India

#### **Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP)**
- **National Network:** 700+ districts covered
- **Daily Reporting:** Outbreak alerts and epidemic intelligence
- **Laboratory Network:** 70 medical colleges and 10 regional labs
- **Data Integration:** Immunization data with disease surveillance

#### **Measles-Rubella Surveillance System**
- **Active Surveillance:** Community-based case finding
- **Laboratory Confirmation:** Serological and PCR testing
- **Case Classification:** Epidemiological and laboratory criteria
- **Elimination Monitoring:** Towards measles elimination by 2023

#### **Vaccine Safety Surveillance**
- **CoWIN AEFI Module:** Real-time adverse event reporting
- **Causality Assessment:** Brighton collaboration case definitions
- **Signal Detection:** Statistical analysis for safety concerns
- **Public Communication:** Transparent reporting and risk communication

## 13.2 COVERAGE MEASUREMENT METHODOLOGIES

### Administrative Coverage Calculation
- **Numerator:** Number of vaccine doses administered to target population
- **Denominator:** Estimated eligible population for vaccination
- **Data Sources:** Immunization registers and electronic systems
- **Frequency:** Monthly aggregation and quarterly reporting

#### Coverage Indicators
- **Basic Coverage:** Doses administered compared to target population
- **Valid Coverage:** Adjusted for operational challenges and denials
- **Timely Coverage:** Vaccinations given within recommended schedule
- **Equity Coverage:** Disaggregated by gender, income, geography

### Survey-Based Coverage Assessment

#### Cluster Sampling Surveys
- **Methodology:** WHO/CDC recommended 30-cluster, 7-household surveys
- **Sample Size:** Minimum 210 children per district
- **Data Collection:** Interview-based vaccination verification
- **Validity:** Concurrent with administrative data for triangulation

#### Rapid Coverage Monitoring
- **Purpose:** Quick assessment during outbreaks or campaigns
- **Methods:** Lot quality assurance sampling (LQAS)
- **Application:** Mopping-up campaigns and supplementary activities
- **Advantage:** Cost-effective and rapid results

### Serological Coverage Surveys
- **Population Immunity:** Serological testing for antibody levels
- **Effectiveness Measurement:** Functional immunity against infection
- **Vaccine Response:** Geometric mean titre (GMT) assessment
- **Program Adjustment:** Based on immunogenicity data

## 13.3 VACCINE-PREVENTABLE DISEASE SURVEILLANCE

### Pneumonia Surveillance Systems

#### **Acute Respiratory Infection (ARI) Surveillance**
- **Case Definition:** ARI rates in under-5 year old children
- **Severe Pneumonia:** Fast breathing + chest in-drawing
- **Very Severe Disease:** Respiratory distress or danger signs
- **Reporting:** Weekly from all health facilities

#### **Invasive Pneumococcal Disease (IPD) Surveillance**
- **Laboratory Network:** Blood culture confirmation at sentinel sites
- **Serotype Determination:** Pneumococcal isolates typing
- **Antibiotic Resistance:** MIC testing for penicillin and ceftriaxone
- **Age Groups:** Special focus on under-2 years and immunocompromised

### Influenza-Like Illness (ILI) Surveillance

#### **ILI Network in India**
- **123 Sentinel Sites:** Distributed across all states
- **Weekly Reporting:** Cases and samples for virological testing
- **Laboratory Diagnosis:** RT-PCR for influenza viruses
- **Epidemiologic Data:** Clinical symptoms and vaccination history

#### **Severe Acute Respiratory Infection (SARI)**
- **Case Definition:** Fever + cough + shortness of breath
- **Hospital-Based:** Active surveillance in designated hospitals
- **Virological Testing:** Influenza, respiratory syncytial virus
- **Pandemic Preparedness:** Global surveillance integration

### Measles Surveillance

#### **Measles Elimination Strategy (MES)**
- **Case-Based Surveillance:** Every suspected case must be investigated
- **Laboratory Network:** Six national labs for confirmation
- **Follow-up:** 3 close contacts per case investigated
- **Elimination Indicators:** Incidence <1 per million population

## 13.4 MONITORING AND EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### Input Indicators
- **Vaccine Availability:** Stock-outs, vaccine wastage rates
- **Human Resources:** Trained staff per population
- **Logistics:** Cold chain infrastructure and maintenance
- **Financial Resources:** Budget allocation per capita

### Process Indicators
- **Session Conduct:** Planned vs actual sessions
- **Vaccine Administration:** Correct technique and dose
- **Documentation:** Register completion and data accuracy
- **Linkages:** MCTS and other health systems

### Output Indicators
- **Coverage:** Percentage of target population vaccinated
- **Drop-out Rates:** Percentage who completed schedules
- **Timeliness:** Vaccines given according to schedule
- **Equity:** Coverage by socioeconomic groups

### Outcome Indicators
- **Disease Incidence:** Vaccine-preventable disease rates
- **Mortality Rates:** Reduction in pneumonia deaths
- **Hospital Admissions:** Reduction in hospitalization rates
- **Economic Impact:** Cost savings from prevented diseases

## 13.5 OUTBREAK INVESTIGATION AND RESPONSE

### Outbreak Investigation Protocol

#### **Step 1: Detection and Confirmation**
- **Signal Detection:** Unusual increase in cases or cluster
- **Laboratory Confirmation:** Aetiological diagnosis establishment
- **Case Definition:** Standard case classification
- **Background Rate:** Comparison with expected incidence

#### **Step 2: Epidemiological Assessment**
- **Person:** Age, sex, geographical distribution
- **Time:** Epidemic curve construction
- **Place:** Geographic mapping of cases
- **Host Factors:** Risk factor identification

#### **Step 3: Response Implementation**
- **Immediate Actions:** Case isolation and contact tracing
- **Vaccination Campaign:** Rapid vaccination in affected areas
- **IPC Measures:** Personal protection and environmental control
- **Communication:** Public awareness and risk communication

### Pneumonia Outbreak Investigation
- **Source**: Water contamination (Legionella), food-borne (Campylobacter)
- **Vectors**: Zoonotic transmission, person-to-person spread
- **Amplifiers**: Crowding in institutions, poor ventilation
- **Response**: Antibiotic prophylaxis, vaccination campaigns

### Influenza Outbreak Investigation
- **Virus Strain**: Hemagglutinin-neuraminidase typing
- **Antiviral Susceptibility**: Resistance monitoring
- **Vaccine Match**: Circulating strains vs vaccine composition
- **Public Health Measures**: Social distancing, mask mandates

## 13.6 DATA MANAGEMENT AND ANALYSIS

### Information Systems

#### **Electronic Immunization Registry**
- **CoWIN Platform:** COVID-19 vaccination now expanded to all vaccines
- **Real-time Data:** Individual vaccination records with QR codes
- **Interoperability:** Integration with HMIS and other systems
- **Mobile Access:** Citizens can view vaccination history

#### **Mobile Tracking System (MCTS)**
- **Pregnant Women Tracking:** Immunization status monitoring
- **Birth Notifications:** Immediate post-delivery vaccinations
- **Child Tracking:** Up to 5 years age group
- **SMS Reminders:** Automated message alerts

### Data Quality Assurance
- **Internal Consistency:** Age-appropriate vaccination validation
- **External Comparison:** Comparison with surveys and HMIS data
- **Logical Checks:** Impossible date and dose combinations eliminated
- **Auditing:** Periodic physical verification of registers

### Statistical Analysis
- **Descriptive Statistics:** Coverage rates, trends over time
- **Regression Analysis:** Factors affecting coverage
- **Geospatial Analysis:** Hotspot identification and mapping
- **Forecasting:** Vaccine requirement estimation

## 13.7 PROGRAM EFFECTIVENESS MEASUREMENT

### Impact Assessment

#### Direct Impact
- **Disease Reduction:** Pneumonia incidence and hospitalization rates
- **Mortality Decrease:** Overall and age-specific mortality
- **Antibiotic Reduction:** Decreased inappropriate antibiotic use
- **Healthcare Costs:** Savings from prevented hospitalizations

#### Indirect Impact
- **Herd Immunity:** Protection of unvaccinated populations
- **Societal Benefits:** Reduced absenteeism, economic productivity
- **Equity Improvements:** Reduction in health disparities
- **Long-term Benefits:** Decreased chronic lung disease burden

### Cost-Effectiveness Analysis
- **Cost per DALY:** Disability-adjusted life years averted
- **Incremental Cost:** Additional investment for improved coverage
- **Return on Investment:** Healthcare system savings
- **Societal Perspective:** Including productivity gains

### Quality Improvement Cycles
- **Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA):** Continuous improvement methodology
- **Root Cause Analysis:** PROBLEM identification and solution implementation
- **Benchmarking:** Comparison with best-performing districts/states
- **Sustainability Planning:** Long-term financing and political commitment
