## 3.1 HISTORY TAKING IN PNEUMONIA

### Essential Components of History

#### Patient Demographics
- **Age:** Children vs Adults (significant differences)
- **Gender:** Some pathogens show predilection
- **Occupation:** Healthcare workers, farmers, veterinarians
- **Habitat:** Institutionalized, homeless populations

#### Symptom Onset and Progression
- **Acute onset (<7 days):** Typical for bacterial pneumonia
- **Subacute onset:** Suggests atypical pathogens
- **Chronic progression:** May indicate tuberculosis, malignancy

#### Specific Respiratory Symptoms
- **Cough:** Productive (typical) vs dry (atypical/mycoplasma)
- **Sputum character:** Rusty (S. pneumoniae), mucopurulent, bloody (hemoptysis)
- **Dyspnea:** Progressive respiratory distress
- **Chest pain:** Pleuritic pain suggests pleural involvement

#### Associated Systemic Symptoms
- **Fever/Chills:** High-grade fever with rigors (S. pneumoniae)
- **Sweating:** Night sweats (tuberculosis differential)
- **Fatigue/Malaise:** Non-specific viral-like symptoms
- **Myalgia:** Muscle aches common in influenza
