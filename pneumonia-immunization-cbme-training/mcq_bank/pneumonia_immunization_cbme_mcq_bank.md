## Pneumonia Epidemiology & Pathophysiology (Chapter 1)

**Q1.** What is the most common cause of death due to infectious diseases worldwide?
A) Tuberculosis
B) HIV/AIDS
C) Malaria
D) Pneumonia

**Answer: D) Pneumonia** (2.5 million deaths annually)

**Q2.** Which age group has the highest pneumonia mortality rate?
A) 1-4 years
B) 5-14 years
C) 15-49 years
D) 50-64 years

**Answer: A) 1-4 years** (>90% of deaths occur in <5 years)

**Q3.** Most common bacterial pathogen in community-acquired pneumonia?
A) Staphylococcus aureus
B) Streptococcus pneumoniae
C) Haemophilus influenzae
D) Klebsiella pneumoniae

**Answer: B) Streptococcus pneumoniae** (30-50% of CAP cases)

## Pneumonia Classification (Chapter 2)

**Q4.** Which is NOT a criteria for healthcare-associated pneumonia (HCAP)?
A) Recent hospitalization within 90 days
B) Nursing home residence
C) Chemotherapy within past month
D) Diabetes mellitus diagnosis

**Answer: D) Diabetes mellitus** (HCAP focuses on healthcare exposure)

**Q5.** CURB-65 score helps assess which aspect of pneumonia?
A) Pathogen identification
B) Treatment duration
C) Disease severity
D) Cost-effectiveness

**Answer: C) Disease severity** (Clinical prediction tool)

**Q6.** Which pneumonia type occurs >48 hours after hospital admission?
A) Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP)
B) Hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP)
C) Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP)
D) Healthcare-associated pneumonia (HCAP)

**Answer: B) Hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP)** (≥48 hours after admission)

## Clinical Diagnosis & Assessment (Chapter 3)

**Q7.** Which physical finding is characteristic of consolidation in pneumonia?
A) Wheezing
B) Decreased breath sounds
C) Inspiratory click
D) Pleural friction rub

**Answer: B) Decreased breath sounds** (Consolidation dulls breath sounds)

**Q8.** Which laboratory parameter is most useful for guiding antibiotic therapy?
A) Serum creatinine
B) Procalcitonin
C) Blood urea nitrogen
D) Serum bilirubin

**Answer: B) Procalcitonin** (Biomarker for bacterial infection)

## Antibiotics & Treatment Guidelines (Chapter 6)

**Q9.** First-line antibiotic for outpatient CAP in otherwise healthy patient?
A) Ciprofloxacin
B) Amoxicillin
C) Vancomycin
D) Azithromycin

**Answer: B) Amoxicillin** (500mg every 8 hours for 5-7 days)

**Q10.** Which antibiotic covers both pneumococcal and atypical pathogens?
A) Azithromycin
B) Ciprofloxacin
C) Metronidazole
D) Gentamicin

**Answer: A) Azithromycin** (Macrolide class covers both)

## Pneumococcal Vaccination (Chapter 10)

**Q11.** How many serotypes does PCV13 vaccine cover?
A) 7
B) 13
C) 23
D) 43

**Answer: B) 13** (Conjugate vaccine, 13 serotypes)

**Q12.** Pneumococcal polysaccharide vaccine (PPSV23) provides protective immunity through:
A) T-cell immunity
B) B-cell antibodies
C) Macrophage activation
D) Complement fixation

**Answer: B) B-cell antibodies** (T-cell independent response)

**Q13.** Recommended pneumococcal vaccination schedule for healthy children?
A) Birth, 6 weeks, 10 weeks
B) Birth, 1.5 months, 2.5 months, 3.5 months
C) 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 12-15 months
D) 9 months, 18 months, 5 years

**Answer: C) 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 12-15 months** (WHO schedule)

## Influenza Vaccination (Chapter 11)

**Q14.** Influenza vaccine is updated annually to protect against which circulating strains?
A) 1 subtype
B) 3 subtypes (2A + 1B)
C) 4 subtypes (3A + 1B)
D) All subtypes

**Answer: B) 3 subtypes** (2 type A, 1 type B)

**Q15.** Most common adverse event after influenza vaccination?
A) Guillain-Barré syndrome
B) Severe allergic reaction
C) Mild soreness at injection site
D) High-grade fever

**Answer: C) Mild soreness at injection site** (Most common, usually resolves in 1-2 days)

## Immunization Implementation (Chapter 12)

**Q16.** Which indicator measures the proportion of eligible population who received vaccination?
A) Vaccination efficacy
B) Vaccination effectiveness
C) Vaccination uptake
D) Vaccination timeliness

**Answer: C) Vaccination uptake** (Coverage measurement)

**Q17.** Cold chain maintenance is most critical for which vaccines?
A) Oral polio vaccine
B) Bacillus Calmette-Guérin
C) Measles-mumps-rubella
D) All of the above

**Answer: A) Oral polio vaccine** (Most temperature-sensitive)

## Vaccine Safety & Adverse Events (Chapter 14)

**Q18.** Most common minor adverse event after vaccination?
A) Severe allergic reaction
B) Fever >103°F
C) Mild pain at injection site
D) Convulsions

**Answer: C) Mild pain** (Local reaction, resolves quickly)

**Q19.** What percentage of adverse events should trigger vaccine safety investigation?
A) 1 or more
B) 5 or more
C) 10 or more
D) Any clustering

**Answer: A) 1 or more** (Each event requires careful evaluation)

## Program Evaluation (Chapter 15)

**Q20.** What does "immunization coverage" assess?
A) Disease incidence rates
B) Vaccine cost-effectiveness
C) Population vaccination uptake
D) Individual immune response

**Answer: C) Population vaccination uptake** (Percentage vaccinated in target group)
