# Pneumonia-Vaccination Integrative Case Study: Comprehensive CBME Assessment

**Clinical Scenario for MBBS Phase 3 Part 1 Integration Class**

## **Patient Profile**

**Name:** Mrs. Lakshmi Devi (65-year-old female)  
**Occupation:** Retired school teacher  
**Presenting Complaint:** Acute onset respiratory distress  
**Date of Admission:** October 15, 2025

---

## **History of Presenting Illness**

Mrs. Lakshmi was well until 3 days ago when she developed:
- High-grade fever with chills
- Dry cough productive of whitish sputum
- Progressive shortness of breath
- Chest pain on right side, worse with deep inspiration
- Fatigue and generalized weakness

**Symptoms Timeline:**
- Day 1: Fever (101°F), mild cough
- Day 2: Cough increased, dyspnea on exertion
- Day 3: Severe dyspnea, pleuritic pain, called ambulance

**Past Medical History:**
- Hypertension (on telmisartan 40mg daily)
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (on metformin 500mg BID)
- No previous lung disease
- No smoking history

**Medications:**
- Telmisartan 40mg once daily
- Metformin 500mg twice daily
- No recent antibiotic use

**Vaccination History:**
- Received COVID-19 vaccination (2 doses)
- Never received pneumococcal or influenza vaccines
- Received adult tetanus-diphtheria booster 5 years ago

**Social History:**
- Lives alone in a two-bedroom house
- Retired school teacher, active in community
- No pets, non-smoker
- No recent travel or exposure to sick people

---

## **Physical Examination**

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: 101.8°F (38.8°C)
- Heart Rate: 98 bpm, regular
- Blood Pressure: 130/85 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 28 breaths/min
- Oxygen Saturation: 90% on room air
- BMI: 28 kg/m² (overweight)

**General Examination:**
- Alert and oriented, appears in respiratory distress
- Slight pallor, no cyanosis
- No lymphadenopathy or pedal edema

**Respiratory System:**
- Tachypnea at rest
- Right sided chest movement decreased
- Trachea central
- Apical impulse palpable at 5th ICS, left MCL

**Right Hemithorax:**
- Dull percussion note in lower zone
- Bronchial breath sounds heard in lower zone
- Increased vocal resonance and tactile fremitus
- Crackles in lower zone, no wheeze
- No pleural rub

**Left Hemithorax:**
- Normal percussion and breath sounds

**Other Systems:** Unremarkable

---

## **Diagnostic Investigations**

### **Chest X-Ray (PA and Lateral Views):**
- Consolidation in right lower lobe (RLL)
- Air bronchogram visible
- Costophrenic angle clear
- No pleural effusion

### **Blood Tests:**
- WBC: 15,200/μL (Neutrophils 78%, Lymphocytes 15%)
- Hb: 12.1 g/dL
- Platelets: 2.8 lakhs/μL
- CRP: 124 mg/L (elevated)
- Procalcitonin: 0.45 ng/mL (suggestive of bacterial infection)
- Blood Sugar: 142 mg/dL (FBS)

### **Sputum Examination:**
- Gram stain: Gram positive diplococci in pairs, lancet-shaped
- Culture: Streptococcus pneumoniae (sensitive to penicillin)

### **Other Tests:**
- Blood culture: Pending
- Arterial blood gas: pH 7.35, PaO2 68 mmHg on room air
- ECG: Sinus tachycardia, no acute ischemic changes

---

## **Clinical Diagnosis**

**Right Lower Lobe Community-Acquired Pneumonia**
- Caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae
- CURB-65 Score: 1 (Age >65 = 1 point)
- Moderate severity, inpatient treatment indicated

## **Management Plan**

### **Immediate Management:**
1. **Supplemental Oxygen:** Titrate to maintain SpO2 >94%
2. **Antibiotic Therapy:** Ceftriaxone 1g IV daily + Azithromycin 500mg IV daily
3. **Fluid Balance:** Monitor intake and output
4. **Vital Signs:** Monitor every 4 hours
5. **Chest Physiotherapy:** Breathing exercises when stable

### **Supportive Care:**
1. **Hydration:** IV fluids initially, oral when tolerated
2. **Nutrition:** High-calorie, protein-rich diet
3. **Mobilization:** Early mobilization when stable
4. **Pain Control:** Paracetamol for fever and pain

### **Vaccination Planning:**
1. **Pneumococcal Vaccination (Day 2 of admission):**
   - PCV13 today
   - PPSV23 after 8 weeks
   - Benefits: Reduces risk of future pneumococcal infections by 50-70%

2. **Influenza Vaccination (Before discharge):**
   - Annual vaccination with quadrivalent vaccine
   - Schedule: October-November (northern hemisphere)

### **Discharge Planning:**
1. **Antibiotic Course:** Complete 7-day course
2. **Follow-up Chest X-ray:** 4-6 weeks after discharge
3. **Smoking Cessation Counseling:** If applicable
4. **Vaccination Adherence:** Pneumococcal and influenza booster doses
5. **Health Maintenance:** Regular blood sugar and BP monitoring

## **Learning Points**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
1. **Severity Assessment:** CURB-65 is essential for deciding treatment setting
2. **Microbiological Diagnosis:** Sputum Gram stain guides empirical therapy
3. **Vaccination Opportunity:** Hospitalization is ideal time to vaccinate high-risk patients
4. **Sequential Vaccination:** Better protection with PCV13 + PPSV23 in elderly
5. **Prevention Focus:** Pneumonia is largely preventable through vaccination

### **Public Health Implications:**
1. **Economic Impact:** Pneumonia costs healthcare systems billions annually
2. **Preventable Burden:** 60% of pneumococcal disease is vaccine-preventable
3. **Health Equity:** Vaccination programs reduce disparities in pneumonia outcomes
4. **Surveillance:** Monitoring trends guides public health interventions
