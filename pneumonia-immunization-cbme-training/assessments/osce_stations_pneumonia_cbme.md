# OSCE Stations: Pneumonia Treatment & Immunization CBME Module
**MBBS Phase 3 Part 1 Integration Class - Practical Assessment**

---

## 🎯 OSCE STATION OVERVIEW

### Purpose
- **Competency Assessment:** Practical skills evaluation using workplace-based scenarios
- **Clinical Decision-Making:** Patient interaction and management simulations
- **Communication Skills:** Professional interaction with patients, families, and colleagues
- **Time Management:** Efficient task completion under time constraints

### Structure
- **Duration:** 5-8 minutes per station + 2 minutes for feedback/transition
- **Format:** Circuit of 6-8 stations focusing on pneumonia competencies
- **Scoring:** Standardized checklists with clear performance criteria
- **Environment:** Simulated clinical settings with standardized patients/mannequins

### Equipment Needed
- **Mannequin/Respiratory Simulator:** For examination practice
- **Sample Medications:** Antibiotic vials, syringes (marked, not functional)
- **Vaccination Supplies:** Syringes, needles, vaccine vials (training versions)
- **Case Histories:** Patient cards with clinical scenarios
- **Assessment Forms:** Checklists for each station

## 📋 STATION 1: RESPIRATORY SYSTEM EXAMINATION
**Time: 6 minutes | Patient: Standardized patient with pneumonia**

### Scenario
**45-year-old male with cough, fever, and shortness of breath for 2 days. Vital signs: T-38.5°C, RR-28/min, BP-120/75, HR-95/min, SpO2-94% on room air.**

### Station Instructions
Perform a focused respiratory system examination on the patient. Demonstrate proper technique and identify key clinical findings.

### Assessment Checklist

#### Communication & Professionalism (20%)
- [ ] Introduces self and explains procedure clearly
- [ ] Obtains patient consent and ensures comfort
- [ ] Maintains patient dignity and privacy
- [ ] Professional demeanor throughout

#### History Taking (20%)
- [ ] Asks about duration and nature of symptoms
- [ ] Inquires about relevant risk factors
- [ ] Checks immunization status
- [ ] Assesses medication allergies

#### Physical Examination (50%)
- [ ] Correctly performs inspection (work of breathing, cyanosis)
- [ ] Proper palpation technique (tactile fremitus, tenderness)
- [ ] Auscultation with correct stethoscope placement:
  - [ ] Anterior chest (all lobes)
  - [ ] Posterior chest (comparative)
  - [ ] Identifies bronchial breath sounds & crepitations

#### Documentation (10%)
- [ ] Appropriate use of PPE throughout
- [ ] Correctly documents findings
- [ ] Identifies need for further investigations

**Total Score: __/100 | Pass Mark: 65%**

### Debrief Points
- Discuss findings and their significance
- Explain next steps in management
- Address patient concerns appropriately

#### Management Planning (35%)

#### Communication (10%)

- [ ] Justifies clinical decisions with evidence

## 📝 OSCE IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES

### Session Structure
- **Total Duration:** 90 minutes (60 minutes stations + 30 minutes feedback)
- **Station Rotation:** Sequential or random assignment
- **Examiner Assignment:** Dual examiners per station split function
- **Patient Management:** Professional SPs with standardized responses

### Examiner Training
- **Calibration Sessions:** Score standardization before OSCE
- **Role Clarity:** Different examiners for different stations
- **Feedback Training:** Constructive feedback delivery skills
- **Calibration Checks:** Regular reliability assessment

### Quality Assurance
- **Station Calibration:** Consistent scenarios and expectations
- **Examiner Reliability:** Inter-rater agreement monitoring
- **Student Experience:** Anonymous feedback collection
- **Continuous Improvement:** Annual OSCE review and updates
