## Slide 3: What is Pneumonia?
**Acute infection of lung parenchyma characterized by:**

- **Consolidation of alveoli**
- **Inflammatory exudate accumulation**
- **Impaired gas exchange**
- **Typical vs Atypical presentations**
- **Community vs Hospital-acquired**

*Visual: Chest X-ray showing lobar consolidation*

---

## Slide 4: Pneumonia Classification

| **Category** | **Definition** | **Examples** | **Microbiology** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Community-Acquired** | <48 hours hospital admission | Healthy adults | S. pneumoniae, M. pneumoniae |
| **Hospital-Acquired** | ≥48 hours admission | ICU patients | MDR pathogens, P. aeruginosa |
| **Ventilator-Associated** | ≥48-72 hours intubation | Mechanically ventilated | ESBL producers, MRSA |
| **Healthcare-Associated** | Recent healthcare exposure | LTCF residents | Mixed bacterial flora |

**📊 Key Statistics:**

- **Annual cases:** 156 million worldwide
- **Annual deaths:** 2.5 million people
- **Leading cause:** Top infectious disease killer
- **Age distribution:** 71% in children <5 years
- **Adult mortality:** 600,000 deaths annually

## Slide 7: Etiological Agents
**Microbial Spectrum in CAP:**

1. **Streptococcus pneumoniae** - 25-40% (most common bacterial)
2. **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** - 15-30% (school-age peak)
3. **Chlamydophila pneumoniae** - 10-15% (young adults)
4. **Haemophilus influenzae** - 5-15% (COPD patients)
5. **Legionella pneumophila** - 2-10% (water systems)
6. **Respiratory viruses** - 15-20% (winter epidemics)
7. **Staphylococcus aureus** - 2-5% (post-viral)

*Pie chart showing distribution of pathogens*

## Slide 8: Risk Factors for Pneumonia

**Host Factors:**
- **Age extremes:** <2 years and >65 years
- **Underlying diseases:** COPD, diabetes, heart failure
- **Immunodeficiency:** HIV, malignancy, immunosuppression
- **Lifestyle:** Smoking, alcoholism, poor nutrition

**Environmental Factors:**
- **Air pollution:** Indoor smoke, particulate matter
- **Crowding:** Urban slums, dormitories
- **Malnutrition:** Vitamin A, zinc deficiency
- **Seasonal:** Winter epidemics, monsoon peaks

---

## Slide 9: Pathophysiology of Pneumonia

**Four-Step Sequence:**

1. **Colonization** - Pathogen adheres to epithelium
2. **Invasion** - Bacterial multiplication in alveoli
3. **Inflammation** - Cytokine release, neutrophil migration
4. **Consolidation** - Exudate fills air spaces, impairs gas exchange

*Animated diagram showing pathogenesis sequence*

---

## Slide 10: Host Defense Mechanisms

**Anatomical Barriers:**
- **Upper airways:** Nasal filtration, mucociliary clearance
- **Cough reflex:** Prevents aspiration
- **Bronchial tree:** Hair cells, mucus production

**Cellular Immunity:**
- **Alveolar macrophages:** Phagocytosis
- **Neutrophils:** Acute inflammatory response
- **Complement system:** Opsonization, chemotaxis

**Humoral Immunity:**
- **Antibodies:** Serotype-specific protection
- **Immunoglobulins:** IgG, IgM, IgA
- **Memory cells:** Long-term immunity

---

## Slide 11: Aspiration vs Inhalation Routes

**Aspiration Pneumonia:**
- **Most common route:** Oropharyngeal contents
- **Risk factors:** CNS disorders, dysphagia, unconsciousness
- **Anaerobic bacteria:** Predominant in elderly
- **Chemical pneumonitis:** Gastric acid injury

**Inhalation Pneumonia:**
- **Droplet nuclei:** Mycobacteria, viruses, Legionella
- **Airborne transmission:** RSV, influenza aerosols
- **Smaller inoculum:** Survives upper airway defenses
- **Diffuse involvement:** Often bilateral distribution

---

## Slide 12: Typical vs Atypical Pneumonia

| **Feature** | **Typical Pneumonia** | **Atypical Pneumonia** |
|---|---|---|
| **Onset** | Acute, sudden | Gradual, subacute |
| **Fever** | High, shaking chills | Low-grade |
| **Cough** | Productive, purulent | Dry, persistent |
| **Chest pain** | Pleuritic | Minimal |
| **X-ray** | Lobar consolidation | Interstitial pattern |
| **White cells** | Elevated neutrophils | Normal/mild elevation |

## Slide 13: Clinical Manifestations

**Respiratory Symptoms:**
- **Dyspnea:** Progressive shortness of breath
- **Cough:** Dry → productive with purulent sputum
- **Chest pain:** Pleuritic, worse on inspiration
- **Hemoptysis:** Rusty sputum (S. pneumoniae)

**Systemic Symptoms:**
- **Fever:** High-grade with chills/rigors
- **Fatigue:** Generalized weakness
- **Anorexia:** Loss of appetite
- **Myalgias:** Muscle aches and pains

---

## Slide 14: Complications of Pneumonia

**Respiratory Complications:**
- **Pleural effusion/empyema**
- **Lung abscess**
- **Pneumothorax**
- **ARDS**

**Systemic Complications:**
- **Sepsis/septic shock**
- **Acute kidney injury**
- **Heart complications**
- **Metabolic disorders**
