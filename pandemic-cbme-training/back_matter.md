### **Appendix A: WHO Pandemic Classification and Risk Assessment**

#### **Table A1: WHO Pandemic Phases**

| **Phase** | **Influenza Pandemic** | **Global Risk Level** | **Key Actions** |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **1** | No animal influenza virus circulating among animals causing infections in humans | **Low** | Enhanced animal health surveillance |
| **2** | Animal influenza virus circulating but no human cases | **Low-Moderate** | Research on virus characteristics |
| **3** | Animal influenza virus with sporadic human cases | **Moderate** | Prepare response strategies |
| **4** | Sustained human-to-human transmission | **High** | Intensify containment measures |
| **5** | Sustained transmission in two WHO regions | **High** | Implement global response |
| **6** | Sustained transmission globally | **Extremely High** | Fully activated pandemic response |

### **Appendix B: Disaster Management Act 2005 and Pandemic Response**

#### **Key Sections Relevant to Pandemics**

**Section 5: National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA):**
- Chaired by Prime Minister
- Coordinates national disaster management
- Develops national disaster management policy
- Lays down guidelines for disaster management

**Section 10: District Disaster Management Authority:**
- Created in all states headed by District Collector
- Empowered to take preventive measures
- Coordinate response activities at district level
- Control over district revenues for emergency response

**Section 14: National Executive Committee:**
- Ministries: Home, Agriculture, Atomic Energy, Defence, Finance, Health, Power
- Coordinates implementation of disaster management policy
- Develops action plans for different disasters

#### **Pandemics as Notified Disasters:**
- **Declaration Process:** Epidemic Act 1897 followed by Disaster Management Act
- **Finance Commission Funds:** Additional resource allocation
- **State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF):** 10% can be utilized
- **National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF):** For catastrophic events

### **Appendix D: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Protocols**

#### **WHO Recommended PPE Matrix**

| **Risk Level** | **Clinical Scenario** | **Recommended PPE** | **Alternative** |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Low Risk** | General practice, no contact | Standard precautions | Basic hand hygiene |
| **Intermediate Risk** | Close contact, no aerosol | Surgical mask, eye protection | Standard medical mask |
| **High Risk** | High aerosol exposure | N95/FFP2/FFP3 respirator, goggles/face shield | Powered air-purifying respirator |
| **Very High Risk** | AGP with COVID patient | PAPR, double gloves, impervious gown | Full body suit (hazardous materials) |

#### **Donning and Doffing Procedure Steps**

**Donning Sequence:**
1. Perform hand hygiene
2. Put on fluid-resistant gown
3. Put on N95 respirator and perform seal check
4. Put on goggles or face shield
5. Put on gloves
6. Perform hand hygiene

**Doffing Sequence:**
1. Remove gloves using glove-in-glove technique
2. Remove gown by untying and rolling inside out
3. Perform hand hygiene
4. Remove goggles/face shield from behind
5. Remove N95 respirator from behind
6. Perform hand hygiene

### **Appendix E: Infectious Disease Control Priorities**

#### **Standard Precautions (Applicable to All Patients)**

| **Component** | **Implementation** | **Indications** | **Evidence Level** |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Hand Hygiene** | Alcohol-based rub or soap/water | Before/after patient contact | IA (High) |
| **Gloves** | Single-use, change between patients | Contact with body fluids | IA (High) |
| **Mask** | Medical/surgical mask | Contact with respiratory secretions | IA (High) |
| **Gown** | Fluid-resistant or impermeable | Risk of body fluid exposure | IA (High) |
| **Eye Protection** | Goggles/face shield | Risk of splash/sprays | IA (High) |
| **Safe Injection** | Single-use sterile equipment | All injections | IA (High) |

#### **Transmission-Based Precautions**

| **Type** | **Definition** | **Examples** | **PPE Requirements** |
|----------|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Contact** | Direct/indirect contact transmission | MRSA, VRE, C. difficile | Gloves, gown |
| **Droplet** | Large respiratory droplets | Influenza, COVID-19 | Mask, eye protection |
| **Airborne** | Small particle aerosol | TB, measles, varicella | N95 respirator, negative pressure |
| **Protective** | To protect immunocompromised patient | Neutropenic patients | Clean environment |

### **Appendix F: Pandemic Response Frameworks and Guidelines**

#### **GOARN (Global Outbreak Alert and Response Network)**

**Activation Criteria:**
- Unusual health event with potential international impact
- Need for international assistance
- Insufficient local capacity for response
- Risk of international spread

**Response Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Support to national response (technical advice)
- **Level 2:** Multi-disciplinary field support
- **Level 3:** International coordinating response

### **Appendix H: Mental Health Support in Pandemics**

#### **Psychological First Aid (PFA) Principles**

| **Principle** | **Actions** | **Goals** | **Evidence** |
|---------------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Contact** | Approach calmly, introduce yourself | Build rapport | High-quality interaction |
| **Comfort** | Listen actively, acknowledge feelings | Emotional support | Empathic communication |
| **Calm** | Reassure safety, provide information | Reduce anxiety | Accurate information |
| **Connect** | Link to social support, services | Prevent isolation | Social connection |
| **Competence** | Encourage self-efficacy, coping strategies | Build resilience | Empowerment |

#### **Mental Health Risk Factors in Pandemics**

| **Population Group** | **Specific Risk Factors** | **Recommended Interventions** |
|---------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Healthcare Workers** | Moral injury, burnout, PTSD | Critical incident debriefing, peer support |
| **COVID-19 Patients** | Delirium, anxiety, depression | Integrated mental health screening |
| **General Population** | Isolation, fear, uncertainty | Community mental health services |
| **High-Risk Groups** | Elderly, immunocompromised | Targeted psychological support |
| **Children/Students** | School closure, learning loss | Educational mental health programs |
