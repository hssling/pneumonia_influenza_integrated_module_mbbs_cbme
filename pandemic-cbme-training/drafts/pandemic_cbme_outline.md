# Comprehensive Outline: Pandemic Module for MBBS & PG Students (CBME Compliant)

## Module Information
**Framework:** Competency-Based Medical Education (CBME)  
**Target Audience:** Undergraduate MBBS students, Postgraduate residents, Public health professionals  
**Total Chapters:** 38  
**Estimated Total Pages:** 800-1000 pages  
**CBME Competencies:** PA1-PA2, PB1-PB2, PC1-PC2, Plus advanced units  

---

## **UNIT 1: FOUNDATIONS OF PANDEMICS (Chapters 1-6)**
### **Competency PA1: Understand pandemic concepts and epidemiology**
### **Competency PA2: Apply infectious disease principles**

#### **Chapter 1: Introduction to Pandemics: Concepts and Historical Overview**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Define pandemics and differentiate from epidemics/endemics
- Understand historical pandemics and their impact
- Appreciate lessons from past pandemics

**Key Topics:**
- Pandemic definitions and classifications
- Historical pandemics: Plague, Spanish Flu, HIV/AIDS, SARS, Ebola, COVID-19
- Social, economic, and health consequences
- Global health security agenda

**CBME Competencies:** PA1.1, PA1.2
**Assessment:** MCQs, Case discussion
**Clinical Correlation:** Current COVID-19 context

#### **Chapter 2: Epidemiological Principles in Pandemic Situations**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply epidemiological methods to pandemic scenarios
- Interpret epidemic curves and transmission patterns
- Calculate and interpret key epidemiological measures

**Key Topics:**
- Epidemic curves: Common source vs. propagated outbreaks
- Reproductive number (R0) and effective reproduction number (Rt)
- Attack rates, case fatality rates, and incidence/prevalence
- Surveillance systems and reporting

**CBME Competencies:** PA1.3, PA2.1
**Assessment:** Data interpretation exercises, Calculations
**Clinical Correlation:** COVID-19 epidemiological patterns in India

#### **Chapter 3: Pathogenesis and Host-Pathogen Interactions in Pandemics**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand viral, bacterial, and zoonotic disease pathogenesis
- Explain host immune responses in pandemic scenarios
- Identify factors influencing disease severity

**Key Topics:**
- Viral pathogenesis: Entry, replication, spread
- Host-pathogen interactions and immune evasion
- Zoonotic spillover events and One Health approach
- Factors affecting disease severity (age, comorbidity, genetics)

**CBME Competencies:** PA2.2, PA2.3
**Assessment:** Pathophysiology diagrams, Case analysis
**Clinical Correlation:** SARS-CoV-2 pathogenesis

#### **Chapter 4: Disease Transmission Dynamics and Modeling**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand modes of disease transmission in pandemics
- Apply mathematical modeling to predict disease spread
- Interpret modeling results for policy decisions

**Key Topics:**
- Transmission modes: Respiratory, fecal-oral, vector-borne, zoonotic
- SIR and SEIR models: Basic concepts and applications
- Factors influencing transmission: Population density, mobility, behavior
- Modeling limitations and uncertainties

**CBME Competencies:** PA2.4, PA2.5
**Assessment:** Model interpretation exercises, Policy recommendations
**Clinical Correlation:** COVID-19 transmission modeling in India

#### **Chapter 5: Pandemic Classification and Risk Assessment**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Classify pandemics according to WHO frameworks
- Conduct pandemic risk assessments
- Develop risk mitigation strategies

**Key Topics:**
- WHO pandemic phases for influenza and COVID-19
- Risk assessment frameworks: Likelihood vs. impact
- National and international risk assessment processes
- Risk communication and preparedness planning

**CBME Competencies:** PA1.4, PA2.6
**Assessment:** Risk assessment scenarios, Mitigation planning
**Clinical Correlation:** India's pandemic risk assessment

#### **Chapter 6: Basic Principles of Infection Prevention and Control**
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply standard precautions in clinical settings
- Understand transmission-based precautions
- Implement infection control in resource-limited settings

**Key Topics:**
- Hand hygiene and personal protective equipment

## **Summary and Cross-Cutting Themes**

### **Cross-Cutting Competencies:**
1. **Communication Skills:** Crisis communication, risk communication, stakeholder engagement
2. **Leadership and Teamwork:** Incident command systems, multidisciplinary coordination
3. **Ethical Practice:** Resource allocation, individual rights vs. public health
4. **Evidence-Based Practice:** Rapid research utilization, treatment guideline adaptation
5. **Cultural Competence:** Diverse population needs, health equity considerations
6. **Professionalism:** Duty to care, self-care, continued learning

### **Assessment Blueprint:**
- **Knowledge Assessment:** 40% (MCQs, SAQs, Essay questions)
- **Skills Assessment:** 30% (OSCEs, Simulations, Procedure checklists)
- **Attitude Assessment:** 20% (Portfolio, Reflective writing, Case discussions)
- **Integrated Assessment:** 10% (Multi-station OSCE, Team-based scenarios)

### **Teaching-Learning Methods:**
- **Didactic:** Lectures, online modules, flipped classroom
- **Interactive:** Case discussions, role-playing, debate sessions
- **Experiential:** Simulations, field visits, community engagement
- **Reflective:** Portfolio development, debriefing sessions
- **Technology-Enhanced:** Virtual reality, serious gaming, e-learning platforms
