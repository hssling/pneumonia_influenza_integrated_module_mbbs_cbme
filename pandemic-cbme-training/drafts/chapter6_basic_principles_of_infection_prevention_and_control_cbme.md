## Introduction

Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) forms the cornerstone of safe healthcare delivery during pandemics. The COVID-19 pandemic vividly demonstrated how healthcare facilities can become transmission hotspots without robust IPC measures. Understanding and implementing IPC principles is essential for healthcare workers to protect themselves, patients, and the community.

This chapter covers the fundamental principles of IPC, including standard and transmission-based precautions, with special emphasis on pandemic-specific considerations and adaptation to resource-limited settings typical in many parts of India.

## Section 1: Core Concepts of Infection Prevention

### 1.1 Chain of Infection

#### **Traditional Chain of Infection Model**
**Six interconnected links:**

1. **Pathogen/Infectious Agent**: SARS-CoV-2, bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites
2. **Reservoir**: Human body, animals, environment, medical devices
3. **Exit Route**: Respiratory droplets, blood, body fluids, contaminated surfaces
4. **Transmission Route**: Contact, droplet, airborne, vector-borne, vehicle-borne
5. **Entry Route**: Mucous membranes, broken skin, invasive devices
6. **Susceptible Host**: Immunocompromised patients, elderly, malnourished individuals

#### **Pandemic-Specific Modifications**
**COVID-19 Chain:**
- **Multiple reservoirs**: Humans (primary), animals (secondary)
- **Complex transmission routes**: Respiratory + surface + aerosol transmission
- **High risk entry points**: Respiratory tract, conjunctiva, gastrointestinal tract

### 1.2 Healthcare-Associated Infections (HAIs)

#### **HCAI Classifications in Pandemics**
**Device-associated infections:**
- **Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP)**: Higher risk during respiratory pandemics
- **Central line-associated bloodstream infections (CLABSI)**: Compromised immunity
- **Catheter-associated urinary tract infections (CAUTI)**: Prolonged catheterization

#### **COVID-19 Impact on HCAI Rates**
**Early 2020 findings:**
- **Increased HCAI burden**: Reduced infection control rigor due to system overload
- **Nosocomial transmission clusters**: Healthcare workers as secondary transmitters
- **Multidrug-resistant organism emergence**: Antibiotic overuse and increased transmission
- **Mental health impact**: Increased burnout and reduced compliance
