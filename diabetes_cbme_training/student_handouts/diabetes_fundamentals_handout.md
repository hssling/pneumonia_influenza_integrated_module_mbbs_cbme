## **Diabetes Mellitus: Essential Knowledge**

### **Definition**
Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by hyperglycemia resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both.

### **Prevalence (2023 Data)**
- **Global:** 537 million adults affected (IDF Atlas 10th Edition)
- **India:** 77 million people affected (15.3% of adults 20-79 years)
- **Rural India:** 57% of diabetics remain undiagnosed
- **Projection 2030:** India's diabetes burden to reach 93 million

### **Classification**
1. **Type 1 DM (5-10%)**
   - Autoimmune destruction of β-cells
   - Absolute insulin deficiency
   - Presentation: Young age, ketoacidosis
   - Management: Insulin replacement

2. **Type 2 DM (90-95%)**
   - Insulin resistance + progressive β-cell failure
   - Strong genetic component
   - Presentation: Adult onset, obesity
   - Management: Lifestyle + oral agents + insulin

3. **Other Types (1-2%)**
   - Gestational diabetes
   - Monogenic diabetes (MODY)
   - Secondary diabetes (pancreatitis, drugs)

## **Diabetes Pathophysiology**

### **Normal Glucose Regulation**
- **Insulin:** Promotes glucose uptake and glycogen synthesis
- **Glucagon:** Increases blood glucose by promoting glycogenolysis
- **Counter-regulatory hormones:** Epinephrine, cortisol, growth hormone

### **Pathogenesis of Type 2 DM**
1. **Genetic Predisposition:** Multiple gene defects
2. **Insulin Resistance:** Peripheral tissues fail to respond normally to insulin
3. **β-Cell Dysfunction:** Progressive loss of insulin secretory capacity
4. **Compensatory Hyperinsulinemia:** Initial response, then failure

### **Hyperglycemia Effects**
- **Acute:** Polyuria, polydipsia, fatigue, blurred vision
- **Chronic:** Microvascular/macrovascular complications

---

## **Clinical Features**

### **Classic Triad**
- **Polyuria:** Frequent urination
- **Polydipsia:** Excessive thirst
- **Polyphagia:** Increased hunger (in type 1)

### **Other Symptoms**
- **Fatigue and weakness**
- **Blurring of vision**
- **Recurrent infections**
- **Delayed wound healing**
- **Weight loss** (type 1)
- **Asymptomatic** in 50% cases (type 2)

### **Complications Screening (Annual)**
- **Retinopathy:** Fundoscopy or dilated eye examination
- **Nephropathy:** Urine albumin/creatinine ratio, eGFR
- **Neuropathy:** Foot examination, vibration sense, reflexes
- **Cardiovascular:** ECG, lipid profile, blood pressure

## **Diagnostic Criteria (ADA 2023)**

### **Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)**
- **Normal:** < 100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L)
- **Pre-diabetes:** 100-125 mg/dL (5.6-6.9 mmol/L)
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) on two occasions

### **2-Hour Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)**
- **Normal:** < 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L)
- **Pre-diabetes:** 140-199 mg/dL (7.8-11.0 mmol/L)
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)

### **HbA1c**
- **Normal:** < 5.7% (39 mmol/mol)
- **Pre-diabetes:** 5.7-6.4% (39-47 mmol/mol)
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 6.5% (48 mmol/mol)

### **Random Blood Glucose**
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) + symptoms

## **Risk Factors**

### **Non-Modifiable**
- **Age ≥ 35 years**
- **Family history** (first-degree relatives)
- **Previous gestational diabetes**
- **Ethnicity** (Asian Indians – 2-4 times higher risk)

### **Modifiable**
- **Obesity/BMI ≥ 25 kg/m²**
- **Abdominal obesity** (waist ≥ 90 cm in men, ≥ 80 cm in women)
- **Physical inactivity** (< 150 minutes moderate activity/week)
- **Unhealthy diet** (high refined carbs, saturated fats)
- **Tobacco use** and excessive alcohol
- **Hypertension** (BP ≥ 140/90 mmHg)
- **Dyslipidemia** (low HDL, high triglycerides)

## **Management Principles**

### **General Approaches**
1. **Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)**
2. **Physical Activity & Exercise**
3. **Pharmacological Therapy**
4. **Self-Monitoring of Blood Glucose (SMBG)**
5. **Education & Psychological Support**
6. **Regular Physician Follow-up**

### **Treatment Goals (ADA 2023)**

| **Parameter** | **Target** |
|---------------|------------|
| **HbA1c** | < 7.0% (< 6.5% if young, no contraindications) |
| **Fasting BG** | 80-130 mg/dL (4.4-7.2 mmol/L) |
| **Postprandial BG** | < 180 mg/dL (< 160 mg/dL after 2 hours) |
| **Blood Pressure** | < 130/80 mmHg |
| **LDL Cholesterol** | < 100 mg/dL (< 70 mg/dL if CVD) |

## **Pharmacological Treatment**

### **Type 2 DM Treatment Algorithm**
1. **Metformin** (first-line): 500-1000 mg twice daily
2. **Add second agent:** DPP-4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, teneligliptin)
3. **Triple therapy:** Add SGLT2 inhibitors or sulfonylureas
4. **Insulin initiation:** When optimal dual/triple therapy fails

### **Common Oral Agents**

| **Class** | **Examples** | **Mechanism** | **Side Effects** |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Biguanides** | Metformin | Decrease hepatic glucose output | GI upset, lactic acidosis |
| **Sulfonylureas** | Glimepiride | Stimulate insulin secretion | Hypoglycemia, weight gain |
| **DPP-4 Inhibitors** | Sitagliptin | Increase incretin levels | Rare - pancreatitis |
| **SGLT2 Inhibitors** | Empagliflozin | Block glucose reabsorption | UTI, genital infections |
| **GLP-1 Agonists** | Dulaglutide | Mimic incretin effects | Nausea, pancreatitis |

### **Insulin Therapy**
- **Basal/Bolus regimen:** Long-acting (e.g., insulin glargine) + short-acting
- **Starting dose:** 0.3-0.5 IU/kg/day
- **Adjustment:** Based on SMBG readings
- **Education essential:** Dosage, administration, storage

## **Diabetes Complications**

### **Acute Complications**
1. **Hypoglycemia:** Common with insulin/sulfonylureas
   - Symptoms: Sweating, tremor, confusion, coma
   - Treatment: 15g carbs, glucagon, hospitalization if severe

2. **Hyperglycemic emergencies:**
   - **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA):** Type 1, infection, miss insulin
   - **Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS):** Type 2, severe dehydration
   - Treatment: Fluid resuscitation, insulin, electrolyte correction

### **Chronic Complications**

#### **Macrovascular (Cardiovascular)**
- Coronary artery disease (2-4x risk)
- Cerebral vascular disease (stroke 2-4x risk)
- Peripheral vascular disease (leading cause of amputation)

#### **Microvascular (Classic Triad)**
1. **Diabetic Retinopathy:** Leading cause of blindness
   - Non-proliferative → proliferative → maculopathy
   - Screening: Annual dilated fundoscopy from diagnosis

2. **Diabetic Nephropathy:** Leading cause of ESRD
   - Microalbuminuria → macroalbuminuria → CKD
   - Screening: Annual UAE and serum creatinine/eGFR

3. **Diabetic Neuropathy:** Most common complication
   - Distal symmetric polyneuropathy
   - Autonomic neuropathy (gastroparesis, orthostatic hypotension)
   - Screening: Foot examination, monofilament testing

## **Community Medicine Perspective**

### **Prevention Strategies**
- **Population Level:** WHO "Best Buys" - sugar taxation, food labeling
- **High-Risk Individuals:** Lifestyle interventions (DPP trials)
- **Screening Programs:** NPCDCS guidelines for community screening
- **Health Promotion:** Community awareness through schools, workplaces

### **Government Programs in India**
- **NPCDCS (2010):** National Programme for Prevention & Control
- **NP-IHP (2017):** Non-Communicable Diseases component
- **Free Medications:** Oral hypoglycemics, insulin through government schemes
- **PP-Hypertension & Diabetes:** Prevention & control program

- Community screening and awareness
- Follow-up of registered diabetics
- Referral coordination
- Community mobilization for prevention
