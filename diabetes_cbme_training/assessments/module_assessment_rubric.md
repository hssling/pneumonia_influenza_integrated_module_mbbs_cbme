# CBME Diabetes Training Module Assessment Framework

## Competency-Based Assessment Rubric

---

## **Assessment Philosophy**

This module follows CBME principles with **progressive entrustment**. Students progress from "shows how" to "does autonomously" based on demonstrated competence rather than time-based progression.

**Entrustment Scale:**
1. **I have no concern** - Student can perform independently
2. **I have minor concern** - Student needs occasional supervision
3. **I need to discuss with student** - Significant supervision required
4. **I need to do it** - Direct faculty intervention needed

---

## **Assessment Timeline & Structure**

### **Weekly Formative Assessment**
- **Monday:** Self-reflection logs (competency self-rating)
- **Wednesday:** Peer assessment of group activities
- **Friday:** Faculty mini-CEX (Clinical Evaluation Exercise)

### **Mid-Module Assessment (Day 5)**
- **Written:** Diabetes knowledge quiz
- **OSCE:** 3 stations (screening, education, referral)
- **Portfolio:** Case logs and reflection journal

### **End-of-Module Assessment (Day 10)**
- **Community Project:** Group presentation and evaluation
- **Integrated OSCE:** 5 stations covering all competencies
- **Portfolio Defense:** Individual competency demonstration

## **Detailed Assessment Rubric**

### **DOMAIN 1: PATIENT CARE (PC) - 30% Weight**

#### **PC1: Community Screening & Diagnosis**
**Behaviors Assessed:**
- Consent obtaining for screening procedures
- Correct glucometer technique and interpretation
- Appropriate counseling based on results
- Identification and management of complications

**Entrustment Scale:**
- **Level 4:** Requires direct supervision for all steps
- **Level 3:** Shows correct technique with supervision
- **Level 2:** Does independently with occasional prompting
- **Level 1:** Performs autonomously in community setting

**Assessment Methods:** Direct observation during screening camps

#### **PC2: Patient Education & Counseling**
**Behaviors Assessed:**
- Use of teach-back method for education verification
- Cultural adaptation of educational materials
- Family involvement in counseling sessions
- Adjustment of communication based on patient feedback

**Criteria:**
- **Communication:** Clear, empathetic, jargon-free
- **Content Accuracy:** Evidence-based information provided
- **Patient Response:** Adapts to patient's emotional state
- **Family Integration:** Appropriate inclusion of support systems

#### **PC4: Complication Assessment & Prevention**
**Behaviors Assessed:**
- Systematic foot examination technique
- Recognition of diabetes-related emergencies
- Preventive care recommendations
- Quality assurance of screening services

---

### **DOMAIN 2: MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE (MK) - 15% Weight**

#### **MK1-MK4: Diabetes Pathophysiology & Management**

**Assessment Format:** Multiple-choice questions + short answer cases

**Content Areas:**
- Pathophysiology of type 1/type 2/gestational diabetes
- Diagnostic criteria and screening recommendations
- Management guidelines (ADA/IDF/Indian protocols)
- Complications spectrum and prevention strategies

**Performance Scales:**
- **80-100%:** Comprehensive understanding with clinical application
- **70-79%:** Good grasp with minor gaps in application
- **60-69%:** Basic knowledge sufficient for daily practice
- **<60%:** Significant knowledge gaps requiring remediation

---

### **DOMAIN 3: PRACTICE-BASED LEARNING (PBLI) - 10% Weight**

#### **PBLI1-PBLI2: Evidence-Based Practice & Self-Learning**

**Assessment Methods:**
- **Literature Search:** "Find evidence for metformin in pre-diabetes"
- **Critical Appraisal:** Case-based evidence evaluation
- **Self-Directed Learning Portfolio:** Learning objectives, progress tracking
- **Reflection Journals:** Daily reflections on patient encounters

**Competency Indicators:**
- Identifies knowledge gaps during patient care
- Uses appropriate resources for information gathering
- Applies evidence to clinical decision-making
- Maintains learning portfolio with reflections

---

### **DOMAIN 4: COMMUNICATION SKILLS (CS) - 15% Weight**

#### **CS1-CS3: Patient-Doctor-Community Communication**

**Assessment in Multiple Settings:**
- **Role-Play Scenarios:** SPIKES protocol for breaking bad news
- **Patient Encounters:** Real-time observation in screening camps
- **Group Presentations:** Community project presentations
- **Family Counseling:** Multi-person communication dynamics

**SMART Communication Criteria:**
- **S** etting: Appropriate private environment
- **M** essage: Clear, concise communication
- **A** ctive listening: Non-verbal cues, paraphrasing
- **R** esponse: Addresses patient concerns
- **T** iming: Appropriate pacing, time management

---

### **DOMAIN 5: PROFESSIONALISM (PRO) - 10% Weight**

#### **PRO1-PRO2: Ethical Practice & Cultural Competence**

**Assessment Areas:**
- **Confidentiality:** Patient data protection during community activities
- **Informed Consent:** Clear explanation and voluntary participation
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Language adaptation, belief system respect
- **Professional Demeanor:** Appropriate attire, punctuality, respect

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Maintains patient privacy in rural settings
- Adapts communication to local dialects and literacy levels
- Shows respect for traditional healing practices
- Demonstrates empathy across socioeconomic divides

---

### **DOMAIN 6: SYSTEMS-BASED PRACTICE (SBP) - 15% Weight**

#### **SBP1-SBP3: Healthcare System Navigation**

**Assessment Methods:**
- **PHC Visit Simulation:** Demonstration of referral process
- **Resource Mapping:** Identification of community support systems
- **Quality Improvement:** Data analysis from screening camps
- **Advocacy Skills:** Presentation of community health needs

**Competency Framework:**
- Understands village health system structure
- Identifies appropriate referral pathways
- Advocates for patient's social determinants of health
- Participates in community health improvement initiatives

## **OSCE Station Blueprint**

### **Station 1: Community Screening (PC1)**
**Task:** Perform screening on standardized patient
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Marks:** 20 points
**Critical Actions:**
1. Obtain informed consent ✓
2. Perform glucometer correctly ✓
3. Interpret results accurately ✓
4. Provide appropriate counseling ✓

### **Station 2: Patient Education (PC2)**
**Task:** Educate patient about lifestyle modification
**Time:** 12 minutes
**Marks:** 25 points
**Competencies:**
- Communication skills ✓
- Content accuracy ✓
- Cultural adaptation ✓
- Family involvement ✓

### **Station 3: Referral Management (SBP1)**
**Task:** Manage patient requiring specialist care
**Time:** 8 minutes
**Marks:** 15 points
**Checklist:**
- Identify appropriate level of care ✓
- Complete referral documentation ✓
- Explain process to patient ✓
- Provide immediate management advice ✓

### **Station 4: Emergency Recognition (PC4)**
**Task:** Assess patient with possible DKA
**Time:** 10 minutes
**Marks:** 20 points
**Critical Skills:**
- History taking for emergencies ✓
- Physical examination for complications ✓
- Emergency management initiation ✓
- Referral coordination ✓

### **Station 5: Community Project (SBP3)**
**Task:** Present community intervention plan
**Time:** 15 minutes
**Marks:** 25 points
**Assessment Areas:**
- Project planning and feasibility ✓
- Cultural appropriateness ✓
- Evaluation methodology ✓
- Resource sustainability ✓

## **Grading and Progression**

### **Competency-Based Grading:**

| **Overall Performance** | **Entrustment Level** | **Grade** | **Progression** |
|-------------------------|---------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| ≥80% in all domains | Level 1-2 | Pass with Honors | Entrustable for independent practice |
| ≥70% in all domains | Level 2-3 | Pass | Entrustable with supervision |
| 60-69% in ≥4 domains | Level 3-4 | Conditional Pass | Additional training required |
| <60% in any domain | Level 4 | Fail | Repeat module required |

### **Remediation Strategy:**
- **Focused Training:** Additional sessions for weak competencies
- **Mentor Assignment:** Individual faculty supervision
- **Portfolio Enhancement:** Additional documentation requirements
- **Reassessment:** Target competency evaluation within 2 weeks
