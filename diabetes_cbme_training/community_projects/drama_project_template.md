# Diabetes Awareness Community Drama Project

## CBME Competency-Based Learning Activity

---

## **Project Overview**

**Title:** "Sugar Shadows" - Diabetes Awareness Street Drama
**Duration:** 3-day community intervention project
**Student Team:** 6 MBBS students (3 groups of 2)
**Target Audience:** Rural community (300-500 people)
**CBME Focus:** Community engagement, health education, advocacy skills

---

## **Learning Objectives (CBME Aligned)**

### **Patient Care (PC)**
- PC2: Educate communities about diabetes prevention
- PC5: Partner with communities to address health needs

### **Communication Skills (CS)**
- CS2: Family and community discussions
- CS3: Interprofessional teamwork for health promotion

### **Systems-Based Practice (SBP)**
- SBP3: Quality improvement through community interventions

### **Professionalism (PRO)**
- PRO2: Cultural competence in health education

---

## **Project Structure (3 Days)**

### **Day 1: Planning & Preparation**
**Time:** 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
**Location:** College campus/community center

**Activities:**
1. **Team Formation:** 6 students divided into 3 teams
   - Writing team (2 students)
   - Acting team (2 students)
   - Production team (2 students)

2. **Community Assessment (2 hours):**
   - Local diabetes prevalence data
   - Cultural beliefs about diabetes
   - Preferred communication styles
   - Existing health programs

3. **Script Development (3 hours):**
   - Identify key messages
   - Create relatable characters
   - Include local cultural elements
   - Balance education with entertainment

4. **Rehearsal Preparation (2 hours):**
   - Basic acting techniques
   - Voice projection training
   - Props and costume discussion

---

### **Day 2: Rehearsal & Refinement**
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
**Location:** Community open space

**Activities:**
1. **Full Script Read-through (2 hours)**
2. **Blocking and Movement (2 hours)**
3. **Props and Costumes Creation (2 hours)**
4. **Cultural Adaptation Session (2 hours)**
   - Local language consultant invited
   - Community elder feedback session

---

### **Day 3: Performance & Evaluation**
**Time:** 6:00 PM - 10:00 PM
**Location:** Village community center

**Activities:**
1. **Evening Performance (1 hour)**
2. **Interactive Q&A Session (1 hour)**
3. **Post-performance Survey (1 hour)**
4. **Community Feedback Collection (1 hour)**

---

## **Drama Script Template**

### **Act 1: The Hidden Danger (10 minutes)**

**Scene:** Rural village courtyard

**Characters:**
- Ramu: 45-year-old farmer (diabetic, unaware)
- Lakshmi: His wife (concerned but traditional)
- Dr. Sharma: Local health worker
- Village elders

**Key Messages:**
- Early symptoms of diabetes often ignored
- Family history increases risk
- Importance of regular health check-ups

**Plot Points:**
- Ramu complains of fatigue, frequent urination
- Wife notices changes in behavior
- Village elder shares family history
- Health worker explains need for testing

---

### **Act 2: The Diagnosis Journey (12 minutes)**

**Characters:** (Previous + doctor and family)

**Key Messages:**
- Different types of diabetes tests
- Emotional aspects of diagnosis
- Importance of family support
- Need for lifestyle changes

**Plot Points:**
- Visit to health camp
- Anxiety during testing
- Breaking the diagnosis news
- Family reactions and support

---

### **Act 3: Living with Diabetes (13 minutes)**

**Characters:** (All above + pharmacist, neighbor)

**Key Messages:**
- Medication adherence
- Dietary modifications
- Regular exercise
- Foot care and complication prevention
- Regular follow-up importance

**Plot Points:**
- Learning about medications
- Dietary changes with family meals
- Exercise routine development
- Complication warning signs
- Hopeful outlook on management

---

### **Act 4: Community Action (5 minutes)**

**Finale with community involvement**

**Key Messages:**
- Prevention through healthy lifestyles
- Community screening importance
- Support systems for diabetics
- Breaking stigma around diabetes

## **CBME Assessment Rubric**

### **Criteria 1: Community Relevance (20%)**
- **Excellent:** Script reflects local culture, language, beliefs
- **Good:** Most elements culturally appropriate
- **Developing:** Some cultural elements included
- **Poor:** No cultural adaptation

### **Criteria 2: Educational Accuracy (25%)**
- **Excellent:** All medical information scientifically accurate
- **Good:** Minor inaccuracies, major points correct
- **Developing:** Some misinformation present
- **Poor:** Significant medical errors

### **Criteria 3: Performance Quality (20%)**
- **Excellent:** Confident, engaging, clear delivery
- **Good:** Adequate performance, good voice projection
- **Developing:** Hesitant delivery, poor enunciation
- **Poor:** Unable to complete performance

### **Criteria 4: Interactive Engagement (15%)**
- **Excellent:** Actively involved audience, answered questions
- **Good:** Good Q&A session post-performance
- **Developing:** Minimal audience interaction
- **Poor:** No community engagement

### **Criteria 5: Project Management (20%)**
- **Excellent:** Well-organized, met all deadlines, effective teamwork
- **Good:** Adequate organization, good team coordination
- **Developing:** Poor time management, team conflicts
- **Poor:** Complete lack of organization
