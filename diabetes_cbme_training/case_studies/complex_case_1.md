# Complex Diabetes Case Study 1: Rural Farmer with Multiple Complications

## **Case Overview**
**Patient Profile**: 58-year-old male farmer, Kumar (pseudonym), living in a rural village near Tumkur.

**Presenting Complaint**: Worsening fatigue, frequent urination, and non-healing foot ulcer for 3 months.

---

## **History of Present Illness**

**Presenting Symptoms:**
- Polyuria (frequent urination) for past 6 months
- Polydipsia (excessive thirst)
- Fatigue and weakness interfering with farming work
- Blurred vision for 2 months
- Weight loss of 8 kg in 4 months despite good appetite
- Non-healing ulcer on right big toe for 3 months

**Past Medical History:**
- Diagnosed with diabetes 12 years ago during routine health camp
- Irregular treatment - takes metformin when "feels sick"
- No formal diabetes education received
- No regular blood glucose monitoring
- Family history: Mother died of "sugar disease" complications

**Social History:**
- 45-year-old farmer, owns 2 acres of land
- Daily physical labor: 8-10 hours farming work
- Family: Wife (housewife), 2 children (15 and 12 years old)
- Tobacco: Chew tobacco 5-6 packets/day for past 20 years
- Alcohol: Occasional (festive drinking)
- Education: Primary school education only
- Monthly income: ₹8,000-10,000

**Current Medications:**
- Metformin 500mg - takes irregularly (2-3 times/week)
- No other medications
- Uses traditional remedies occasionally

---

## **Physical Examination**

**Vital Signs:**
- BP: 160/95 mmHg (multiple readings)
- Pulse: 84/min regular
- Temperature: 98.4°F
- BMI: 22.1 kg/m² (underweight)
- Waist circumference: 88 cm

**General:**
- Pale conjunctiva, decreased skin turgor
- Evidence of nutritional deficiency
- Mild dehydration

**Systemic Examination:**

**Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds, no murmurs
**Respiratory:** Normal vesicular breath sounds
**Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender, no organomegaly

**Neurological:**
- Peripheral neuropathy: Vibration sense reduced in both feet
- Loss of ankle reflexes bilaterally
- Pain sensation present

**Foot Examination:**
- Right big toe: 2x2 cm ulcer with necrotic base
- Surrounding cellulitis with erythema
- No odor or discharge
- Pedal pulses palpable
- Monofilament testing: Reduced sensation

**Vision:**
- Best corrected visual acuity: 6/24 right eye, 6/18 left eye
- Cataract changes evident

---

## **Laboratory Investigations**

**Blood Glucose:**
- Random blood glucose: 280 mg/dL
- HbA1c: 11.5% (very poor control)
- Fructosamine: 385 µmol/L (elevated)

**Lipid Profile:**
- Total cholesterol: 220 mg/dL
- LDL: 140 mg/dL
- HDL: 32 mg/dL (low)
- Triglycerides: 220 mg/dL

**Kidney Function:**
- Serum creatinine: 1.4 mg/dL
- eGFR: 52 mL/min/1.73m² (Stage 3 CKD)
- Urine albumin: 150 mg/g creatinine (macroalbuminuria)
- Urine ketones: Negative

**Other Tests:**
- ECG: Normal sinus rhythm, no ischemic changes
- Hb: 10.8 g/dL (anemia of chronic disease)
- Vitamin B12: 180 pg/mL (borderline low)

---

## **Diagnosis**

**Primary Diagnosis:**
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with poor glycemic control (HbA1c 11.5%)
- Multiple chronic complications:
  - Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
  - Diabetic nephropathy (Stage 3 CKD)
  - Diabetic foot ulcer with cellulitis
  - Suspected diabetic retinopathy

**Associated Conditions:**
- Hypertensive cardiovascular disease
- Dyslipidemia
- Tobacco-related oral health issues
- Mixed nutritional deficiencies
- Depressive symptoms (undiagnosed)

---

## **Management Plan**

### **Immediate Interventions (Community Level):**
1. **Wound Care & Infection Control:**
   - Daily dressing changes with saline and povidone-iodine
   - Oral antibiotics (amoxicillin-clavulanate for 10 days)
   - Elevation and rest instructions
   - Daily wound monitoring

2. **Glycemic Control:**
   - Regular insulin initiation (basal-bolus regimen education)
   - SMBG teaching (3 times daily)
   - Diet counseling for nutritional rehabilitation
   - Family involvement in diabetes management

3. **Blood Pressure Management:**
   - Antihypertensive addition (ACE inhibitor preferred)
   - Lifestyle modifications emphasis
   - Regular monitoring protocol

### **Referral Strategy:**
1. **Primary Care Level:** Initial management and stabilization
2. **Secondary Care Level:** Diabetes specialist consultation within 1 week
3. **Tertiary Care Level:** Nephrology referral for CKD management
4. **Specialist Referrals:**
   - Ophthalmologist for retinopathy screening
   - Vascular surgeon if wound deteriorates
   - Nutritionist for comprehensive dietary planning

- Apply complication screening protocols

### **Communication Skills:**
- Explain complex medical conditions to low-literacy patients
- Coordinate multidisciplinary care team
- Educate family members effectively

### **Systems-Based Practice:**
- Navigate limited resources for maximum patient benefit
- Coordinate across primary/secondary/tertiary care levels
- Access available government diabetes schemes
