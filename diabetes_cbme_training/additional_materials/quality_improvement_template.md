## **QI METHODOLOGY - PDSA CYCLE**

### **PDSA Cycle Stages**
```
P (Plan): Design intervention based on problem analysis
D (Do): Implement intervention on small scale
S (Study): Analyze results and feedback
A (Act): Refine and implement broadly or discontinue if ineffective
```

### **Multiple PDSA Cycles**
```
Plan Implementation → Study Results → Act on Findings
                     ↓
            Plan-Do-Study-Act (Repeat with Refinements)
```

## **STEP-BY-STEP QI PROJECT DEVELOPMENT**

### **Step 1: Problem Identification & Definition**

**AIMS Template Structure**:
```
What are we trying to accomplish?
─────────────────────────────
- Increase screening completion rate for diabetes in rural communities

What will it look like when it is accomplished?
────────────────────────────────────────────
- 95% eligible adults screened annually
- Positive feedback from community members
- Integration into routine PHC services

What changes can we make that will result in an improvement?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Mobile screening units with facilitated access
- Community health education campaigns
- ASHAs coordinating appointment scheduling

What are we measuring to know that a change is an improvement?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Screening completion percentage
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Cost per screening completed
```

### **Step 2: Analysis & Cause-Out Root**

**5-Whys Technique**:
```
Problem: Only 40% of eligible adults get screened for diabetes annually
         ↓
Why? Screening facility is 5 km away with poor road access
         ↓
Why? No mobile screening services available
         ↓
Why? Limited transportation budget for health department
         ↓
Why? Priority given to curative services over preventive care
         ↓
Solution: Advocate for preventive care budget allocation + mobile clinics
```

**Fishbone Diagram Categories**:
- **People**: Provider knowledge, patient awareness, staff training
- **Processes**: Appointment systems, clinic workflow, referral mechanisms
- **Equipment**: Availability of glucometers, maintenance schedules
- **Environment**: Distance to clinic, social stigma, economic barriers
- **Materials**: Informed consent forms, educational materials
- **Policies**: Government guidelines implementation, insurance coverage

### **Step 3: Intervention Design**

**Change Package Development**:
```
Intervention Elements:
1. Mobile Screening Camp Schedule (Weekly rotation)
2. Community Awareness Campaign (Local radio + posters)
3. ASHA Coordination System (Door-to-door mobilization)
4. Appointment Recall System (SMS reminders)
5. Patient Feedback Mechanism (Suggestion boxes)

Implementation Checklist:
□ Trained staff for mobile unit operations
□ Supplies stocked and validated
□ Community leaders informed and supportive
□ Emergency response protocol established
□ Data collection forms prepared
□ Quality control measures in place
```

### **Step 4: Measurement & Data Collection**

**Outcome Measures** (Results Focus):
- **Process**: % eligible adults screened
- **Impact**: HbA1c reduction in diagnosed patients
- **Outcome**: Hospitalization rates for diabetic complications

**Balancing Measures** (Unintended Consequences):
- Patient satisfaction with screening process
- Staff workload and burnout levels
- Cost per patient screened
- No-show rates and appointment wastage

**Data Collection Methods**:
```
Quantitative Methods:
- Screening rates (daily/monthly tracking)
- Blood glucose distribution curves
- Referral completion percentages
- Cost analysis per patient screened

Qualitative Methods:
- Patient feedback interviews
- Healthcare provider experience surveys
- Community leader consultations
- Process observation notes
```

### **Step 5: PDSA Cycle Implementation**

#### **Plan Phase**:
1. **Objective Statement**: Develop 3-month plan to increase diabetes screening by 50%
2. **Intervention Details**: Weekly mobile camps + community education
3. **Success Criteria**: >60% screening completion in pilot areas
4. **Data Collection Plan**: Daily screening logs + monthly patient surveys

#### **Do Phase**:
1. **Pilot Implementation**: Start with one village, 4-week intervention
2. **Team Responsibilities**: Each student takes specific roles (screening, education, data)
3. **Quality Control**: Daily review of glucometer accuracy, consent procedures
4. **Contingency Plans**: Backup power, weather delays, equipment failure protocols

#### **Study Phase**:
1. **Data Analysis**: Daily screening numbers, quality of service metrics
2. **Process Review**: Team debriefs to identify successes and challenges
3. **Patient Feedback**: Experience surveys and suggestion collection
4. **Comparative Analysis**: Compare pilot with baseline performance

#### **Act Phase**:
1. **Scale-Up Decisions**: Successful interventions expanded to more villages
2. **Refinement**: Based on lessons learned from pilot implementation
3. **Sustainability Planning**: Training of local health workers for continuation
4. **Documentation**: Final project report with recommendations for health system

## **SAMPLE QI PROJECT TOPIC: DIABETES SCREENING IMPROVEMENT**

### **Project Title**
"Improving Diabetes Screening Coverage in Rural Karnataka Through Mobile Health Camps"

### **Problem Statement**
In our assigned rural community, only 35% of adults aged 30-60 years complete annual diabetes screening, below the national target of 60%.
This leads to delayed diagnosis and higher complication rates.

### **Root Cause Analysis**
- **Access issues**: 3-5 km distance to PHC, poor roads during monsoons
- **Awareness gaps**: Limited understanding of asymptomatic nature of diabetes
- **Perception barriers**: "Screening only for sick people" stigma
- **Supply constraints**: Limited testing supplies and staff time

### **Intervention Design**
1. **Mobile Diabetes Van**: Equipped with glucometer, educational materials, trained staff
2. **Weekly Schedule**: Rotation through 8 villages in 2-month period
3. **Community Mobilization**: ASHA coordination + local announcements
4. **Screening Package**: FBG + education + follow-up appointment scheduling
5. **Quality Standards**: <15 minutes per patient, >95% consent rates

### **Expected Outcomes**
- **Process**: 70-80% screening completion in target villages
- **Impact**: Early detection of 15-20% new diabetes cases
- **Outcome**: Improved glycemic control through timely management
- **Sustainability**: Model that can be adopted by local PHCs
