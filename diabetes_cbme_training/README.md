# Diabetes Mellitus CBME Training Module

## For MBBS Community Medicine Posting

**Competency-Based Medical Education Materials**

---

## 📋 **Overview**

This module provides comprehensive CBME (Competency-Based Medical Education) training materials specifically designed for MBBS students during their Community Medicine posting. The materials focus on diabetes mellitus management in community settings, emphasizing prevention, screening, and multidisciplinary care.

**Target Audience**: MBBS Students (Community Medicine Posting)
**Duration**: 2-week module with hands-on community activities
**Learning Approach**: Case-based, skill-oriented, community-focused

---

## 🎯 **CBME Competencies Covered**

### **1. Patient Care (PC)**
- PC1: Conduct screening for diabetes and pre-diabetes in community settings
- PC2: Educate patients and families about lifestyle modification
- PC3: Coordinate multidisciplinary care for diabetic patients
- PC4: Perform basic foot examination and wound care assessment

### **2. Medical Knowledge (MK)**
- MK1: Pathophysiology of diabetes mellitus
- MK2: Risk factors and prevention strategies
- MK3: Management guidelines and treatment protocols
- MK4: Complications and referral indications

### **3. Practice-Based Learning (PBLI)**
- PBLI1: Self-directed learning through patient encounters
- PBLI2: Evidence-based approach to diabetes management
- PBLI3: Application of community health principles

### **4. Communication Skills (CS)**
- CS1: Patient education and counseling
- CS2: Family and community discussions
- CS3: Interdisciplinary team communication

### **5. Professionalism (PRO)**
- PRO1: Ethical screening and data handling
- PRO2: Cultural sensitivity in diabetes care
- PRO3: Patient privacy and confidentiality

### **6. Systems-Based Practice (SBP)**
- SBP1: Community health system navigation
- SBP2: Resource allocation and management
- SBP3: Quality improvement initiatives

## 🔄 **Daily Schedule (2 Weeks)**

### **Week 1: Foundations & Assessment**
- **Day 1**: Diabetes epidemiology and risk factors
- **Day 2**: Screening protocols and diagnostic criteria
- **Day 3**: Patient education and lifestyle counseling
- **Day 4**: Community survey and data collection
- **Day 5**: Case presentations and group discussions

### **Week 2: Management & Intervention**

## 🛠️ **Teaching Learning Materials (TLM)**

### **1. Experiential Learning**
- **Community Screening Camps**: Hands-on blood glucose testing
- **Patient Home Visits**: Family assessment and education
- **PHC Visit Simulations**: Government health system navigation

### **2. Problem-Based Learning**
- **Case Studies**: Complex diabetes management scenarios
- **Grand Rounds**: Community case discussions
- **Journal Clubs**: Evidence-based diabetes research

### **3. Collaborative Learning**
- **Group Projects**: Community diabetes intervention programs
- **Role-Playing**: Patient-doctor-family communication
- **Peer Teaching**: Student-led diabetes workshops
