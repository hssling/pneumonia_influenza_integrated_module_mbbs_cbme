## **Answer Key & Learning Points**

### **Section A Answers:** 1-3, 2-2, 3-2, 4-2, 5-3, 6-4, 7-2, 8-2

**Learning Points:**
- **Epidemiology:** India has world's largest diabetes burden
- **Risk Factors:** Family history is non-modifiable; others can be changed
- **Pathophysiology:** Type 2 involves insulin resistance + β-cell failure
- **Diagnosis:** HbA1c ≥6.5% confirms diabetes on two occasions
- **Symptoms:** Hypertension is not classic triad (thirst, urination, hunger)
- **Classification:** Type 2 accounts for 90-95% of cases
- **Complications:** Peripheral neuropathy most common (60-70% of patients)
- **Genetics:** Strong family history increases risk significantly

### **Section B Answers:** 9-3, 10-2, 11-2, 12-3, 13-3, 14-3, 15-3

**Learning Points:**
- **Exercise:** 150 minutes moderate-intensity + resistance training 2x/week
- **Treatment:** Metformin remains first-line unless contraindicated
- **Targets:** HbA1c <7.0% for most patients (individualized)
- **SMBG:** Most valuable for insulin users and hypoglycemia-prone patients
- **Hypoglycemia:** Oral carbs first (Rule of 15), then glucagon/IM/subcutaneous
- **Prevention:** DPP showed 58% risk reduction with lifestyle intervention
- **Screening:** NPCDCS recommends screening from age 30

### **Section C Answers:** 16-3, 17-3, 18-1, 19-2, 20-3

**Learning Points:**
- **Programs:** NPCDCS provides free OHA through PHCs and subcenters
- **ASHAs:** Key community link - awareness, follow-up, referral (not diagnosis/prescription)
- **Anthropometry:** Lower cutoffs for Asian Indians (>80cm female, >90cm male)
- **Screening:** All adults ≥30 years as per NPCDCS guidelines
- **Outcomes:** Longer-term measures like follow-up better than immediate counts
