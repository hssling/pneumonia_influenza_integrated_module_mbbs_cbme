# Chapter 15: Toxicology and Drug-Induced ECG Changes

## Learning Objectives
After completing this chapter, the reader will be able to:
1. Recognize ECG manifestations of electrolyte and drug-induced abnormalities
2. Understand QT interval prolongation and its clinical implications
3. Identify ECG patterns associated with overdose and intoxication
4. Apply knowledge of drug interactions in cardiac rhythm management
5. Implement appropriate clinical management for ECG abnormalities

## Introduction

Drug-induced ECG changes are common in clinical practice and can lead to serious arrhythmias or mimic cardiac pathology. Understanding these patterns enables clinicians to diagnose medication-related toxicity and prevent adverse outcomes. This chapter covers the spectrum of toxicological ECG manifestations and their management implications.

## Electrolyte Abnormalities and ECG

### Hyperkalemia (“Tall T Wave”) Syndrome

#### ECG Features
- **Tall, peaked T waves**: Most visible in precordial leads
- **Narrow, peaked P waves**: Atrial depolarization changes
- **Progressive PR interval prolongation**: Atrioventricular conduction delay
- **Widened QRS complex**: Intraventricular conduction slowing
- **Serrated QT interval**: Resulting in sinusoidal pattern

#### Mechanisms
- **K+ channel blockade**: Abnormal repolarization current flow
- **Conduction system depression**: Potassium flux imbalances
- **Automaticity alteration**: Enhanced abnormal automaticity risk
- **Excitation threshold reduction**: Increased myocardial irritability

#### Clinical Management
- **Calcium gluconate administration**: Membrane stabilization priority
- **Sodium polystyrene sulfonate**: Potassium removal therapy
- **Insulin-glucose infusion**: Intracellular potassium shift
- **Dialysis indications**: Non-responsive or cardiovascular deterioration

### Hypokalemia (“U Wave”) Pattern

#### ECG Manifestations
- **Prominent U waves**: Increased amplitude (>25% of T wave height)
- **ST segment depression**: Slight velvity segment lowering
- **T wave flattening**: Reduced electrodiacry amplitude
- **P-wave changes**: Slight widening possible
- **Premature contractions**: Increased ventricular ectopy incidence

#### Pathophysiological Basis
- **Repolarization prolongation**: Potassium uptake reduction impact
- **Afterdebbbpolarization risk**: Enhanced triggered activity potential
- **Automaticity enhancement**: Myocardial irritability elevation
- **QT interval management**: Interval standardization considerations

#### Therapeutic Approaches
- **Potassium chloride supplementation**: Oral or parenteral repletion
- **Magnesium cosupplementation**: Complete intracellular replenishment
- **NTTP
- **Arrhythmia monitoring**: Close surveillance restriction prevention

### Calcium Abnormalities

#### Hypercalcemia ECG
- **Shortened QT interval**: Rapid repolarization consequence
- **Widened T waves**: Hyperacute appearance possible
- **First-degree AV block**: Atrioventricular conduction delay
- **J-point elevation**: J-wave syndrome associations
- **Resistance to cardiokinesis**: Atrial arrhythmia management

#### Hypocalcemia Effects
- **Prolonged QT interval**: Delayed ventricular repolarization
- **T-wave shortening**: Repolarization abnormality manifestation
- **U-wave prominence**: Prominent relaxation period presence
- **Neuromuscular irritability**: Chvostek's sign associations
- **QT prolongation management**: Arrhythmia prevention strategies

### Magnesium Abnormalities

#### Hypermagnesemia
- **PRT prolongation**: Atrioventricular conduction depression
- **QRS complex widening**: Intraventricular conduction delay
- **AV node blockade**: Progressive atrioventricular inhibition
- **Sinus node inhibition**: Bradycardia and arrest risk elevation

#### Hypomagnesemia
- **QT interval prolongation**: Medication synchronization
- **T-wave abnormalities**: Repolarization disturbance patterns
- **Supraventricular arrhythmias**: Familial fibrosis mechanisms
- **Ventricular arrhythmias**: Torsades de pointes predisposition
- **Electrolyte correction**: Magnesium replacement protocolization

## Antiarrhythmic Drug Effects by Class

### Class IA Antiarrhythmics

#### Mechanism of Action
- **Sodium channel blockade**: Fast channel inhibition pharmacodynamic effect
- **Repolarization prolongation**: Delayed potassium current consequences
- **Atrioventricular conduction depression**: Calcium channel secondary effects
- **Use-dependent blocking**: Rate-dependent influence on ion channels

#### ECG Considerations
- **QT interval prolongation**: Class IA characteristic effect
- **QRS widening**: Conduction velocity reduction demonstration
- **Significantly bradycardia**: Sinus node automaticity suppression
- **Proarrhythmic potential**: Torsades de pointes development risk

#### Clinical Examples
- **Quinidine considerations**: QT prolongation and diarrhea associations
- **Procainamide sotalol effects**: Lupus-like syndrome risk elevation
- **Disopyramide coronary vasospasm**: Significant cardiovascular toxicity risk
- **Proarrhythmic monitoring**: Serial ECG evaluation commitment

### Class IC Antiarrhythmics

#### Pharmacotherapeutic Profile
- **Pronounced sodium channel blockade**: Marked conduction velocity reduction
- **Minimal repolarization effects**: Potassium channel sparing approach
- **Atrioventricular conduction slowing**: PR/QRS interval prolongation
- **Supraventricular tachycardia control**: Reentry circuit disruption method

#### ECG Manifestations
- **Marked QRS prolongation**: Significant intracy tical delay demonstration
- **AV conduction depression**: PR interval lengthening evidence
- **QT interval preservation**: Minimal retrolarization effect
- **Bundle branch block appearance**: Aberrant conduction presentation

#### Representative Agents
- **Flecainide effectiveness**: Sustained atrial fibrillation treatment
- **Proprafenone considerations**: Moderate sodium channel suppression
- **Encainide complications**: Increased ICD abbreviation development

### Class III Antiarrhythmic Agents

#### Pharmacological Mechanisms
- **Potassium channel inhibition**: Delayed rectifier current blockade
- **Repolarization prolongation**: Characteristic QT interval extension
- **Action potential duration increase**: Enhanced electrical stability
- **Anti-depolarization effect**: Early afterdepolarization prevention

#### ECG Parameters
- **QT interval prolongation**: Primary electrocardiographic effect
- **T-wave morphology change**: Flattened or biphasic appearance
- **Ventricular arrhythmia reduction**: SCD prevention effectiveness
- **Proarrhythmic risk management**: Polynucleotide arrhythmia monitoring

#### Therapeutic Examples
- **Amiodarone considerations**: Widespread system toxicity association
- **Sotalol effects**: Beta-blockade combination therapy benefit
- **Dofetilide precautions**: Renal function monitoring requirement
- **Ibutilide applications**: Rapid atrial arrhythmia termination

## Psychotropic Medications

### Tricyclic Antidepressants

#### Cardiotoxicity Mechanisms
- **Sodium channel blockade**: QRS complex widening pathogenesis
- **Anticholinergic effects**: Sinus tachycardia production cause
- **QT interval prolongation**: Torsades de pointes arrhythmia risk
- **Histamine receptor antagonism**: Immunoprovisional effects presentation

#### ECG Features
- **Rightward axis deviation**: Terminal QRS complex rightward shift
- **Widened QRS complex**: >0.12 second conduction disturbance
- **QT interval prolongation**: Repolarization delay characteristic
- **Secondary T-wave abnormalities**: Non-specific morphology changes

#### Clinical Correlations
- **Seizure threshold reduction**: Convulsant properties demonstration
- **Hypotension development**: Alpha-adrenergic blockade consequence
- **Arrhythmia predisposition**: Ventricular tachycardia risk elevation
- **Overdose lethality**: Management urgency documentation

### Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors

#### Cardiovascular Effects
- **QT interval prolongation**: Torsades de pointes risk concern
- **Bradyarrhythmia occurrence**: Sinus node suppression possibility
- **Serotonin syndrome syndrome review**: Toxicological interaction monitoring
- **Hyponatremia association**: SIADH induction mechanism

#### ECG Monitoring
- **Baseline QT measurement**: Pre-treatment assessment requirement
- **Serial ECG evaluation**: Concentration dependent effect monitoring
- **Drug interactions recognition**: CYP metabolism factor consideration
- **Risk factor reduction**: Age and cardiac disease modification

### Atypical Antipsychotics

#### QT Prolongation Concerns
- **Delayed potassium conductance**: QT interval extension mechanism
- **Torsades de pointes association**: Ventricular tachycardia susceptibility
- **Sudden cardiac death correlation**: Long-term usage implication
- **Electrolyte abnormality potentiation**: Hypokalemia synergy effect

#### Risk Stratification
- **Dose-dependent toxicity**: Cumulative exposure relationship
- **Congenital QT syndrome**: Baseline risk factor evaluation
- **Electrolyte monitoring**: Potassium and magnesium correction
- **Alternative medication selection**: Low-risk agent replacement

## Antimicrobial and Other Drug Effects

### Macrolide Antibiotics

#### Mechanism of Interaction
- **QT interval prolongation**: CYP3A4 inhibition consequence
- **Torsades de pointes induction**: Polymorphic ventricular tachycardia
- **Protein binding competition**: Digoxin displacement interaction
- **CYP metabolism interference**: Tacrolimus and warfarin effects

#### Clinical Management
- **QT monitoring requirement**: Pre-treatment baseline establishment
- **Alternative antimicrobial selection**: Azithromycin conversion risk
- **Electrolyte correction**: Potassium and magnesium optimization
- **EKG monitoring initiation**: Symptom-free QT prevention protocols

### Chemotherapy Agents

#### Anthracycline Cardiotoxicity
- **QRS voltage reduction**: Left ventricular systolic function impairment
- **QT interval prolongation**: Repolarization abnormality induction
- **Atrial arrhythmia precipitation**: POAF occurrence mechanism
- **Left bundle branch block**: Conduction system toxicity manifestation

#### Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors
- **QT interval prolongation**: QTc extension >500 ms risk
- **T-wave morphology changes**: Flattening and inversion patterns
- **Bradyarrhythmic consequences**: Sinus node dysfunction occurrence
- **Sudden cardiac death potential**: Dose-dependent toxicity relationship

### Recreational Drug Toxicity

#### Cocaine Cardiotoxicity
- **Sympathetic stimulation elevation**: Catecholamine surge mechanism
- **Coronary vasospasm induction**: Myocardial ischemia pathogenesis
- **QT interval prolongation risk**: Torsades de pointes predisposition
- **Ventricular tachycardia potential**: Monomoprocessed arrhythmia trigger

#### Amphetamine Effects
- **Tachycardia development**: Sympathetic nervous system activation
- **Hypertension association**: Vascular tone elevation consequence
- **QT interval alteration**: Prolongation or shortening possibility
- **Arterial vasospamtion**: Myocardial oxygen demand mismatch

#### Cannabinoid Derivatives
- **Transient bradycardia occurrence**: Vagal stimulation effect
- **Orthostatic hypotension risk**: Vascular resistance reduction
- **Atrial fibrillation provocation**: Lone atrial fibrillation trigger
- **Structural heart disease concern**: Underlying cardiac pathology consideration

## ECG Monitoring for Drug Therapy

### Baseline Assessment
- **Pre-treatment ECG recording**: QT interval measurement standard
- **Risk factor identification**: Familial QTC prolongation screening
- **Electrolyte level evaluation**: Potassium, magnesium, calcium verification
- **Medication review completion**: Potential interaction identification

### Serial Monitoring Protocols
- **Post-initiation monitoring**: First week clinical evaluation priority
- **QT interval quantification**: QTc calculation standardized methodology
- **Symptom assessment inclusion**: New arrhythmia symptom investigation
- **Dose adjustment consideration**: Toxicity manifestation management

### Risk Mitigation Strategies
- **Alternative therapy selection**: Reduced QT prolongation agent preference
- **Electrocardiographical family screening**: Genetic arrhythmia evaluation
- **Prophylactic cardiac protection**: Beta-blocker magnesium coating
- **Multidisciplinary communication**: Comprehensive pharmacokinetic monitoring

## Clinical Management Algorithms

### Acute Overdose Presentation
- **ABC stabilization priority**: Airway breathing circulation maintenance
- **Gastric decontamination consideration**: Limited effectiveness recognition
- **Specific antidotes utilization**: QRS widening calcium administration
- **Hemodynamic support requirement**: Vasopressor agent selection criteria

### Drug-Induced Arrhythmia Management
- **Hemodynamic stability assessment**: Confusion start anticoagulation consideration
- **Epidemiological categorization**: Sustained vs nonsustained rhythm qualification
- **Defibrillation temporal consideration**: Potential spontaneous resolution evaluation
- **Ischemic etiology investigation**: Thyroid medication review determination

### Chronic Toxicity Prevention
- **Therapeutic drug monitoring**: Antiarrhythmic concentration optimization
- **Serial ECG documentation**: QT interval trending establishment
- **Electrolyte abnormality correction**: Repletion strategy implementation
- **Patient education emphasis**: Self-monitoring impact recognition

## Key Takeaways

1. Drug-induced ECG changes are frequently encountered and require systematic evaluation for toxicity identification.
2. QT interval prolongation represents a critical proarrhythmic marker with torsades de pointes risk elevation.
3. Electrolyte abnormalities produce characteristic ECG patterns that guide targeted therapeutic intervention.
4. Antiarrhythmic drug effects vary by pharmacological classification with Class IA/III agents notably proarrhythmic.
5. Psychotropic medications commonly cause QT interval prolongation requiring careful baseline and monitoring assessment.
6. Antimicrobial chemotherapy agents demonstrate significant cardiac toxicity with ECG monitoring recommendations.
7. Recreational drug usage displays varied cardiovascular effects dependent on mechanism of sympathetic stimulation.
8. Comprehensive medication review and ECG monitoring represent essential strategies for adverse event prevention.
9. Clinical management requires multidisciplinary approach integrating cardiology pharmacy and toxicology expertise.
10. Patient education and drug interaction awareness serve as primary prevention strategies for medication cardiac toxicity.

## Clinical Cases

### Case 1: Antipsychotic QT Prolongation
A psychiatric inpatient developed new-onset palpitations five days post-atypical antipsychotic initiation. Serial ECG monitoring demonstrated progressive QT interval prolongation exceeding 500 ms.

### Case 2: Digoxin Toxicity Manifestation
Elderly patient presented with progressive lethargy and atrial fibrillation with slow ventricular response. ECG revealed tall peaked T waves and decreased amplitude P waves consistent with digoxin toxicity.

### Case 3: Tricyclic Overdose Presentation
Comatose patient brought by ambulance showed widened QRS complexes exceeding 200 ms with retrograde P waves suggestive of tricyclic antidepressant intoxication.
