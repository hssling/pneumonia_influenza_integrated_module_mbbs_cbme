# Chapter 13: ECG in Critical and Emergency Conditions

## Learning Objectives
After completing this chapter, the reader will be able to:
1. Identify ECG manifestations of acute respiratory failure and pulmonary embolism
2. Differentiate between cardiac tamponade and constrictive pericarditis
3. Recognize ECG patterns in shock states and hypotension
4. Apply ECG findings in trauma and postoperative cardiac monitoring
5. Understand ECG interpretation in the critically ill patient

## Introduction

Critical illness frequently involves cardiac complications, with ECG changes providing crucial diagnostic and prognostic information. In emergency and intensive care settings, ECG patterns often guide immediate therapeutic decisions and predict outcomes. Understanding these alterations enables healthcare providers to deliver timely, evidence-based care in high-stakes clinical environments.

## Acute Respiratory Failure and Mechanical Ventilation

### ECG Changes in Acute Respiratory Failure

#### Respiratory Acidosis
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Compensatory response to hypoxia and acidosis
- **P Pulmonale Development**: Right atrial strain pattern (>2.5 mm in lead II)
- **Right Ventricular Strain**: Right axis deviation and tall R waves in V1
- **ST Segment Depression**: Diffuse, likely due to subendocardial ischemia
- **Arrhythmias**: Frequent atrial fibrillation and premature ventricular complexes

#### Positive Pressure Ventilation Effects
- **Cardiac Output Reduction**: Decreased venous return affects ventricular filling
- **QT Interval Prolongation**: Mechanical ventilation impact on repolarization
- **T-wave Abnormalities**: Nonspecific flattening or inversion patterns
- **Axis Changes**: Clockwise rotation from lung expansion alterations
- **Lead Positioning**: Modified precordial lead placement in ICU patients

### ARDS-Specific ECG Findings
- **Tachycardia Universal**: Present in virtually all ARDS cases
- **P Pulmonale Prevalence**: Higher incidence with severe hypoxemia
- **Ventricular Conduction**: Incomplete right bundle branch block patterns
- **Low Voltage Limb Leads**: Due to lung hyperinflation and pleural effusion
- **Recovery Correlations**: ECG improvements parallel clinical resolution

## Pulmonary Embolism and Cor Pulmonale

### Acute Pulmonary Embolism Classic Findings

#### Massive Pulmonary Embolism Pattern
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Immediate compensation for hypoxemia
- **Right Bundle Branch Block**: Incomplete to complete RBBB development
- **S1Q3T3 Pattern**: Deep S in lead I, Q and T in III (S1, Q3, T3)
- **Right Axis Deviation**: Acute right ventricular strain manifestation
- **P Pulmonale Recognition**: Prominent P waves in patients with pulmonary hypertension

#### Submassive Pulmonary Embolism
- **Heart Rate Elevation**: Tachycardia out of proportion to systemic response
- **Incomplete RBBB**: Rsr' pattern in V1 becoming progressively abnormal
- **T-wave Inversions**: Anterior precordial leads with prolonged QT interval
- **Atrial Arrhythmias**: New-onset atrial fibrillation development
- **D 892 Sign**: Differential diagnosis challenge with S1Q3ot3 pattern

### Chronic Pulmonary Hypertension Evolution
- **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy**: Tall R waves in V1-V2, deep S in V5-V6
- **Right Atrial Enlargement**: P pulmonale with notched P wave morphology
- **Strain Pattern Enhancement**: Secondary ST-T changes in right precordial leads
- **QRS Fragmentation**: Multiple RsR' complexes suggestive of fibrosis
- **Low Voltage Recognition**: Generalized amplitude reduction in limb leads

### Diagnostic Utility and Limitations
- **Sensitivity Assessment**: 10-30% of PE patients demonstrate S1Q3T3 pattern
- **Branch Artery Distribution**: Posterior basal segment embolism challenges
- **Imaging Correlation**: CT pulmonary angiogram definitive diagnostic confirmation
- **Risk Stratification**: Large clot burden associated with ECG abnormalities

## Cardiac Tamponade and Constrictive Pericarditis

### Cardiac Tamponade ECG Characteristics

#### Beck's Triad Recognition
- **Hypotension**: Decreased cardiac output compensation mechanisms
- **Jugular Venous Distension**: Elevated jugular venous pressure manifestation
- **Muffled Heart Sounds**: Pericardial fluid interference with auscultation

#### ECG Manifestations
- **Electrical Alternans**: Beat-to-beat variation in QRS complex amplitude
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Compensatory response to decreased stroke volume
- **Low Voltage Complexes**: Attenuation from surrounding pericardial fluid
- **P Wave Aberrancy**: Intra-atrial conduction delay with widened P waves
- **Generalized ST Depression**: Subendocardial ischemia pattern development

### Constrictive Pericarditis Differentiation
- **Absence of Pulsus Paradoxus**: No respiratory variation in blood pressure
- **Persistent Low Voltage**: Chronic pericardial constriction effects
- **AV Valve Regurgitation**: Echocardiographic findings suggestive of pathology
- **Myocardial Restriction**: Doppler patterns showing constrictive physiology
- **Surgical Intervention**: Pericardiectomy consideration for refractory cases

### Diagnostic Challenge Differentiation
- **Echocardiography Correlation**: Doppler findings confirm tamponade pathophysiology
- **CT Scan Utility**: Pericardial thickening characteristic of constrictive disease
- **Clinical Progressions**: Rapid deterioration in tamponade versus chronic course
- **Therapeutic Responses**: Pericardiocentesis immediate improvement in tamponade
- **Comprehensive Assessment**: Multimodal imaging essential for accurate diagnosis

## Shock States and Hypotension ECG Patterns

### Cardiogenic Shock Characteristics
- **Tachycardia Responses**: Compensatory catecholamine release mechanisms
- **ST Elevation Patterns**: Ongoing myocardial ischemia manifestations
- **QRS Widening Risks**: Conduction delays with electrolyte abnormalities
- **Low Voltage Complexes**: Reduced cardiac output cardiac muscle contraction
- **Arrhythmic Disturbances**: Premature ventricular contractions and tachycardia episodes

### Distributive Shock (Septic/Cardiogenic Mix)
- **Relative Bradycardia**: Inappropriate heart rate compared to shock severity
- **QT Prolongation Effects**: Electrolyte imbalances typically causative factors
- **Atrial Arrhythmias**: Frequent atrial fibrillation in critically ill patients
- **Bundle Branch Blocks**: Pre-existing conduction system disease manifestations
- **Resolution Correlations**: ECG recovery parallel to clinical stabilization

### Hypovolemic Shock Recognition
- **Tachycardia Prevalence**: Early compensation for volume depletion
- **QRS Amplitude Reduction**: Decreased preload ventricular filling impact
- **T-wave Abnormalities**: Nonspecific changes from metabolic disturbances
- **AV Block Occurrences**: Transient blocks during severe hypotension episodes
- **Recovery Patterns**: Rapid ECG normalization with fluid resuscitation success

## Trauma-Related ECG Changes

### Blunt Cardiac Trauma Considerations
- **Arrhythmic Vulnerabilities**: Ventricular tachycardia and fibrillation risks
- **Conduction System Injuries**: Bundle branch blocks and AV dissociation
- **Myocardial Contusion Patterns**: Segmental wall motion abnormalities
- **Pericardial Involvement**: Hemopericardium development with tamponade risks
- **Valvular Disruption**: Chordal rupture causing acute regurgitation

### Myocardial Contusion Diagnosis
- **Troponin Elevation**: Biochemical markers cardiac muscle injury detection
- **Echocardiographic Correlation**: Regional wall motion abnormalities confirmation
- **Serial ECG Monitoring**: Evolution tracking over initial 48 hours critical
- **Arrhythmia Surveillance**: Continuous monitoring myocardial irritability
- **Clinical Correlation**: Chest pain assessment with radiographic findings

### Penetrating Trauma Assessment
- **Immediate Stabilization**: ABC prioritization airway, breathing, circulation
- **Cardiac Tamponade**: Rapid deterioration pericardial sac accumulation
- **Pericardial Window**: Drainage procedure emergent cardiac decompression
- **Surgical Exploration**: Definitive repair penetrating cardiac injuries
- **Arrhythmia Management**: Temporary pacing potentially life-saving intervention

## Postoperative Cardiac Monitoring

### Perioperative Myocardial Infarction Detection
- **Isolated T-wave Inversions**: Commonly observed postoperative phenomenon
- **Continuous ST Monitoring**: Most sensitive myocardial ischemia detection
- **Troponin Correlation**: Biochemical markers post-operative risk stratification
- **Regional Wall Motion**: Echocardiographic assessment ischemia localization
- **Clinical Context Integration**: Baseline comparison crucial risk assessment

### Postoperative Arrhythmias Assessment
- **Atrial Fibrillation Incidence**: 20-40% post cardiac surgery patients
- **Junctional Rhythms**: Expected early postoperative recovery period
- **Ventricular Arrhythmias**: Careful monitoring tachycardia prone periods
- **Pacemaker Dependencies**: Temporary pacing transition permanent device selection
- **Antiarrhythmia Prophylaxis**: Beta-blocker continuation perioperative periods

### High-Risk Surgery Categories
- **Vascular Surgery Patients**: Atherosclerotic burden perioperative MI risks
- **Emergency Operations**: Hemodynamic instability intubation challenges
- **Diabetes Mellitus Correlation**: Independent perioperative cardiac risk factor
- **Chronic Kidney Disease**: Pre-existing cardiac conditions complications
- **Advanced Age Considerations**: Multidisciplinary perioperative risk assessments

## Neurological Emergencies and Cardiac Effects

### Subarachnoid Hemorrhage Effects
- **QT Prolongation Patterns**: Significant arrhythmic risk development
- **T-wave Inversions**: Nonspecific but concerning postoperative findings
- **Myocardial Stunning**: Takotsubo cardiomyopathy neurological trigger potential
- **Neurogenic Pulmonary Edema**: Mechanical ventilation respiratory support requirement
- **Dexamethasone Treatment**: Early intervention neurological injury mitigation

### Status Epilepticus Recognition
- **Tachycardia Responses**: Expected catecholamine surge activation mechanism
- **ST Segment Elevation**: Transient changes post-ictal myocardial ischemia
- **QT Interval Changes**: Prolongation with torsades de pointes risk potential
- **Electrolyte Monitoring**: Critical calcium and magnesium level assessments
- **Anticonvulsant Therapy**: Benzodiazepine loading early intervention protocol

### Traumatic Brain Injury Considerations
- **Hemodynamic Responses**: Bradycardia hypertension Cushing reflex development
- **Myocardial Injury Detection**: Troponin test elevated values investigation
- **Arrhythmic Propensities**: Atrial fibrillation perioperative complication risk
- **Endocrine Disruption**: Pituitary injury hormonal imbalance cardiac effects
- **Multi-organ Assessment**: Systemic evaluation necessary comprehensive care

## Renal Failure and Cardiac Interactions

### Uremic Cardiomyopathy Evolution
- **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy**: Pressure overload pathophysiology development
- **Amyloid Deposition**: Beta-2 microglobulin significance recognition
- **Conduction System Involvement**: Bundle branch blocks electrophysiological disturbances
- **Pericardial Inflammation**: Uremic pericarditis ECG voltage reduction
- **Reverse Remodeling Potential**: Strict hemodialysis compliance improvements

### Electrolyte Abnormalities in ESRD
- **Hyperkalemia Recognition**: Tall T waves distinctive emergency finding
- **QT Interval Management**: Calcium administration immediate stabilization
- **Hypocalcemia Correlation**: Prolonged QT interval calcium supplementation
- **Magnesium Abnormalities**: Torsades de pointes potential magnesium repletion
- **Phosphate Imbalances**: ECMO induction extreme refractory cases

### Dialysis-Related Cardiac Events
- **Intradialytic Hypotension**: Myocardial stunning repetitive dialysis cycles
- **Arrhythmic Risk Amplification**: Post-dialysis potassium rebound potential
- **Pericardial Inflammation**: Viral etiology infections cardiac involvement
- **Valvular Calcification**: Advanced calcification severe regurgitation risk
- **Management Strategies**: Optimized dialysis protocols cardiac risk reduction

## Key Takeaways

1. Critical illness ECG interpretation requires consideration of underlying physiological stress and organ system interactions.
2. Acute pulmonary embolism presents classic S1Q3T3 pattern, though sensitivity remains limited.
3. Cardiac tamponade demonstrates electrical alternans, guiding emergent pericardiocentesis.
4. Shock states manifest tachycardia with variable conduction and repolarization abnormalities.
5. Postoperative ECG monitoring detects myocardial ischemia and arrhythmia complications.
6. Neurological emergencies produce secondary cardiac manifestations requiring comprehensive management.
7. Renal failure patients exhibit characteristic conduction abnormalities and electrolyte-related ECG changes.
8. Clinical correlation remains essential, integrating ECG findings with echocardiographic and biochemical data.

## Clinical Cases

### Case 1: Acute Pulmonary Embolism Presentation
A 65-year-old postoperative patient presented with acute dyspnea and chest pain. ECG demonstrated sinus tachycardia at 120 bpm with right bundle branch block and S1Q3T3 pattern. Immediate CT pulmonary angiogram confirmed large bilateral pulmonary emboli.

### Case 2: Cardiac Tamponade Recognition
A 70-year-old patient post-cardiac surgery developed tachycardia hypotension and pulsus paradoxus. ECG revealed electrical alternans with low voltage complexes. Urgent echocardiography confirmed large pericardial effusion requiring drainage.

### Case 3: Postoperative Myocardial Infarction
A 75-year-old vascular surgery patient developed asymptomatic troponin elevation postoperatively. Continuous ST monitoring revealed evolving ST depression with T-wave inversions in lateral leads, confirmed myocardial infarction by cardiac catheterization.
