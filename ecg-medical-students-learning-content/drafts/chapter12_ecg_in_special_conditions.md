## Introduction

ECG interpretation requires consideration of various physiological states and systemic conditions that can alter cardiac electrical activity. Metabolic derangements, endocrine disorders, systemic diseases, and special populations each present unique ECG challenges. Understanding these alterations prevents misdiagnosis and enhances clinical correlation for accurate patient assessment.

## Metabolic and Electrolyte Disorders

### Hyperkalemia

#### Pathophysiology
- **Potassium Excess**: Cellular depolarization affecting action potential generation
- **Conduction Delay**: Progressive slowing through specialized conduction system
- **Cardiac Toxicity**: Risk of asystole or malignant ventricular arrhythmias
- **Urgent Recognition**: Critical for immediate therapeutic intervention

#### ECG Features
- **Tall T Waves**: First manifestation with symmetric peaking
- **QRS Widening**: Progressive conduction delay beyond 7.0 mEq/L
- **Flat P Waves**: Atrial conduction failure with decreased amplitude
- **Sine Wave Pattern**: Pre-terminal finding indicating imminent arrest
- **Conduction Blocks**: AV dissociation with ventricular escape rhythms

#### Clinical Correlation
- **Creatinine Evaluation**: Assess renal function status immediately
- **Blood Glucose**: Differential consideration for hyperglycemia
- **Calcium Administration**: Supportive treatment until definitive management
- **Dialysis Consideration**: For refractory or severe electrolyte abnormalities

### Hypokalemia

#### Pathophysiological Basis
- **Potassium Deficit**: Impaired repolarization causing prolonged action potentials
- **Ventricular Arrhythmias**: Increased automaticity and re-entry risk
- **Muscle Weakness**: Skeletal and cardiac manifestations
- **Digitalis Toxicity**: Enhanced susceptibility to rhythm disturbances

#### ECG Manifestations
- **ST Depression**: Prominent T-wave changes with plateau morphology
- **T-wave Inversion**: Flattened or asymmetrically inverted configurations
- **U-wave Prominence**: Increased amplitude with merging into T waves
- **QT Prolongation**: Risk stratification for adverse arrhythmia outcomes
- **Arrhythmic Tendencies**: PVCs and ventricular tachycardia associations

#### Potassium Repletion Strategy
- **Oral Supplementation**: Magnesium monitoring for adequate replacement
- **Intravenous Interventions**: Symptomatic patients with ECG abnormalities
- **Serial ECG Patterns**: Continuous monitoring for improvement timelines
- **Polystyrene Binding**: Severe cases requiring specialized preparations

### Hypercalcemia and Hypocalcemia

#### Hypercalcemia Lon ECG Responses
- **QT Shortening**: Rapid repolarization with narrowed QTc measurement
- **T-wave Peaking**: Accelerated ventricular recovery pattern
- **J-wave Formation**: Osborne wave appearance in hypothermic patients
- **Conduction Effects**: Minimal impact on atrioventricular conduction pathway

#### Hypocalcemia Induced Changes
- **QT Prolongation**: Delayed repolarization sympathetic nervous activation
- **T-wave Alterations**: Flat or profoundly inverted morphology
- **U-wave Amplitudes**: Prominent ventricular relaxation markers
- **Torsades Risk**: Genetic syndrome predisposing individuals involved

#### Therapeutic Considerations
- **Calcium Supplementation**: Parenteral options for acute correction needs
- **Vitamin D Management**: Underlying deficiency treatment protocol
- **Parathyroid Dysfunction**: Endocrine etiology investigation plan
- **Monitoring Strategy**: Sequential ECG assessment post-intervention

### Acid-Base Imbalances

#### Acidosis Implications
- **Repolarization Changes**: Wide complex tachycardia resembling Brugada pattern
- **Heightened Sensitivity**: Cardiac rhythm disturbance predisposition
- **Ventricular Arrhythmias**: Nonsustained episodes with potential hemodynamic impact
- **Sodium Bicarbonate**: Urgent correction protocol for severe metabolic abnormalities

#### Alkalosis Effects
- **QT Interval Reduction**: Shortened ventricular repolarization phases
- **T-wave Modifications**: Flattened morphology with diminished amplitudes
- **U-wave Reductions**: Less prominent ventricular relaxation indicators
- **Concurrent Electrolyte Shifts**: Potassium and magnesium level correlations

## Endocrine Disorders

### Thyroid Dysfunction

#### Hyperthyroidism Manifestations
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Resting heart rate elevations beyond 100 bpm
- **Atrial Fibrillation Prevalence**: Increased incidence with thyrotoxicosis involvement
- **Ventricular Preexcitation**: Wolf-Parkinson-White syndrome associations
- **Rate-related Cardiomyopathy**: High output cardiac failure potential

#### Hypothyroidism Features
- **Sinus Bradycardia**: Resting heart rate reductions below 60 bpm
- **QRS Broadening**: Intraventricular conduction delay characteristics
- **T-wave Depressions**: Low-voltage repolarization abnormalities
- **Pericardial Effusions**: Electrical alternans rhythm disturbances

#### Clinical Management
- **Anti-thyroid Therapies**: Radioactive iodine and pharmacological interventions
- **Beta-blocker Stabilization**: Symptom control during treatment initiation
- **Serial Cardiac Assessments**: Echocardiography and ECG follow-up monitoring
- **Endocrine Specialist Consultations**: Comprehensive hormonal replacement strategies

### Hypoglycemia and Hyperglycemia

#### Hypoglycemic States
- **Tachycardic Responses**: Sympathetic nervous system activation markers
- **Conduction Anomalies**: Temporary atrioventricular block manifestations
- **Prolonged QT Intervals**: Repolarization delay with arrhythmia risks
- **Urgent Glucose Administration**: Restoration of normal cardiac electrophysiological function

#### Diabetic Complications
- **Silent Myocardial Ischemia**: Typical angina symptom absence
- **Autonomic Neuropathy**: Cardiac innervation impairment effects
- **Cardiovascular Disease Burden**: Accelerated atherosclerosis progression
- **ECG Hypertension Correlations**: Left ventricular hypertrophy associations

### Pheochromocytoma Genetics
- **Paroxysmal Hypertension**: Episodic blood pressure elevation episodes
- **T-wave Abnormality Development**: Dynamic apex-focused changes
- **Myocardial Oxygen Supply Mismatches**: Catecholamine-induced coronary vasoconstriction

## Systemic Diseases with ECG Manifestations

### Systemic Hypertension

#### ECG Hypertension Patterns
- **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy**: Voltage criteria Sokolow-Lyon quantification
- **Strain Repolarization**: Asymmetrically inverted lateral precordial leads
- **P-wave Enlargement**: Left atrial pressure elevation indicators
- **RBBB Prevalence**: Increased right bundle branch block occurrences

#### Prognostic Significance
- **Mortality Risk Assessment**: ECG scoring system coronary artery disease discrimination
- **Therapeutic Intervention Trials**: Antihypertensive medication efficacy evaluations
- **LVH Regression Potential**: Treatment response serial ECG monitoring
- **Diastolic Dysfunction Detection**: Novovec concepts limit diagnostic utility

### Acute Pulmonary Embolism

#### ECG Embolus Indicators
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Most frequent initial arrhythmia manifestation
- **T-wave Inverting**: Right precordial chronic anteroseptal lead inversions
- **P-pulmonale Development**: Atrial pressure elevation morphological changes
- **RBBB Progression**: Complete right bundle branch block development
- **Clockwise Rotation**: Cardiac axis deviation possible occurrence

#### Advanced Patterns
- **Right Ventricular Stress**: RV strain triangle configuration
- **Precordial Balance Shifts**: Increased R-wave inferior V1-3 representations
- **Atrial Flutter Potential**: Rapid atrial rhythms complicating presentation
- **Low Voltage Patterns**: Extensive embolization pericardial effusion simulations

#### Diagnostic Utility Limitations
- **Nonspecific Presentation**: Alternative diagnostic pathway justifications
- **D-Dimer Correlation**: Laboratory confirmation supporting ECG findings
- **Imaging Confirmation**: CT pulmonary angiography definitive diagnoses
- **Risk Stratification Tools**: Clinical probability assessment integration

- **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus**: Libman-Sacks endocarditis rhythm disturbance risks

## Clinical Cases

### Case 1: Hyperkalemic ECG Recognition
A dialysis patient presented with weakness and cardiac monitoring demonstrated tall T-waves with widened QRS complexes.

**Interpretation:** Hyperkalemia requiring immediate intervention confirmed with laboratory correlates.
**Management Strategy:** Insulin-glucose intravenous insulin therapy and calcium gluconate administration.

### Case 2: Thyrotoxic Atrial Fibrillation
Young patient with weight loss and palpitations showed irregular narrow complex tachycardia without distinct P waves.

**ECG Analysis:** Atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response thyrotoxicosis induced.
**Therapeutic Approach:** Thyroid hormonal normalization and anticoagulation medications.

### Case 3: Elderly Patient with Mobitz II Block
Nursing home resident experienced syncopal episodes with ECG showing constant PR intervals followed by dropped QRS complexes.

**Diagnosis Confirmation:** Second-degree AV block type II requiring permanent pacemaker consideration.
**Management Decisions:** Cardiac pacemaker implantation with monitoring outpatient follow-up.

### Case 4: Infant with VSD Findings
Baby with prominent murmur showed increased R-wave voltages in left precordial leads with abnormal progression patterns.

**ECG Interpretation:** Left ventricular volume overload suggesting ventricular septal defect confirmation.
**Diagnostic Pathway:** Echocardiography definitive characterization and surgical planning discussion.
