# ECG Learning Content Outline

## Unit 1: ECG Fundamentals

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Electrocardiography
- Historical background of ECG discovery
- Basic principles of cardiac electrophysiology
- Cardiac conduction system anatomy
- ECG as a diagnostic tool in clinical practice
- Clinical relevance and indications for ECG

### Chapter 2: ECG Recording Technique and Lead Systems
- ECG machine setup and calibration
- Electrode placement and skin preparation
- Standard 12-lead ECG system
- Limb leads (I, II, III, aVR, aVL, aVF)
- Precordial leads (V1-V6)

### Chapter 3: ECG Waves, Segments, and Intervals
- P wave: atrial depolarization
- PR interval: atrioventricular conduction
- QRS complex: ventricular depolarization
- ST segment: phase 2 of ventricular repolarization
- T wave: phase 3 of ventricular repolarization
- U wave: late repolarization
- QT interval: total ventricular depolarization and repolarization
- Measurement techniques and normal values

### Chapter 4: Normal ECG Patterns and Variants
- Normal sinus rhythm characteristics
- Rate, rhythm, and axis interpretation
- Age and sex variations
- Normal variants (juvenile T waves, early repolarization)
- Axis calculation and clinical significance
- Chamber hypertrophy criteria (voltage criteria)

## Unit 3: Cardiac Arrhythmias

### Chapter 9: Supraventricular Arrhythmias
- Premature atrial complexes (PACs)
- Atrial fibrillation: irregularly irregular rhythm
- Atrial flutter: sawtooth pattern
- Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT): narrow complex
- Wolff-Parkinson-White syndrome: delta wave, short PR
- Atrioventricular nodal reentrant tachycardia (AVNRT)

### Chapter 10: Ventricular Arrhythmias
- Premature ventricular complexes (PVCs)
- Monomorphic ventricular tachycardia
- Polymorphic ventricular tachycardia (torsades de pointes)
- Ventricular fibrillation: chaotic rhythm
- Idioventricular rhythm and accelerated idioventricular rhythm

### Chapter 11: Conduction Block and Bradyarrhythmias
- First-degree AV block: prolonged PR
- Second-degree AV block: Mobitz I (Wenckebach) and II
- Third-degree AV block: complete dissociation
- Right bundle branch block: rsR' in V1, wide S in V6
- Left bundle branch block: wide R in V6, wide S in V1
- Left anterior/posterior fascicular blocks

### Chapter 12: Pacemakers and Device Therapy
- Single chamber vs dual chamber pacing
- ECG recognition of paced rhythms
- Failure to pace, failure to sense, failure to capture
- Biventricular pacing (CRT) optimization
- Implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICD) shocks

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this comprehensive ECG learning content, medical students will be able to:

1. **Interpret Basic ECG Tracings**: Identify normal waveforms, intervals, and rhythms
2. **Diagnose Cardiac Conditions**: Recognize ischemic, arrhythmic, and structural heart diseases
3. **Apply Clinical Correlations**: Integrate ECG findings with clinical presentations
4. **Use Advanced Tools**: Incorporate AI and digital technologies in ECG analysis
5. **Practice Evidence-Based Medicine**: Apply guidelines in ECG-guided clinical decision-making
6. **Communicate Effectively**: Document and report ECG findings appropriately
7. **Pursue Lifelong Learning**: Stay updated with evolving ECG technology and applications
