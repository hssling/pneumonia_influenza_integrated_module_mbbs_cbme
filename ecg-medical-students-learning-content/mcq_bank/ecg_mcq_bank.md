# ECG MCQ Bank

## Topic: Chapter 1 - Introduction to Electrocardiography

### MCQ 1.1 (Knowledge Level)
**Question:** Who is considered the father of modern electrocardiography?
**Options:**
A) Augustus Waller
B) Gabriel Lippmann
C) Willem Einthoven
D) Carlo Matteucci

**Correct Answer:** C) Willem Einthoven

**Explanation:** Willem Einthoven developed the string galvanometer in 1901-1903, enabling stable ECG recordings, and received the Nobel Prize in 1924 for his contributions to electrocardiography.

**Difficulty:** Easy
**CBME Mapping:** K - Basic knowledge
**Competency:** ECG fundamentals

### MCQ 1.2 (Skills Level)
**Question:** A 65-year-old male patient complains of shortness of breath. His ECG shows regular rhythm at 72 bpm with normal P-QRS-T morphology. What is the most appropriate interpretation?
**Options:**
A) Atrial fibrillation requiring anticoagulation
B) Normal sinus rhythm - further evaluation needed
C) Complete heart block needing pacemaker
D) Ventricular tachycardia requiring cardioversion

**Correct Answer:** B) Normal sinus rhythm - further evaluation needed

**Explanation:** Normal ECG parameters do not rule out cardiac pathology. Clinical correlation with symptoms, physical examination, and additional tests (echo, stress testing) is essential.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** S - Clinical interpretation skills
**Competency:** ECG-clinical correlation

### MCQ 1.4 (Integration Level)
**Question:** Which cardiac structure is primarily responsible for automaticity and serves as the pacemaker of the heart?
**Options:**
A) Atrioventricular node
B) Bundle of His
C) Sinoatrial node
D) Purkinje fibers

**Correct Answer:** C) Sinoatrial node

**Explanation:** The SA node generates impulses at 60-100 bpm due to slow diastolic depolarization (phase 4). It is located in the right atrial wall and has the highest automaticity among cardiac pacemakers.

**Difficulty:** Easy
**CBME Mapping:** I - Integration with anatomy
**Competency:** Conduction system knowledge

## Topic: Chapter 2 - ECG Recording Technique and Lead Systems

### MCQ 2.1 (Knowledge Level)
**Question:** Which precordial lead is positioned in the 5th intercostal space at the mid-clavicular line and primarily views the anterior left ventricle?
**Options:**
A) V1
B) V2
C) V4
D) V6

**Correct Answer:** C) V4

**Explanation:** V4 is placed in the 5th ICS mid-clavicular line, viewing anterior septal and apical regions. V1-V2 view septum, V5-V6 view lateral wall.

### MCQ 2.2 (Skills Level - Clinical Scenario)
**Question:** During ECG recording, you notice baseline wander artifact. The most appropriate first step is:
**Options:**
A) Increase paper speed to 50 mm/s
B) Ask patient to hold breath during recording
C) Adjust filter settings for muscle tremor
D) Replace faulty electrode

**Correct Answer:** B) Ask patient to hold breath during recording

**Explanation:** Baseline wander is typically caused by respiration. Having the patient hold breath in expiration eliminates this artifact without changing technical settings.

### MCQ 2.3 (Integration Level)
**Question:** In the standard 12-lead ECG, which lead is electrically neutral and provides perspective on the heart's mean electrical axis?
**Options:**
A) Lead I
B) Lead aVF
C) Wilson Central Terminal (WCT)
D) Lead V2

**Correct Answer:** C) Wilson Central Terminal (WCT)

**Explanation:** The WCT serves as the central reference point for unipolar leads (V1-V6, aVR, aVL, aVF). The mean electrical axis is calculated using leads I and aVF.

**Difficulty:** Advanced
**CBME Mapping:** I - Electrical principles integration
**Competency:** Lead system understanding

## Topic: Chapter 3 - ECG Waves, Segments, and Intervals

### MCQ 3.1 (Knowledge Level)
**Question:** The PR interval represents the time from:
**Options:**
A) Atrial depolarization to ventricular depolarization
B) Ventricular depolarization to repolarization
C) End of T wave to next P wave
D) QRS onset to T wave peak

**Correct Answer:** A) Atrial depolarization to ventricular depolarization

**Explanation:** PR interval measures conduction from SA node through AV node and His-Purkinje system. Normal range is 0.12-0.20 seconds.

**Difficulty:** Easy
**CBME Mapping:** K - Waveform identification
**Competency:** ECG measurements

---

### MCQ 3.2 (Clinical Skills)
**Question:** A patient's ECG shows QT interval of 0.52 seconds at heart rate of 60 bpm. The corrected QT interval (QTc) is calculated using which formula?
**Options:**
A) QTc = QT + 0.154(1-RR)
B) QTc = QT x √(RR interval)
C) QTc = QT / √RR
D) QTc = QT - (60/HR - 1) x 0.02

**Correct Answer:** C) QTc = QT / √RR

**Explanation:** Bazett's formula (QTc = QT / √RR) corrects QT interval for heart rate. QT shortens with faster rates, lengthens with slower rates.

**Difficulty:** Advanced
**CBME Mapping:** S - Interval calculation skills
**Competency:** ECG measurements and calculations

---

### MCQ 3.3 (Integration Level - Clinical Scenario)
**Question:** A patient taking amiodarone has QT interval of 0.50 seconds at heart rate 70 bpm. What action is most appropriate?
**Options:**
A) No action needed - within normal range
B) Calculate QTc using Bazett's formula
C) Immediate drug discontinuation
D) Start magnesium supplementation

**Correct Answer:** B) Calculate QTc using Bazett's formula

**Explanation:** Raw QT interval assessment without rate correction is inadequate. QTc evaluation is essential for drugs affecting repolarization.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** I - Clinical application
**Competency:** Drug effect monitoring

## Clinical Case-Based MCQ (Chapter 4 - Normal ECG Patterns)

### Case Scenario:
A 25-year-old athletic male presents for routine examination. ECG shows heart rate 50 bpm, normal axis, early repolarization pattern with J-point elevation and tall T waves in precordial leads. No symptoms reported.

**Question:** What is the most likely interpretation of this ECG finding?
**Options:**
A) Acute myocardial infarction
B) Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
C) Normal variant in young athletic individuals
D) Early stages of pericarditis

**Correct Answer:** C) Normal variant in young athletic individuals

**Explanation:** Early repolarization pattern with J-point elevation >1mm and tall symmetrical T waves is common in healthy young adults, especially athletes. It does not require intervention unless associated with symptoms.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** I - Pattern recognition and clinical correlation
**Competency:** Normal variants distinction

## Topic: Chapter 5 - Coronary Artery Disease and Myocardial Ischemia

### MCQ 5.1 (Knowledge Level)
**Question:** Which ECG change is most specific for myocardial ischemia?
**Options:**
A) ST segment depression
B) T wave inversion
C) ST segment elevation
D) Pathological Q waves

**Correct Answer:** A) ST segment depression

**Explanation:** Horizontal or downsloping ST depression ≥1mm in multiple leads is highly specific for subendocardial ischemia, though not sensitive.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** K - Ischemia patterns
**Competency:** ECG-pathology correlation

---

### MCQ 5.2 (Clinical Integration)
**Question:** A 55-year-old male presents with exertional chest pain. ECG shows 2mm ST elevation in leads V1-V4 with reciprocal changes. What coronary artery is most likely involved?
**Options:**
A) Left anterior descending artery
B) Right coronary artery
C) Left circumflex artery
D) Left main coronary artery

**Correct Answer:** A) Left anterior descending artery

**Explanation:** Anterior wall ST elevation (V1-V4) typically indicates LAD occlusion, especially with inferior reciprocal changes. LAD supplies anterior septum and anterior wall.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** I - Anatomical correlation
**Competency:** ECG-guided diagnosis

## Topic: Chapter 6 - Myocardial Infarction and ECG Evolution

### MCQ 6.1 (Skills Level - STEMI Recognition)
**Question:** Which finding is NOT required for STEMI diagnosis?
**Options:**
A) New ST elevation ≥1mm in limb leads
B) ST elevation ≥2mm in precordial leads
C) Symptoms consistent with myocardial ischemia
D) ST depression in reciprocal leads

**Correct Answer:** D) ST depression in reciprocal leads

**Explanation:** Reciprocal changes support diagnosis but are not required. STEMI requires new ST elevation + ischemic symptoms or new LBBB.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**CBME Mapping:** S - Diagnostic criteria application
**Competency:** Acute coronary syndrome diagnosis

---

### MCQ 6.2 (Integration Level)
**Question:** In acute inferior wall MI, which complication is most likely to cause heart block?
**Options:**
A) Right ventricular infarction
B) Left ventricular failure
C) Atrial fibrillation
D) Ventricular septal defect

**Correct Answer:** A) Right ventricular infarction

**Explanation:** Inferior wall MI often involves RCA supplying both inferior wall and RV. AV nodal artery may be affected causing conduction disturbances.

**Difficulty:** Advanced
**CBME Mapping:** I - Complication prediction
**Competency:** MI management
