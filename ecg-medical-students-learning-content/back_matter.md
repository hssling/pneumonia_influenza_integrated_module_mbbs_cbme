## Appendices

### **Appendix A: Normal ECG Parameters Reference**

#### **ECG Intervals and Measurements**

| **Parameter** | **Normal Range** | **Significance** | **Clinical Significance of Abnormalities** |
|---------------|------------------|------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **PR Interval** | 0.12-0.20 sec | Atrioventricular conduction time | Prolonged in AV block, shortened in WPW |
| **QRS Duration** | <0.12 sec | Ventricular depolarization | Widened in bundle branch blocks |
| **QT Interval** | 0.35-0.45 sec | Ventricular repolarization | Prolonged (drug toxicity), shortened (electrolyte disorders) |
| **RR Interval** | Variable | Heart rate (60-100 bpm) | Irregular in arrhythmias |
| **P Wave** | <0.12 sec, <2.5 mm | Atrial depolarization | Tall in atrial hypertrophy |
| **QRS Voltage** | Variable | Cardiac mass | Low in pericardial effusion |
| **ST Segment** | Isoelectric | Recovery period | Elevated/depressed in ischemia |
| **T Wave** | Variable | Ventricular repolarization | Inverted in ischemia, tall in hyperkalemia |

#### **ECG Axis Parameters**

| **Axis Degree** | **Interpretation** | **Clinical Correlation** |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| **0° to +90°** | Normal axis | Standard cardiac position |
| **+90° to +180°** | Right axis deviation | Pulmonary hypertension, RVH |
| **-30° to 0°** | Left axis deviation | LVH, LBBB |
| **-30° to -90°** | Extreme axis deviation | Ventricular tachycardia, inferior MI |

### **Appendix B: ECG Leads and Their Views**

#### **Standard Limb Leads**
- **Lead I:** Right arm (-) to left arm (+); views lateral wall
- **Lead II:** Right arm (-) to left leg (+); views inferior wall
- **Lead III:** Left arm (-) to left leg (+); views inferior wall

#### **Augmented Limb Leads**
- **aVR:** Views right atrium and cavity; normally all complexes negative
- **aVL:** Views lateral wall, especially atria and septum
- **aVF:** Views inferior wall and right ventricle

#### **Precordial Leads (V1-V6)**
- **V1:** 4th ICS right sternal border; septal view
- **V2:** 4th ICS left sternal border; septal view
- **V3:** Between V2-V4; anterior septal view
- **V4:** 5th ICS mid-clavicular line; anterior wall
- **V5:** Anterior axillary line, same level as V4; lateral wall
- **V6:** Mid-axillary line, same level; lateral wall

### **Appendix C: Common ECG Artifacts**

#### **Technical Issues**
- **Baseline drift:** Patient movement, loose electrodes
- **Muscle tremor:** Patient shivering, anxiety
- **60-Hz interference:** Electrical interference from lighting
- **Saturated signal:** Amplitude too high for recording

#### **Patient-Related**
- **Electrode contact:** Poor skin preparation, dry skin
- **Patient movement:** Deep breathing, repositioning
- **Pacing spike:** If patient has pacemaker

### **Appendix D: Medication Effects on ECG**

#### **Drugs Prolonging QT Interval**
- Antiarrhythmics (quinidine, sotalol)
- Antibiotics (erythromycin, clarithromycin)
- Antipsychotics (haloperidol, risperidone)
- Antidepressants (SSRIs, TCAs)

#### **Drugs Shortening QT Interval**
- Digoxin
- Catecholamines
- Some antiarrhythmics (lidocaine)

#### **Drugs Affecting Conduction**
- Beta-blockers: Bradycardia, AV block
- Calcium channel blockers: Bradycardia, AV block
- Antiarrhythmics: QRS widening, QT prolongation
