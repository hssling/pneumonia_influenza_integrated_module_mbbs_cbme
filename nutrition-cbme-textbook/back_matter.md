### **Appendix B: National Nutrition Programs in India**

#### **POSHAN Abhiyan (Prime Minister's Overarching Scheme for Holistic Nutrition)**
- **Launch:** 2018, extended to 2026
- **Coverage:** 112 districts across 8 states
- **Objectives:** Improve nutritional outcomes, reduce stunting, undernutrition
- **Components:** Convergence of ICDS, NHM, PD, DWCD
- **Focus Areas:** First 1000 days, adolescent girls, women of reproductive age

#### **National Iron Plus Initiative (NIPI)**
- **Weekly Iron Folic Acid Supplementation (WIFS):** 10-19 year adolescents
- **Pregnancy Iron Folic Acid (IFA) Program:** Daily supplementation through ANC
- **Anemia Mukt Bharat:** School children anemia prevention
- **Deworming:** 2x yearly with IFA in high-risk areas

#### **Vitamin A Supplementation Program**
- **Bi-annual dosing:** Children 9-59 months
- **Coverage:** >80% in most states
- **Dosage:** 50,000 IU for infants, 100,000 IU for older children
- **Monitoring:** District level coverage tracking

#### **Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS)**
- **Coverage:** 95 million children in 9.3 lakh schools
- **Nutritional standards:** 450-700 kcal, 12-20g protein
- **Micronutrients:** Iron, vitamin A, folic acid fortification
- **Objectives:** Improve enrollment, reduce classroom hunger

### **Appendix C: Cultural and Religious Nutritional Considerations**

#### **Hindu Dietary Guidelines**
- **Satvik food:** Pure, natural, non-violent preparation
- **Vegetarian preferences:** Most Hindis avoid meat, some fish/eggs
- **Festive foods:** Prasad offerings, fasting periods
- **Regional variations:** North vs South Indian preferences

#### **Islamic (Halal) Nutrition**
- **Permitted foods:** All except pork, alcoholic beverages
- **Preparation:** Meat slaughtered according to Islamic method
- **Ramadan fasting:** Modified meal timing considerations
- **Halal certification:** Processed food compliance

#### **Sikh Dietary Practices**
- **Amritdhari preferences:** No meat, fish, eggs (Khalsa commitment)
- **Langar tradition:** Community kitchen promoting equality
- **Jhatka method:** Alternative meat preparation if consumed

#### **Christian Dietary Variations**
- **No specific restrictions:** Most follow general nutrition guidelines
- **Lenten fasting:** Modified meal patterns during Lent
- **Regional influences:** North-east tribal vs South Indian practices

#### **Cultural Competency in Nutrition Counseling**
- **Language barriers:** Use of local languages, family members
- **Belief systems:** Respect for religious dietary modifications
- **Festive seasons:** Adjustment of advice for cultural celebrations
- **Gender dynamics:** Male/female preferences for certain foods

### **Appendix E: Anthropometric Reference Standards**

#### **WHO Growth Standards for Children**

**Weight-for-Age (Underweight):**
- **Severe:** < -3 SD below median
- **Moderate:** -3 to -2 SD below median
- **Mild:** -2 to -1 SD below median

**Height-for-Age (Stunting):**
- **Severe:** < -3 SD below median
- **Moderate:** -3 to -2 SD below median

**Weight-for-Height (Wasting):**
- **Severe:** < -3 SD below median
- **Moderate:** -3 to -2 SD below median

#### **Body Mass Index (BMI) Classifications**

| **BMI (kg/m²)** | **Adult Classification** | **Child Risk** |
|----------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| <18.5 | Underweight | Underweight |
| 18.5-24.9 | Normal weight | Healthy weight |
| 25.0-29.9 | Overweight | At risk |
| 30.0-34.9 | Obese Class I | Obese |
| 35.0-39.9 | Obese Class II | Severely obese |
| ≥40.0 | Obese Class III | Morbidly obese |

### **Appendix F: Drug-Nutrient Interactions**

#### **Common Medications Affecting Nutrition**

| **Medication Class** | **Nutritional Impact** | **Management** |
|----------------------|-----------------------|--------------|
| **Proton Pump Inhibitors** | Vitamin B12, iron, calcium deficiency | Monitor levels, consider supplementation |
| **Metformin** | Vitamin B12 deficiency, GI upset | Periodic B12 monitoring, gradual dose titration |
| **Diuretics** | Potassium, magnesium depletion | Monitor electrolytes, increase fruit/vegetable intake |
| **Statins** | Potential coenzyme Q10 depletion | Consider supplementation if muscle symptoms |
| **Antacids** | Calcium, iron, folate absorption | Separate medication timing from meals |

#### **Nutrient-Medication Conflicts**

| **Nutrient/Medication** | **Interaction** | **Clinical Implication** |
|------------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| **Calcium supplements + Thyroxine** | Reduced thyroid absorption | Separate administration by 4 hours |
| **Iron + Quinolones/Tetracyclines** | Reduced antibiotic absorption | Administer drugs 1-2 hours before/after iron |
| **Vitamin K + Warfarin** | Altered anticoagulation | Consistent vitamin K intake, regular INR monitoring |
| **Potassium + ACE inhibitors** | Hyperkalemia risk | Monitor serum potassium levels |
