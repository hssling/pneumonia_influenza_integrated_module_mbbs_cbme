# Appendix A: Comprehensive Glossary of Medical Statistics Terms

## A

**Absolute Risk**: The probability of an event occurring in a specific time period
**Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR)**: Difference in event rates between treatment and control groups
**Adaptive Design**: Clinical trial design that allows modifications based on accumulating data
**Alpha (α)**: Probability of Type I error (rejecting true null hypothesis)
**Analysis of Covariance (ANCOVA)**: Combines ANOVA with regression to control for covariates
**Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)**: Statistical test comparing means across multiple groups
**Area Under Curve (AUC)**: Measure of diagnostic test accuracy (ROC curve)
**Attributable Risk**: Risk difference due to specific exposure

## B

**Bayesian Statistics**: Statistical approach incorporating prior knowledge with current data
**Beta (β)**: Probability of Type II error (failing to reject false null hypothesis)
**Bias**: Systematic error leading to incorrect results
**Bimodal Distribution**: Distribution with two peaks
**Binomial Distribution**: Discrete probability distribution for binary outcomes
**Blinding**: Masking treatment assignment to reduce bias
**Bonferroni Correction**: Multiple testing adjustment dividing alpha by number of tests
**Bootstrap**: Resampling method for statistical inference
**Box Plot**: Graphical display of data distribution using quartiles

## C

**Case-Control Study**: Retrospective study comparing cases with disease to controls without disease
**Categorical Data**: Data divided into distinct groups or categories
**Censoring**: Incomplete observation of survival time
**Central Limit Theorem**: Sample means become normally distributed as sample size increases
**Chi-Square Test**: Statistical test for categorical data associations
**Clinical Significance**: Meaningful impact on patient outcomes
**Cluster Sampling**: Sampling groups rather than individuals
**Cohort Study**: Prospective study following groups over time
**Confidence Interval**: Range of values likely containing true population parameter
**Confounding**: Mixing of effects between exposure, outcome, and third factor
**Continuous Data**: Data that can take any value within a range
**Correlation Coefficient**: Measure of linear relationship strength (-1 to +1)
**Cox Proportional Hazards Model**: Regression model for survival data
**Crossover Design**: Each participant receives multiple treatments
**Cumulative Incidence**: Probability of event occurring by specific time

## D

**Data Dredging**: Searching data for significant findings without pre-specified hypotheses
**Degrees of Freedom**: Number of independent values in statistical calculation
**Diagnostic Odds Ratio**: Measure of diagnostic test performance
**Discrete Data**: Data that can only take whole number values
**Dose-Response Relationship**: Association between exposure level and outcome
**Dummy Variable**: Binary variable representing categorical data in regression

## E

**Ecological Fallacy**: Incorrect assumption about individuals based on group-level data
**Effect Size**: Standardized measure of treatment effect magnitude
**Efficacy**: Treatment benefit under ideal conditions
**Effectiveness**: Treatment benefit in real-world practice
**Epidemiology**: Study of disease patterns in populations
**Equipoise**: Genuine uncertainty about treatment benefits
**Evidence-Based Medicine**: Using best available evidence for clinical decisions
**External Validity**: Generalizability of results to other populations

## F

**False Discovery Rate (FDR)**: Expected proportion of false positives among significant results
**False Negative**: Test result indicating no disease when disease present
**False Positive**: Test result indicating disease when no disease present
**Fixed Effects Model**: Assumes all studies estimate same true effect
**Forest Plot**: Graphical display of meta-analysis results
**Frequentist Statistics**: Traditional statistical approach based on long-run frequency
**Funnel Plot**: Plot to assess publication bias in meta-analysis

## G

**Generalizability**: Ability to apply results to broader populations
**Gold Standard**: Best available test for diagnosing a condition
**GRADE System**: Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation

## H

**Hazard Ratio (HR)**: Risk ratio in survival analysis
**Heterogeneity**: Variation between study results in meta-analysis
**Hierarchical Model**: Statistical model accounting for clustering
**Hypothesis**: Testable statement about population parameters
**Homoscedasticity**: Constant variance across predictor values

## I

**Incidence**: Rate of new disease cases in population
**Intention-to-Treat (ITT)**: Analyze all randomized patients in assigned groups
**Interaction**: Effect of one variable depends on another variable
**Internal Validity**: Freedom from bias within study
**Interquartile Range (IQR)**: Range containing middle 50% of data
**Interval Data**: Numeric data with equal intervals but no true zero

## J

**Joint Model**: Combines longitudinal and survival data analysis

## K

**Kaplan-Meier Curve**: Non-parametric estimate of survival function
**Kendall's Tau**: Non-parametric correlation coefficient
**Kruskal-Wallis Test**: Non-parametric alternative to ANOVA

## L

**Latent Variable**: Unobserved variable inferred from observed variables
**Likelihood Ratio**: Ratio of probabilities used in Bayesian analysis
**Linear Regression**: Models linear relationship between variables
**Logistic Regression**: Models binary outcomes using log-odds
**Longitudinal Study**: Repeated measurements over time
**Loss to Follow-Up**: Participants who cannot be contacted for study completion

## M

**Machine Learning**: Algorithms that learn patterns from data
**Mann-Whitney U Test**: Non-parametric test for comparing two groups
**Marginal Effect**: Effect of variable averaging over other variables
**Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)**: Bayesian computational method
**Matching**: Pairing similar individuals in case-control studies
**McNemar's Test**: Statistical test for paired categorical data
**Median**: Middle value in ordered dataset
**Meta-Analysis**: Statistical combination of multiple studies
**Meta-Regression**: Regression of effect sizes on study characteristics
**Missing Data**: Incomplete observations in dataset
**Mode**: Most frequent value in dataset
**Multicollinearity**: High correlation between predictor variables
**Multiple Imputation**: Method for handling missing data
**Multiple Testing**: Conducting multiple statistical tests
**Multivariate Analysis**: Analysis involving multiple outcome variables

## N

**Negative Predictive Value (NPV)**: Probability of no disease given negative test
**Network Meta-Analysis**: Compare multiple treatments using indirect evidence
**Nominal Data**: Categorical data without inherent order
**Non-Inferiority Trial**: Shows new treatment not worse than standard by specified margin
**Non-Parametric Tests**: Distribution-free statistical tests
**Normal Distribution**: Bell-shaped symmetric probability distribution
**Null Hypothesis**: Statement of no effect or no difference
**Number Needed to Treat (NNT)**: Number of patients to treat to prevent one event

## O

**Observational Study**: Study without researcher intervention
**Odds**: Ratio of probability of event to probability of no event
**Odds Ratio (OR)**: Ratio of odds in exposed to unexposed groups
**One-Tailed Test**: Hypothesis test in one direction
**Ordinal Data**: Categorical data with meaningful order
**Outlier**: Data point substantially different from others
**Overfitting**: Model fits training data too well but generalizes poorly

## P

**p-Value**: Probability of observing data if null hypothesis true
**Parallel Design**: Different groups receive different treatments
**Parametric Tests**: Statistical tests assuming specific data distributions
**Per-Protocol Analysis**: Analyze only participants who completed treatment as planned
**Phase I Trial**: First human testing, focus on safety and dosage
**Phase II Trial**: Test efficacy and side effects in target population
**Phase III Trial**: Large-scale efficacy and safety testing
**Phase IV Trial**: Post-marketing surveillance
**Point Estimate**: Single value estimate of population parameter
**Positive Predictive Value (PPV)**: Probability of disease given positive test
**Posterior Distribution**: Updated probability distribution after seeing data
**Power**: Probability of detecting true effect
**Precision**: Closeness of repeated measurements
**Prevalence**: Proportion of population with disease at specific time
**Prior Distribution**: Initial beliefs about parameters before seeing data
**Propensity Score**: Probability of treatment assignment given covariates
**Proportional Hazards**: Assumption that hazard ratio constant over time
**Prospective Study**: Study planned before data collection
**Publication Bias**: Studies with significant results more likely published

## Q

**Q-Statistic**: Measure of heterogeneity in meta-analysis
**Quality-Adjusted Life Year (QALY)**: Measure combining quantity and quality of life
**Quartile**: Values dividing data into four equal parts
**Quasi-Experimental Design**: Study design without full randomization

## R

**Random Effects Model**: Accounts for between-study variation in meta-analysis
**Randomization**: Random allocation to treatment groups
**Range**: Difference between maximum and minimum values
**Ratio Data**: Numeric data with true zero point
**Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) Curve**: Plot of sensitivity vs 1-specificity
**Regression Coefficient**: Change in outcome per unit change in predictor
**Relative Risk (RR)**: Ratio of risks in exposed to unexposed groups
**Reliability**: Consistency of measurements
**Repeated Measures**: Multiple measurements on same subjects
**Retrospective Study**: Study using historical data
**Risk Difference**: Absolute difference in risk between groups
**Robust Statistics**: Methods less sensitive to outliers

## S

**Sample**: Subset of population selected for study
**Sampling Distribution**: Distribution of statistic across repeated samples
**Sampling Error**: Difference between sample and population due to random variation
**Selection Bias**: Non-random selection leading to biased results
**Sensitivity**: Probability of positive test given disease present
**Sensitivity Analysis**: Assess impact of varying assumptions
**Specificity**: Probability of negative test given no disease
**Standard Deviation**: Measure of data dispersion around mean
**Standard Error**: Standard deviation of sampling distribution
**Standardized Mean Difference**: Effect size for continuous outcomes
**Statistical Significance**: Unlikely to occur by chance
**Stratified Analysis**: Analysis within subgroups
**Study Design**: Framework for conducting research
**Survival Analysis**: Analysis of time-to-event data
**Systematic Review**: Comprehensive review of literature on specific topic

## T

**t-Test**: Statistical test comparing means
**Test Statistic**: Value calculated from data to test hypothesis
**Time-to-Event Data**: Data measuring time until event occurs
**Treatment Effect**: Impact of intervention on outcomes
**Trim and Fill**: Method to assess and adjust for publication bias
**True Negative**: Correctly identified absence of disease
**True Positive**: Correctly identified presence of disease
**Two-Tailed Test**: Hypothesis test in either direction
**Type I Error**: Rejecting true null hypothesis
**Type II Error**: Failing to reject false null hypothesis

## U

**Unsupervised Learning**: Machine learning without labeled outcomes
**Utility**: Measure of preference or value in decision analysis

## V

**Validity**: Accuracy of measurement or study design
**Variable**: Characteristic that can vary among individuals
**Variance**: Measure of data dispersion
**Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)**: Measure of multicollinearity

## W

**Washout Period**: Time between treatments in crossover design
**Wilcoxon Test**: Non-parametric test for paired data
**Within-Subject Design**: Same participants in multiple conditions

## Z

**Z-Score**: Number of standard deviations from mean
**Zero-Inflated Model**: Model for data with excess zeros
