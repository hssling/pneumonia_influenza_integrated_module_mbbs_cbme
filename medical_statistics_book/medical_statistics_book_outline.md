# Medical Statistics Book - Comprehensive Outline

## Book Overview
**Title:** Essential Medical Statistics: A Practical Guide for Healthcare Professionals
**Target Audience:** Medical students, healthcare professionals, researchers
**Style:** Easy to understand, clear presentation, practical examples, exercises with answers
**Coverage:** Based on Betty Kirkwood's "Essential Medical Statistics" with modern additions

## Chapter Structure

### Part I: Foundations of Medical Statistics
1. **Introduction to Medical Statistics**
   - What is medical statistics?
   - Role in evidence-based medicine
   - Types of data in healthcare
   - Basic concepts: population vs sample, variables, measurement scales

2. **Describing Data**
   - Tables and graphs
   - Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
   - Measures of dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation)
   - Frequency distributions and percentiles

3. **Probability and Probability Distributions**
   - Basic probability concepts
   - Binomial distribution
   - Normal distribution
   - Poisson distribution
   - Applications in medical contexts

### Part II: Statistical Inference
4. **Sampling and Sampling Distributions**
   - Random sampling methods
   - Sampling error and bias
   - Central Limit Theorem
   - Standard error

5. **Confidence Intervals**
   - Concept of confidence intervals
   - CI for means and proportions
   - Interpretation in medical context
   - Width and precision

6. **Hypothesis Testing**
   - Null and alternative hypotheses
   - Type I and Type II errors
   - p-values and significance levels
   - One-tailed vs two-tailed tests

### Part III: Statistical Tests
7. **Tests for Categorical Data**
   - Chi-square test for association
   - Chi-square test for trend
   - Fisher's exact test
   - McNemar's test for paired data

8. **Tests for Continuous Data**
   - t-tests (one-sample, two-sample, paired)
   - Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
   - Non-parametric alternatives (Mann-Whitney, Wilcoxon)

9. **Correlation and Regression**
   - Correlation coefficients (Pearson, Spearman)
   - Simple linear regression
   - Multiple regression
   - Logistic regression basics

### Part IV: Clinical Study Designs
10. **Epidemiological Study Designs**
    - Cross-sectional studies
    - Case-control studies
    - Cohort studies
    - Clinical trials
    - Bias and confounding

11. **Measures of Disease Frequency**
    - Prevalence and incidence
    - Risk ratios and odds ratios
    - Attributable risk
    - Population attributable risk

12. **Diagnostic and Screening Tests**
    - Sensitivity and specificity
    - Predictive values
    - ROC curves
    - Likelihood ratios

### Part V: Survival Analysis and Advanced Topics
13. **Survival Analysis**
    - Kaplan-Meier curves
    - Log-rank test
    - Cox proportional hazards model
    - Censored data

14. **Sample Size Calculations**
    - Power and sample size for means
    - Power and sample size for proportions
    - Clinical trial sample size
    - Ethical considerations

15. **Meta-Analysis**
    - Systematic reviews
    - Forest plots
    - Heterogeneity assessment
    - Publication bias

### Part VI: Practical Applications
16. **Statistical Software in Medicine**
    - Introduction to SPSS, R, Stata
    - Data entry and management
    - Basic statistical functions
    - Interpreting output

17. **Critical Appraisal of Medical Literature**
    - Reading scientific papers
    - Assessing study quality
    - Common statistical pitfalls
    - Evidence-based medicine

18. **Medical Statistics in Practice**
    - Clinical decision making
    - Health policy and planning
    - Quality improvement
    - Real-world examples

## Book Features
- **Examples:** Real medical scenarios for each concept
- **Exercises:** Progressive difficulty with detailed solutions
- **Key Points:** Summary boxes for quick reference
- **Medical Context:** Clinical relevance for each statistical concept
- **Visual Aids:** Clear diagrams, flowcharts, and graphs
- **Self-Assessment:** Chapter quizzes with answers
- **Glossary:** Key terms and definitions
- **Appendices:** Statistical tables, formulas, software guides

- Understand fundamental statistical concepts
- Apply appropriate statistical tests to medical data
- Interpret results of statistical analyses
- Critically evaluate medical research
- Use statistics for clinical decision making
- Calculate sample sizes for research studies

- Multiple choice questions
- Data interpretation problems
