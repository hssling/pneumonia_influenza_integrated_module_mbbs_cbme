## Competency Framework Preface

In the era of competency-based medical education, anatomical knowledge is not merely about memorization of structures but about understanding their clinical relevance and application in patient care. This textbook represents a paradigm shift from traditional anatomy teaching to CBME-aligned learning that integrates structure, function, and clinical application from the very beginning.

### Why CBME-Aligned Anatomy?

The National Medical Council (NMC) has mandated CBME curriculum that emphasizes:
- **Integration**: Anatomy with preclinical and clinical sciences
- **Patient-Centered Learning**: Using clinical cases to drive anatomical understanding
- **Competency Development**: Clear learning outcomes for each anatomical system
- **Assessment Reform**: Continuous assessment focused on application rather than recall
- **Technology Integration**: Digital tools for enhanced learning

### Key CBME Principles Applied

- **Entrustable Professional Activities (EPAs)**: Anatomy competencies linked to clinical procedures
- **Milestones and Entrustment**: Progressive development of anatomical skills
- **Case-Based Learning**: Real patient scenarios drive anatomical exploration
- **Vertical and Horizontal Integration**: Anatomy connected across all years of medical training
- **Assessment for Learning**: Formative assessments to guide competency development

### Target Learning Outcomes

By the end of this curriculum, students should be able to:
1. Apply anatomical knowledge to clinical problem-solving
2. Demonstrate surface anatomy skills for clinical examinations
3. Interpret radiological images using anatomical knowledge
4. Explain anatomical basis for clinical signs and symptoms
5. Perform basic anatomical procedures safely and effectively

### Educational Philosophy

*"Anatomy is the foundation of medicine. When learned through CBME principles, it becomes the bridge between basic sciences and clinical practice."*

This textbook embraces active learning through:
- **Case-Based Scenarios**: Clinical problems drive anatomical learning
- **Imaging Integration**: Modern imaging techniques throughout chapters
- **Surgical Correlations**: Surgical anatomy and procedures explained
- **Pathological Connections**: Understanding disease processes anatomically
- **Functional Integration**: Linking structure with physiology and biochemistry

### How to Use This Book

**For Students:**
- Follow the CBME progression: Knowledge → Skills → Application → Integration
- Complete learning activities in each chapter
- Use digital resources for 3D visualization
- Engage with case studies and MCQs for self-assessment

**For Faculty:**
- Use as curriculum guide for CBME implementation
- Utilize assessment frameworks provided
- Integrate with clinical departments for early clinical exposure
- Monitor student competency progression

### Book Structure and CBME Integration

The book follows the systems approach while maintaining CBME principles:

**Foundation Phase** (Chapters 1-2): Core anatomical concepts and basic histology  
**Systems Integration** (Chapters 3-14): Anatomical systems with clinical correlations  
**Applied Anatomy** (Chapters 15-16): Living anatomy, radiology, and clinical applications

Each chapter includes:
- **Competency Statements**: Clear learning outcomes
- **Clinical Entrustment Scales**: Progressive skill development indicators
- **Integration Boxes**: Connections to other medical sciences
- **Skill Stations**: Practical learning activities
- **Assessment Rubrics**: Competency-based assessment tools

### CBME Curriculum Domains

1. **Domain 1: Anatomical Knowledge** - Structure, function, development
2. **Domain 2: Clinical Application** - Anatomical basis of clinical care
3. **Domain 3: Professionalism** - Ethical use of anatomical knowledge
4. **Domain 4: Communication** - Explaining anatomy to patients and peers
5. **Domain 5: Lifelong Learning** - Resources for continuous professional development

## Competency Achievement Indicators

### Knowledge Domain
- **Level 1**: Recognizes basic anatomical structures
- **Level 2**: Describes relationships between structures
- **Level 3**: Explains functional-clinical correlations
- **Level 4**: Applies anatomy in clinical decision-making
- **Level 5**: Teaches anatomical concepts effectively

### Skills Domain
- **Observation**: Identifies anatomical landmarks
- **Assistance**: Aids in anatomical procedures
- **Independent Practice**: Performs anatomical examinations
- **Teaching**: Guides peers in anatomical learning
- **Innovation**: Contributes to anatomical education advances

### Attitude Domain
- Respects anatomical donation and ethical practices
- Values patient dignity in teaching/learning
- Collaborates with clinical faculty
- Seeks continuous professional development
