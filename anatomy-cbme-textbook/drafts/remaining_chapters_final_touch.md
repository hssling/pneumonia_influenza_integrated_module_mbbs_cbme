# Chapter 6: Respiratory System

## Competency Statements
1. **KS/KP.1**: Describe respiratory tract structure and correlate with ventilation mechanics
2. **PC.3**: Perform pulmonary examination systematically
3. **COM.2**: Explain pulmonary pathology to patients

## Key CBME Case: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
- **60-year-old ICU patient** with bilateral infiltrates
- **Anatomical correlation**: Alveolar-capillary barrier disruption
- **Clinical competency**: Interpreting chest imaging and correlating with gas exchange abnormalities

## Major Respiratory Structures

### Upper Respiratory Tract
- **Nasal Cavity**: Turbinates maximize surface area, conchae increase turbulence
- **Pharynx**: Nasopharynx, oropharynx, laryngopharynx - shared airway and digestive tract
- **Larynx**: Framework of cartilages with vocal folds between thyroid and arytenoid cartilages
- **Trachea**: C-shaped cartilages, posterior membranous wall

### Lower Respiratory Tract
- **Main Bronchi**: Right wider, more vertical (aspirations tend to go right)
- **Bronchial Tree**: 23 divisions from trachea to alveoli
- **Bronchopulmonary Segments**: 10 right, 8-9 left lung segments
- **Respiratory Bronchioles**: Begin gas exchange
- **Alveoli**: 300 million units, type I/II pneumocytes

### Pleural Relations
- **Visceral Pleura**: Fused to lung surface
- **Parietal Pleura**: Lines thoracic wall
- **Pleural Space**: Potential space with capillary fluid film
- **Recesses**: Costodiaphragmatic, costomediastinal spaces where effusions collect

## Clinical Correlations

### Obstructive vs Restrictive Disease
- **Obstruction**: Airways narrowed - emphysema, asthma, bronchitis
- **Restriction**: Lung tissue stiff - fibrosis, effusion, pneumothorax
- **Diffusing Capacity**: Alveolar membrane permeability

### Bronchopneumonia Patterns
- **Lobar Pneumonia**: Streptococcus pneumoniae fills alveolar spaces
- **Bronchopneumonia**: Begins in bronchi, spreads to adjacent alveoli
- **Interstitial Pneumonia**: Viral, involves alveolar walls

## CBME Skills Assessment
- Trachea palpation in suprasternal notch
- Breath sound auscultation in systematic fashion
- Interpretation of respiratory patterns and accessory muscle use

# Chapter 7: Nervous System - Central and Peripheral

## Competency Statements
1. **PC.4**: Perform comprehensive neurological assessment
2. **KS/KP.2**: Localize neurological lesions anatomically
3. **PC.5**: Apply anatomical knowledge in neuroimaging interpretation

## Nervous System Organization

### Central Nervous System
**Brain**:
- **Forebrain**: Telencephalon (cerebral hemispheres), diencephalon (thalamus)
- **Midbrain**: Visual reflexes, cranial nerves 3-4
- **Hindbrain**: Cerebellum, pons, medulla (cardiorespiratory control)

**Spinal Cord**:
- **Cervical Enlargement**: Upper limb innervation (C5-T1)
- **Lumbar Enlargement**: Lower limb innervation (L2-S1)
- **Conus Medullaris**: Tapered tip at L1-2
- **Cauds Equina**: Nerve roots emerging from conus

### Peripheral Nervous System
**Somatic Nervous System**:
- 31 pairs spinal nerves, 12 pairs cranial nerves
- Sensory and motor innervation of body surface and muscles

**Autonomic Nervous System**:
- **Sympathetic**: Fight/flight response, thoracolumbar outflow
- **Parasympathetic**: Rest/digest, craniosacral outflow

## Clinical Localization

### Upper Motor Neuron Lesions
- Contralateral weakness below lesion
- Increased tone, hyperreflexia, Babinski sign
- Absence of muscle atrophy or fasciculations

### Lower Motor Neuron Lesions
- Ipsilateral weakness at level of lesion
- Decreased tone, hyporeflexia, fasciculations
- Muscle atrophy over time

### Sensory Loss Patterns
- **Dermatomes**: Spinal nerve distribution patterns
- **Brown-Sequard Syndrome**: Half of cord affected
- **Syringomyelia**: Dissociated sensory loss

### Cranial Nerve Assessment
- **Olfactory (I)**: Smell identification
- **Optic (II)**: Visual acuity, fields, fundoscopy
- **Oculomotor (III)**: Pupil reactivity, eye movements
- **Trochlear (IV)**: Superior oblique function
- **Trigeminal (V)**: Facial sensation, motor jaw strength
- **Abducens (VI)**: Lateral gaze
- **Facial (VII)**: Facial symmetry, taste
- **Auditory (VIII)**: Hearing, balance
- **Glossopharyngeal (IX)**: Swallow, gag reflex
- **Vagus (X)**: Voice quality, palatal elevation
- **Accessory (XI)**: Shoulder shrug, head turning
- **Hypoglossal (XII)**: Tongue movement

# Chapter 8: Digestive System

## Competency Statements
1. **PC.3**: Perform systematic abdominal examination
2. **PC.4**: Interpret gastrointestinal imaging
3. **COM.2**: Explain digestive disorders clearly

## Digestive Tract Anatomy

### Mouth and Pharynx
- **Tongue**: Extrinsic (styloglossus, hyoglossus, genioglossus) and intrinsic muscles
- **Teeth**: 32 permanent teeth - incisors (cutting), canines (tearing), premolars/molcas (grinding)
- **Salivary Glands**: Parotid, submandibular, sublingual secretions
- **Pharyngeal Muscles**: Elevators and constrictors coordinate swallowing

### Esophagus
- **Muscular Tube**: Striated upper ⅓, mixed middle, smooth lower
- **Sphincters**: Upper esophageal (UES), lower esophageal (LES)
- **Clinical**: Achalasia (LES failure), reflux esophagitis

### Stomach
- **Anatomy**: Cardia, fundus, body, antrum, pylorus
- **Mucosal Folds**: Rugae for accommodation
- **Glands**: Gastric pits with parietal (HCl) and chief (pepsinogen) cells
- **Clinical**: Helicobacter pylori gastritis, peptic ulcers

### Small Intestine
- **Duodenum**: Short, C-shaped, receives biliary and pancreatic ducts
- **Jejunum**: Longer, more vascular, primary nutrient absorption
- **Ileum**: Terminal portion, vitamin B12 absorption, Peyer's patches
- **Villi and Microvilli**: Massive surface area amplification (500x)

### Large Intestine
- **Cecum**: Sac-like structure, appendix attachment
- **Colon**: Ascending, transverse, descending, sigmoid portions
- **Rectum**: Straight portion, internal/external anal sphincters
- **Clinical**: Diverticulosis, ulcerative colitis, colorectal cancer

### Accessory Organs
**Liver**: Right lobe larger, porta hepatis (vascular hilum), Couinaud segments
**Gallbladder**: Thin-walled sac, fundus below liver edge
**Pancreas**: Head, body, tail; exocrine (ducts) and endocrine (islets) functions
**Spleen**: Immunologic and hematologic functions in left upper quadrant

# Chapter 9: Urinary System

## Competency Statements
1. **PC.3**: Perform renal and urinary tract assessment
2. **KS/KP.1**: Correlate kidney structure with urine formation
3. **COM.2**: Explain renal pathology to patients

## Kidney Structure and Function

### Gross Anatomy
- **Bean-shaped organs**, 10-12 cm long, 150-170g each
- **Hilum**: Renal vessels, nerves, lymphatics, ureter exit
- **Superior/Inferior Poles**: Marked by adrenal gland superiorly
- **Position**: Retroperitoneal in lumbar region (T12-L3)

### Microscopic Structure
**Renal Cortex**:
- Glomeruli: Filtration units (1 million per kidney)
- Proximal convoluted tubules: Active transport site
- Distal convoluted tubules: Aldosterone targets
- Collecting ducts: ADH targets

**Renal Medulla**:
- Pyramids with papillae
- Medullary rays fan upward
- **Pyelocalyceal system**: Minor calyces → major calyces → renal pelvis

### Nephrons
- **Cortical Nephrons**: 85%, short loops of Henle
- **Juxtamedullary Nephrons**: 15%, long loops for concentration
- **Filtration**: Glomerular capillaries → Bowman's capsule
- **Reabsorption**: Proximal tubule (70% filtrate), loop of Henle, collecting duct

## Ureters and Bladder
**Ureters**: 25-30cm long, peristaltic transport
- **Narrowings**: Pelviureteric junction, pelvic brim crossing, ureterovesical junction
**Bladder**: Capacity 500-600mL
- **Trigone**: Area between ureteric orifices and internal urethral orifice
- **Detrusor Muscle**: Smooth muscle for emptying
