## 15.1 Plain Radiography (X-rays)

### Chest Radiology: Systematic Interpretation

**Technical Factors**:
- **PA View**: Patient facing detector (AP for bedside films)
- **Lateral View**: Left side pressed against detector
- **Technical Quality**: Penetration (ribs visible through heart), rotation (spinous processes centered), inspiration (6 anterior ribs)

**Systematic Approach**:
- **Airway**: Tracheal position at midline, no deviation
- **Breathing**: Clear lung fields, costophrenic angles, no fluid
- **Cardiac**: Cardiothoracic ratio (<0.5), cardiac borders, mediastinal contours
- **Devices/Heart**: Abnormal shadows, foreign objects
- **Everything**: Bones, soft tissues, diaphragm

**Common Findings**:
- **Pneumothorax**: Absent pulmonary vascular markings, pleural line
- **Pleural Effusion**: Bigelow's sign (fluid tracking up lung), meniscus sign
- **Pneumonia**: Alveolar infiltrates, air bronchograms
- **Cardiac Failure**: Perihilar edema, Kerley's lines (B/C), pleural effusions

**Key Anatomical Correlations****Pineapple**:
- **Hilum**: Pulmonary arteries overlay bronchi (hilum rules)
- **Fissures**: Horizontal fissure at level 4-6 anterior ribs
- **Aorta**: Thoracic aortic arch silhouette
- **Diaphragm**: Right diaphragm higher (liver), gastric air bubble

---

### Abdominal Radiology

**Supine Abdominal Film**:
- **Gas Patterns**: Stomach air bubble, small bowel loops, large bowel haustra
- **Solid Organs**: Liver edge beneath diaphragm, kidneys outlined by fat
- **Calcifications**: Gallstones, renal stones, pancreatic calcifications
- **Pathology**: Intestinal obstruction (dilatation), pneumoperitoneum

**Erect Abdominal Film**:
- **Air-Fluid Levels**: Multiple in small bowel obstruction
- **Pneumoperitoneum**: Free intraperitoneal air under diaphragm
- **Diaphragm Monitoring**: Rippling, herniation detection

**Upright Chest X-ray**: For same patient
- **Subdiaphragmatic Air**: Move to abdomen if suspected

**Common Abdominal Pathologies**:
- **Acute Abdomen**: Free air, dilated loops, rigler's sign (gas on both sides of bowel)
- **Small Bowel Obstruction**: Ladder pattern of dilated loops centered around umbilicus
- **Large Bowel Obstruction**: Peripheral dilatation, haustral markings

**Urinary Tract X-rays**:
- **KUB (Kidneys-Ureters-Bladder)**: Renal shadows, ureteric calculi, bladder outline
- **Pitfalls**: Phleboliths (pelvic veins) mimic ureteric calculi

## 15.2 Computed Tomography (CT)

### Head CT Interpretation

**Brain Windows**:
- **Grey Scale**: Brain parenchyma, ventricles, CSF spaces
- **White Scale**: Bone windows for skull fractures
- **Vessels**: For aneurysms, hematomas (hyperdense)

**Systematic Reading**:
- **Scalp/Skin**: Epidural hematomas may show "swirl sign"
- **Bone**: Skull fractures, pneumocephalus
- **Cerebellum/Brainstem**: Posterior fossa lesions
- **Posterior Fossa Contents**: Cerebellum, pons, medulla
- **Middle Cranial Fossa**: Temporal lobes, pituitary
- **Anterior Cranial Fossa**: Frontal lobes, olfactory bulbs
- **Supratentorial Compartments**: White matter changes, ischemic strokes

**Hounsfield Units (HU)**:
- **Air**: -1000 HU (pneumothorax, pneumocephalus)
- **Fat**: -50 to -100 HU (orbital fat, mediastinal fat)
- **Water/Soft Tissue**: 0 HU (CSF), 40-60 HU (grey matter)
- **Blood**: 40-60 HU (acute hematoma >60 HU)
- **Bone**: 100-1000 HU (hyperdense structures)

### Contrast-enhanced CT

**Oral Contrast (PO)**:
- **Small Bowel**: Follow transit through jejunum/ileum
- **Large Bowel**: Rectal contrast for diverticulosis coli

**Intravenous Contrast (IV)**:
- **Arterial Phase**: Hepatic lesions, renal masses
- **Venous Phase**: Liver metastases, pulmonary emboli detection
- **Triple Phase**: Liver (arterial, portal venous, equilibrium)

---

### Abdominal CT Interpretation

**Vascular Structures**:
- **Aortic-Calming**: Intra-abdominal aorta, celiac, superior mesenteric, common iliac arteries
- **Venous System**: Inferior vena cava, portal/splenic/superior mesenteric veins
- **Hepatic**: Portal triad branches, hepatic veins to IVC

**Hollow Viscera**:
- **Stomach**: Hiatal hernia, gastric outlet obstruction, perigastric masses
- **Small Bowel**: Intestinal obstruction, mural thickening, mesenteric masses
- **Large Bowel**: Colonic diverticula, inflammatory bowel disease, colorectal tumors
- **Urinary Tract**: Hydronephrosis, renal calculi, bladder malignancy

**Solid Organs**:
- **Liver**: Segments I-VIII (Couinaud classification), foci of cirrhosis, masses
- **Spleen**: Splenomegaly, splenic infarction, traumatic laceration
- **Pancreas**: Head/uncinate process/body/tail, ductal dilatation
- **Adrenal**: Left adrenal crus, right adrenal contact with liver

---

### Chest CT Interpretation

**Mediastinal Compartments**:
- **Anterior**: Thymus, lymph nodes, vascular structures, tumors
- **Middle**: Heart, great vessels, trachea, bronchi, esophagus
- **Posterior**: Descending aorta, thoracic duct, sympathetic chains

**Lung Parenchyma**:
- **Bronchovascular Bundles**: Bronchi surrounded by two pulmonary arteries
- **Centrilobular Nodules**: Small airway inflammation, hypersensitivity pneumonitis
- **Ground Glass Opacity**: Interstitial inflammation, viral pneumonias
- **Honeycombing**: End-stage fibrosis, usual interstitial pneumonitis

**Lymph Node Stations** (IASLC)**:
- **Hilar Nodes**: 10R/10L, bronchopulmonary nodes
- **Mediastinal**: 4R/4L aortic-pulmonary nodes
- **Subcarinal**: 7 station
- **Carinal**: 2R/2L stations

## 15.3 Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

### Brain MRI Sequences

**T1-Weighted**:
- **CSA/Myelin Bright**: CSF dark, normal anatomy visualization
- **Contrast Enhancement**: Meningeal enhancement, tumors, abscesses
- **Fat Suppression**: Suppression of orbital fat, turbo inversion recovery

**T2-Weighted**:
- **CSF Bright**: Pathologies appear as hyperintense signals
- **FLAIR**: CSF suppression for periventricular lesions detection
- **Turbo Spin Echo**: Rapid T2 imaging for clinical practice

**Diffusion-Weighted Imaging (DWI)**:
- **Ischemic Stroke**: Cytotoxic edema shows hyperintensity
- **Apparent Diffusion Coefficient (ADC)**: Confirmatory hypointensity
- **Reversal of Dawn**: Diabetic hypoglycemia appearance

### Advanced MRI Techniques

**Magnetic Resonance Angiography (MRA)**:
- **Time-of-Flight**: Flow-related enhancement, arterial stenosis
- **Contrast-enhanced MRA**: Gadolinium injection, 3D reconstructions
- **92Phase Contrast**: Flow quantification, vein of Galen anomalies

**Functional MRI (fMRI)**:
- **Blood Oxygen Coping**: Active brain areas show hemoglobin changes
- **Presurgical Mapping**: Language/motor cortex identification
- **Resting State Connectivity**: Default mode network assessment

---

### Body MRI Interpretation

**Cardiac MRI**:
- **Anatomic Planes**: Short axis, long axis, four-chamber views
- **Function Assessment**: Myocardial thickening, ejection fraction
- **Viability**: Late gadolinium enhancement for scarred myocardium

**Musculoskeletal MRI**:
- **Tendon/Ligament**: Thickened/enlarged structures in tendinitis/tears
- **Bone Marrow**: Edema patterns in trauma, infection, malignancy
- **Joint Pathology**: Cartilage loss, synovitis, loose bodies

**Abdominopelvic MRI**:
- **Pelvic Anatomy**: Female reproductive organs, bladder, rectosigmoid
- **Pelvic Floor**: Sphincter assessment, hernias, bladder descent
- **Male Pelvis**: Prostate zones, seminal vesicles, lymph nodes

## 15.4 Ultrasound Imaging

### Abdominal Ultrasound

**Liver Assessment**:
- **Parenchyma**: Homogeneous echo-pattern, focal lesions
- **Vascular**: Biliary ducts, hepatic arteries, portal veins
- **Gallbladder**: Wall thickness, contents, positive sonographic Murphy

**Renal Ultrasound**:
- **Cortex/Medulla**: Improved renal parenchyma, masses
- **Collecting System**: Hydronephrosis, calculi (echogenic foci)
- **Perinephric Fat**: Soft tissue assessment

**Pelvic Ultrasound**:
- **Gynecologic**: Uterus size, endometrial thickness, ovarian follicles
- **Obstetric**: Embryo development, placenta location, gestational age
- **Male Prostate**: Prostate size, nodules, bladder outlet obstruction

### Vascular Ultrasound (Doppler)

**Color Doppler**:
- **Flow Direction**: Red (artery toward probe), blue (away from probe)
- **Pulse Wave Doppler**: Peak systolic velocity, resistive index
- **Power Doppler**: All-in-flow detection, low flow sensitivity

**Carotid Doppler**:
- **Plaque Characterization**: Ulceration, stenosis percentage
- **Velocity Measurements**: Internal carotid artery peak systolic
- **Microemboli Detection**: High-intensity transient signals

**Venous Doppler**:
- **Compressibility**: Venous thrombosis (loss of compressibility)
- **Respiratory Variation**: Caval diameter changes with respiration
- **Augmentation**: Manual compression for deep vein thrombosis

## 15.5 Interventional Radiology

### Image-guided Procedures

**Percutaneous Biopsies**:
- **Liver Biopsy**: Right intercostal approach, posterior or lateral segments
- **Lung Biopsy**: Transthoracic, pleural effusion for planning
- **Kidney Biopsy**: Lower pole approach to avoid vascular structures

**Image-guided Drainage**:
- **Abscess Drainage**: Path of least resistance, avoiding vital structures
- **Pleural Effusions**: Suprapatellar access avoiding lung trespass
- **Biliary Drainage**: PTCD for obstructed systems

### Angiographic Interventions

**Embolization Procedures**:
- **Uterine Fibroids**: Paired internal iliac arteries, secondary arteries
- **GI Bleeding**: Celiac/superior mesenteric arteries, microcatheter selectivity
- **Bronchial Arteries**: Hypervascular metastases embolization

**Angioplasty and Stenting**:
- **Renal Artery Stenosis**: Ostial stenosis, atherosclerotic plaque reduction
- **Carotid Stenosis**: Protected angioplasty to reduce stroke risk
- **Iliac Arteries**: Limb-threatening ischemia prevention

## Common Radiology Errors

### Observational Errors

**Edge of Visual Field**: Lesions at image margins
**Hidden in Fat**: Breast lesions in dense axillary fat
**Hidden in Bones**: Pineal tumors behind calcium in plain x-rays

### Interpretative Errors

**Contiguous Structures**: Hiatus hernia mimicking pneumonia
**Over-reliance on One Modality**: CT negative findings but MRI positive
**Technical Factors**: Poor timing contrast causing false appearances

### Cognitive Bias

**Availability Heuristic**: Recent experience influences current interpretation
**Anchoring Effect**: First impression prevents reassessment
**Confirmation Bias**: Evidence fits preexisting diagnosis expectation

**Prevention**:
- **Systematic Approach**: Every image read methodically
- **Comparison Views**: Previous films for temporal assessment
- **Second Opinions**: Complex cases always discussed

## Summary and CBME Integration

Radiological anatomy transforms anatomical structure into diagnostic tools through systematic image interpretation. CBME learning creates physicians who see images as precise anatomical representations requiring methodical correlation with clinical findings:

**From Anatomy to Images**: Normal structures become expected appearances on diagnostic studies
**From Pathology to Patterns**: Disease processes exhibit characteristic radiographic signatures
**From Diagnosis to Therapy**: Imaging findings guide precise interventional procedures
**From Technology to Practice**: Understanding physics enables appropriate modality selection

**CBME Key Insight**: "Radiology isn't just technical—it's precision anatomy where every pixel reveals the patient's internal story."

**Question 1**: On chest X-ray, the aortic knuckle represents the shadow of the:
A) Aortic valve cusps
B) Coronary sinus
C) Arch of aorta inflexion
D) Pulmonary outflow tract

**Answer: C** - Aortic knuckle is the apex of aortic arch curvature

**Question 2**: On abdominal CT, the inferior vena cava lies immediately to the right of the:
A) Portal vein confluence
B) Pancreatic neck
C) Coeliac axis origin
D) Abdominal aortic bifurcation

**Answer: A** - Portal vein confluence forms main portal vein crossing posteriorly to IVC
