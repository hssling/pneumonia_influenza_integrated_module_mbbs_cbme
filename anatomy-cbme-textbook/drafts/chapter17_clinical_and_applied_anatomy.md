## 17.1 Surgical Anatomy Principles

### Abdominal Wall Layers

**Surgical Approaches**:
- **Laparoscopic Entry**: Umbilical triangle (fetal septum), Palmer's point for difficult laparoscopy
- **Open Incisions**: Midline preferred for access, paramedian/minisubcostal closure reliability
- **Robotic Ports**: 8 mm umbilical camera, 8 mm working ports, careful trocar placement

**Boundaries and Spaces**:
- **Linea Alba**: Decussating aponeurotic fibers, weak in obese patients
- **Arcuate Line**: Bum incision loses posterior sheath
- **Myopectineal Orifice**: Inguinal canal reconstruction site

### Vascular Anatomy in Surgery

** ports System**:
- **Hepatic Portal Vein**: Bile duct, hepatic artery (Pringle maneuver site)
- **Caval Tributaries**: Hepatic veins drain directly to IVC
- **Retroperitoneal Vessels**: Consider in lumbar spine surgery

**Retropancreatic Structures**:
- **Splenic Vein Junction**: Portal confluence landmark
- **Superior Mesenteric Root**: Between SMA and SMV at fourth duodenum part
- **Left Renal Vein**: Anterior to aorta, posterior to SMA

## 17.2 Regional Surgical Approaches

### Head and Neck Procedures

**Carotid Endarterectomy**:
- **Branches to Avoid**: Superior thyroid, lingual, facial arteries
- **Nerve Monitoring**: Hypoglossal, vagus, superior laryngeal nerves
- **Shunting Decision**: Back pressure measurement for postoperative stroke prevention

**Thyroidectomy**:
- **Cranial Nerve Protection**: Superior laryngeal (external branch), recurrent laryngeal nerves
- **Parathyroid Preservation**: Sea star dissection for blood supply identification
- **Tracheal Deviation**: Unilateral resection requires laryngeal stabilization

### Respiratory Surgery

**Lobectomy Anatomy**:
- **Right Upper Lobe**: Bronchus crosses pulmonary artery
- **Lingular Segment**: Left upper lobe frequent atelectasis site
- **Fissure Variations**: Incomplete fissures complicate stapling

**Thoracic Incisions**:
- **Posterolateral Thoracotomy**: Rib resection between scapula borders
- **Median Sternotomy**: Retrosternal space, innominate vein care
- **Video-assisted Thoracoscopy**: Port placement for optimal exposure

### Cardiac Surgery

**Coronary Bypass Grafting**:
- **Left Anterior Descending**: Interventricular sulcus
- **Circumflex Artery**: Left atrioventricular groove
- **Right Coronary Artery**: Right atrioventricular groove

**Valve Repairs**:
- **Aortic Valve**: Sinotubular junction, coronary artery origins
- **Mitral Valve**: Annulus, chordae tendineae, papillary muscles
- **Tricuspid Valve**: Systemic venous return competence

## 17.3 Gastrointestinal Surgery

### Esophageal Surgery

**Fundoplication**:
- **Eastwood-Garrick**: Fundus secured lateral to esophagus
- **Watson Procedure**: Esophageal mobilization reduces wrap tension
- **Hill Operation**: Gastropexy through esophageal hiatus

**Esophagectomy**:
- **Transhiatal Approach**: Neck/anterior abdominal combined
- **Ivor Lewis**: Thoracotomy/celiotomy combination
- **McKeown Modification**: Neck/thorax/abdomen access

### Bariatric Surgery

**Sleeve Gastrectomy**:
- **Vascular Pedicle**: Gastroepiploic arcade preservation
- **Bougie Size**: 32-36 F maintains sleeve function
- **Staple Line**: Greater curvature resection

**Roux-en-Y Bypass**:
- **Alimentary Limb**: 100-150 cm jejunum segment
- **Biliopancreatic Limb**: Distal small bowel drainage
- **Common Channel**: Nutrient mixing site

### Colorectal Surgery

**Right Hemicolectomy**:
- **Ileocolic Pedicle**: Blood supply through SMV confluence
- **Vascular Division**: Ileocolic, right colic, right branch MCA
- **Lymph Nodes**: D3 lymphadenectomy includes ileocolic nodes

**Anterior Resection**:
- **Mesorectal Excision**: Total in mid-low rectal cancer
- **Sphincter Preservation**: 1-2 cm above dentate line safe
- **Autonomic Nerve Preservation**: Pelvic plexus integrity

## 17.4 Reproductive Surgery

### Gynecological Procedures

**Hysterectomy Approaches**:
- **Total Abdominal**: Uterine arteries/divided at cervix, cardinal-sacro-uterine ligaments
- **Vaginal Approach**: Bladder mobilization, paravaginal defects verification
- **Laparoscopic**: Round/suspensory ligaments transfusion, uterine vessels transection

**Pelvic Reconstructive Surgery**:
- **Colposuspension**: Paravaginal defect (sling through cardinal ligaments)
- **Sacrocolpopexy**: Abdominal mesh suspension (L5 visibility required)
- **Sacral Neuromodulation**: Third sacral foramen electrode placement

### Obstetric Procedures

**Cesarean Section**:
- **Lower Uterine Segment**: Better healing than classical incision
- **Bladder Mobilization**: Intervesical space development
- **Uterine Closure**: Specific stitch techniques for hemostasis

**Forceps Delivery**:
- **Fetal Head Position**: Osteologic assessment required
- **Caput vs Molded Suture**: Overlapping confirmation
- **Face Presentation**: Vertical descent noted differently

**Perineal Repair**:
- **Episiotomy Closure**: Vicryl Rapide subcutaneous, subcuticular skin
- **Anal Sphincter Repair**: Overlap technique for complete tears
- **Postrepair Care**: Delayed bowel movement for healing

## 17.5 Orthopedic Surgery

### Upper Limb Approaches

**Shoulder Arthroscopy**:
- **Portal Placement**: Posterior viewing, anterior working portals
- **Rotator Cuff Repair**: Anchor fixation in greater tuberosity
- **Labral Repair**: Suture anchor through glenoid labrum

**Carpal Tunnel Release**:
- **Incision Placement**: 2 cm proximal to distal wrist crease
- **Flexor Sheath Preservation**: Minimizes pillar pain
- **Median Nerve Care**: Microdissection near second/third webspace

### Lower Limb Surgery

**Total Knee Replacement**:
- **Patellar Tracking**: Insall-Salvati ratio tibia attachment
- **Distal Femoral Cutting**: 5-7° valgus femoral axis restoration
- **Tibial Slope**: 7° posterior slope for normal loading

**Hip Replacement**:
- **Acetabular Placement**: 45° abduction, 15° anteversion
- **Femoral Preparation**: Canal fill and alignment using templating
- **Offset Optimization**: Equal limb length for abductor function

**Anterior Cruciate Ligament Reconstruction**:
- **Graft Options**: Autogenous hamstring, patellar tendonBTB
- **Tunnel Placement**: ACL insertion footprint identification
- **Tensioning Protocol**: Extension with posterior tibial slope consideration

## 17.6 Neurological Surgery

### Cranial Surgery

**Craniotomy Approaches**:
- **Pterional Approach**: Anterior temporal/sylvian fissure exposure
- **Retrosigmoid**: Cerebellopontine angle pathology access
- **Interhemispheric**: Corpus callosum through sagittal sinus
- **Supraorbital**: Keyhole approach for anterior fossa

**Ventricular Drainage**:
- **Kocher's Point**: Middle frontal gyrus puncture
- **Dandy's Point**: Maximum convexity perpendicular entry
- **Freehand Technique**: Ultrasound-guidance preferred

### Spinal Surgery

**Microdiscectomy**:
- **Paraspinal Fascia**: Splinting fiber direction preservation
- **Lamina Exposure**: Partial or total laminectomy approach
- **Dural Incision**: Longitudinal bevelled to facilitate dural closure

**Spinal Instrumentation**:
- **Pedicle Screws**: Known landmarks for safe placement
- **Interbody Fusion**: Transforaminal lumbar interbody fusion (TLIF) exposure
- **Vertebral Column Endplate**: Subchondral bone preparation for fusion

### Peripheral Nerve Surgery

**Carpal Tunnel Release**:
- **Transverse Fascia**: Flexor retinaculum division under epineurium
- **Bifid Median Nerve**: Examine for abnormal branching
- **Branches Preservation**: Motor and cutaneous branches protection

## 17.7 Interventional Procedures

### Central Venous Access

**Internal Jugular Line**:
- **Landmarks**: Sternocleidomastoid-Sternum angle
- **Needle Direction**: Nipple of right breast at clavicle
- **Guidewire**: J-wire preferred over straight shaft
- **Chest X-ray**: Carina level confirms position

**Subclavian Line**:
- ** infraclavicular fossa**: Immediately lateral to sternoclavicular joint
- **Needle Angle**: Along clavicle toward sternal notch
- **Complications Awareness**: Pneumothorax prevention techniques

### Arterial Access

**Radial Artery Cannulation**:
- **Modified Allen Test**: Palmar arch confirmation
- **Anterolateral Approach**: Felt pulsation greatest point
- **Wire Advancement**: Gentle rotation reduces vasospasm

**Femoral Artery Access**:
- **Femoral Head Guide**: Below inguinal ligament, lateral third
- **Medial Femoral**: Safer but higher complication risk
- **Fluoroscopic Guidance**: Closure device deployment

### Joint Aspiration

**Knee Arthrocentesis**:
- **Suprapatellar Bursa**: Lateral approach above patella
- **Femoropatellar Joint**: Inferolateral parapatellar approach
- **Tibiofemoral Joint**: Superior or inferior approach consideration

**Shoulder Bursitis**:
- **Subacromial Space**: Lateral approach to acromion
- **Glenohumeral Joint**: Posterior approach with patient sitting
- **AC Joint Injection**: Direct palpation of area tenderness

## 17.8 Obstetric and Gynecologic Procedures

### Obstetric Interventions

**Amniocentesis**:
- **Axial View**: Distance from insertion site measurement
- **Placental Avoidance**: Anterior/ posterior placenta locations
- **Free Hand Technique**: Solid organ trajectories verification

**Cerclage Placement**:
- **McDonald Procedure**: Purse-string suture at internal cervical os
- **Shirodkar Method**: Caudal insertion for longer cervical coverage
- **Transvaginal Approach**: Percentile range appropriate gestational timing

### Gynecologic Office Procedures

**Endometrial Biopsy**:
- **Tenaculum Application**: Anterior lip grip for uterine stabilization
- **Sound Insertion**: Measurement recording for endometrial cavity size
- **Curette Placement**: Blind endometrial strip sampling

**Intrauterine Device Insertion**:
- **Tenaculum Use**: Cervical traction for alignment
- **Uterine Sound**: Confirm depth and direction
- **String Retrieval**: Application after IUD placement confirmation

**Colposcopic Biopsy**:
- **Transformation Zone**: Squamocolumnar junction complete visualization
- **Acetic Acid Application**: Epithelial changes identification
- **LLETZ**: Loop electrode at margin for complete resection

## 17.9 Pediatric Surgical Considerations

### Neonatal Surgery

**Neonatal Hernia Repair**:
- **Umbilical Hernia**: Natural closure in first year
- **Inguinal Hernia**: Direct canal reduction, orchidopexy assessment
- **Premature Infants**: Higher incarceration risk (immaturity)

**Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia**:
- **Bochdalek Defect**: Posterolateral most common
- **Attached Liver**: Hepaclysmic variant (worse prognosis)
- **Initial Stabilization**: ECMO preparation if severe

### Pediatric Renal Procedures

**Circumcision**:
- **Dorsal Slit**: Vaseline to protect urethra
- **Gomco Clamp**: Bell placement for distal adnexa protection
- **Post-operative Care**: Petroleum jelly dressing Moist healing

**Orchiopexy**:
- **Inguinal Approach**: Muscle-splitting incision
- **Patent Processus Vaginalis**: Ligation using silk suture
- **Dartos Layer Closure**: Cremasteric fiber inclusion

## Summary and CBME Integration

Clinical anatomy transforms anatomical theory into surgical and procedural practice. CBME learning creates physicians who bridge understanding between study diagrams and operating suites:

**From Cadaver to Operation**: Classroom anatomy becomes surgical surface anatomy
**From Text to Incision**: Understanding layers assembled for optimized approaches
**From Diagnosis to Treatment**: Anatomical correlation guides therapeutic choices
**From Surgery to Recovery**: Postoperative anatomy predicts complication patterns

**CBME Key Insight**: "Clinical procedures aren't technical maneuvers—they're high-precision anatomical surgery where every cut, clamp, and closure respects the body's hidden design."

**Competency-Based MCQs**

**Question 1**: During a laparoscopic cholecystectomy, a critically injured structure most likely indicates damage to which anatomical structure?
A) Proper hepatic artery
B) Right hepatic duct
C) Cystic artery
D) Common bile duct

**Answer: D** - Common bile duct is most frequently injured in laparoscopic cholecystectomy due to anatomical relationship variation

**Question 2**: In right hemicolectomy, the arterial supply requiring secure ligation at the superior mesenteric origin is:
A) Ileocolic artery
B) Right colic artery
C) Middle colic artery
D) Inferior pancreaticoduodenal artery

**Answer: C** - Right branch of middle colic may require ligation during extended right hemicolectomy procedures
