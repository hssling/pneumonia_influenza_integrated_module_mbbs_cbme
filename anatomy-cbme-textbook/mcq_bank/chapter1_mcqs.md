## Section A: Anatomical Terminology (25 Questions)

### Question 1 (KS/KP.1 - Knowledge Application)
**Scenario**: During a ward round, a patient asks why their "upper arm" pain radiates to the forearm. The house officer explains this using anatomical terminology.

**Question**: In anatomical terminology, the term that means "closer to the trunk or point of origin" is:
A) Distal
B) Proximal
C) Lateral
D) Medial

**Answer: B) Proximal**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: In anatomical terms, proximal refers to structures closer to the trunk or point of origin, while distal refers to structures farther away. This is clinically relevant for describing pain radiation patterns.  
**Reference**: Chapter 1.1, Anatomical Terminology

---

### Question 2 (PC.1 - Clinical Application)
**Scenario**: A surgeon is planning an incision for appendectomy.

**Question**: Which plane would the surgeon use to describe making an incision through the linea alba to access the abdominal cavity?
A) Sagittal plane
B) Coronal plane
C) Horizontal plane
D) Oblique plane

**Answer: A) Sagittal plane**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: The sagittal plane divides the body into right and left portions. The linea alba runs vertically in the midline, making it a sagittal plane incision.  
**Clinical Correlation**: Midline laparotomy is preferred for abdominal emergencies

### Question 3 (KS/KP.2 - Embryological Knowledge)
**Scenario**: A newborn is diagnosed with esophageal atresia.

**Question**: Esophageal atresia most commonly results from abnormal partitioning of which embryonic structure?
A) Primitive gut
B) Neural tube
C) Pharyngeal arches
D) Primitive streak

**Answer: A) Primitive gut**  
**Competency Level**: KS/KP.2  
**Explanation**: The esophagus develops from the primitive foregut. Abnormal separation between the trachea and esophagus during the 4-6 week embryonic period leads to esophageal atresia.  
**Reference**: Chapter 1.3, Embryological Development

## Section B: Embryological Correlations (20 Questions)

### Question 6 (KS/KP.2 - Development Principles)
**Scenario**: Understanding congenital malformations.

**Question**: Which germ layer gives rise to the nervous system and epidermis?
A) Mesoderm
B) Endoderm
C) Ectoderm
D) Extraembryonic mesoderm

**Answer: C) Ectoderm**  
**Competency Level**: KS/KP.2  
**Explanation**: The ectoderm forms the nervous system, skin epidermis, and associated structures. Neural tube defects result from ectoderm abnormalities.  
**Reference**: Chapter 1.3, Germ Layer Formation

### Question 7 (PC.1 - Clinical Embryology)
**Scenario**: Patient with situs inversus.

**Question**: Situs inversus typically results from abnormal rotation during development of which embryonic structure?
A) Heart loop
B) Gut loop
C) Neural tube
D) Primitive streak

**Answer: B) Gut loop**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: Abnormal rotation of the gut loop during embryonic development leads to situs inversus, where abdominal organs are mirrored.  
**Clinical Application**: Must consider anatomical variations in surgery

## Section C: Surface Anatomy and Landmarks (20 Questions)

### Question 11 (PC.1 - Surface Marking)
**Scenario**: Central venous catheter insertion.

**Question**: Which surface landmark is used as the superior boundary for identifying the position of the suprasternal notch?
A) Sternal angle
B) Xiphisternal junction
C) Clavicles
D) Costal margin

**Answer: A) Sternal angle**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: The sternal angle marks the junction of manubrium and body of sternum, serving as an important surface landmark for central line placement.  
**Clinical Application**: Incorrect identification can lead to procedural complications

---

### Question 12 (PC.1 - Palpation)
**Scenario**: Cardiovascular examination.

**Question**: During cardiac auscultation, the point of maximal impulse (PMI) in a normal adult is usually located at the:
A) 2nd intercostal space, left sternal border
B) 4th intercostal space, midclavicular line
C) 5th intercostal space, anterior axillary line
D) Xiphisternal junction

**Answer: B) 4th intercostal space, midclavicular line**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: The PMI is typically palpable in the 4th-5th intercostal space at the midclavicular line in adults.  
**Reference**: Chapter 1.6, Surface Anatomy

---

### Question 13 (PC.1 - Imaging Correlation)
**Scenario**: Chest X-ray interpretation.

**Question**: On a PA chest X-ray, the aortic shadow (knuckle) is normally visualized:
A) To the right of the thoracic vertebra
B) To the left of the thoracic vertebra
C) Superior to the pulmonary trunk
D) Inferior to the left atrial appendage

**Answer: A) To the right of the thoracic vertebra**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: The aortic knuckle is the radiographic shadow of the aortic arch, visible to the right of the thoracic spine on PA views.  
**Clinical Correlation**: Enlargement indicates aortic pathology

## Section E: Clinical Integration (20 Questions)

### Question 21 (PC.1 - Case-Based Learning)
**Scenario**: Patient with acute abdominal pain suspected appendicitis.

**Question**: The anatomical basis for McBurney's point tenderness in appendicitis is that the appendix is normally located at:
A) The intersection of the right midclavicular line and a line joining the anterior superior iliac spines (spino-umbilical line)
B) The junction of the midclavicular line and the subcostal margin
C) The midline just below the umbilicus
D) The left iliac fossa

**Answer: A) The intersection of the right midclavicular line and a line joining the anterior superior iliac spines**  
**Competency Level**: PC.1  
**Explanation**: McBurney's point is a surface landmark corresponding to the base of the appendix, vital for clinical diagnosis of appendicitis.  
**Clinical Application**: Point tenderness here suggests appendicitis

### Question 22 (KS/KP.2 - Embryological Pathology)
**Scenario**: Newborn with congenital diaphragmatic hernia.

**Question**: Congenital diaphragmatic hernia most commonly results from failure of which embryological process?
A) Neural crest migration
B) Septum formation
C) Pleuroperitoneal membrane closure
D) Gut rotation

**Answer: C) Pleuroperitoneal membrane closure**  
**Competency Level**: KS/KP.2  
**Explanation**: The diaphragm forms from pleuroperitoneal membranes, septum transversum, and body wall. Incomplete closure leads to congenital diaphragmatic hernia.  
**Reference**: Chapter 1.3, Embryology
