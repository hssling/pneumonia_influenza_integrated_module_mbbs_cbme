# Malnutrition CBME Training Module

## Competency-Based Medical Education Unit

### Title: Comprehensive Malnutrition Management for MBBS and PG Students

This module provides comprehensive teaching and learning resources on malnutrition for medical students following CBME guidelines.

## Learning Objectives

At the end of this module, students should be able to:

1. **Define and classify malnutrition** using WHO standards and understand the different types
2. **Identify etiological factors** contributing to malnutrition at individual, household, and community levels
3. **Conduct comprehensive nutritional assessment** including anthropometric, clinical, and biochemical evaluation
4. **Diagnose and classify** different forms of malnutrition (PEM, Marasmus, Kwashiorkor, etc.)
5. **Develop and implement** management protocols for malnutrition cases
6. **Apply preventive strategies** at individual, community, and population levels
7. **Address micronutrient deficiencies** including Vitamin A, Iron, Iodine, and Zinc deficiencies

- MBBS Phase III students (Community Medicine posting)

This module aligns with NMC CBME curriculum competencies for:
- **Community Medicine**: Nutrition and Malnutrition management
- **Pediatrics**: Childhood malnutrition assessment
- **Internal Medicine**: Adult malnutrition in clinical practice

1. **Formative Assessment**: MCQs, case-based discussions
2. **Summative Assessment**: End-module written examination
3. **OSCE**: Nutritional assessment skills demonstration
4. **Practicum**: Community-based malnutrition screening
