# Nutritional Assessment Guide

## COMPETENCY-BASED MEDICAL EDUCATION

**Subject:** Community Medicine/Pediatrics  
**Module:** Malnutrition CBME Training  
**Level:** MBBS Phase III Students

---

## **STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE TO NUTRITIONAL ASSESSMENT**

### **1. PREPARATION**
- **Equipment Required:**
  - Digital weighing scale (0.1 kg accuracy)
  - Stadiometer/infantometer for height/length
  - Color-coded MUAC tape
  - Child Growth Standards charts
  - Disposable gloves, masking tape
- **Patient Preparation:**
  - Explain procedure to mother/child
  - Remove heavy clothing/shoes
  - Ensure privacy and comfort
- **Calibration Check:**
  - Zero weighing scale daily
  - Verify MUAC tape at 0 cm

---

### **2. ANTHROPOMETRIC MEASUREMENTS**

#### **WEIGHT MEASUREMENT**
```
Steps:
1. Place the child on the scale (mothers help for infants)
2. Ensure minimal clothing, no diaper
3. Read scale to nearest 0.1 kg
4. Repeat measurement for accuracy

Recording: Weight = ______ kg
Age of Child = ______ months/years
Date = ______
```

#### **HEIGHT/LENGTH MEASUREMENT**
```
Steps:
1. Child stands erect against wall (or supine for <2 years)
2. Head in Frankfort plane
3. Measurements taken to nearest 0.1 cm

Recording: Height = ______ cm
Measurement Type: Standing/Supine
```

#### **MID-UPPER ARM CIRCUMFERENCE (MUAC)**
```
Steps:
1. Expose left arm completely
2. Locate midpoint between shoulder and elbow
3. Color-coded tape: Loose fitting, flat tape
4. Read with zero point visible

Recording: MUAC = ______ cm
Color Zone: RED/YELLOW/GREEN
```

#### **HEAD CIRCUMFERENCE (0-36 months)**
```
Steps:
1. Measure around largest circumference
2. Above brows, around fullest part
3. Record to nearest 0.01 cm

Recording: Head Circumference = ______ cm
```

---

### **3. INTERPRETATION USING WHO GROWTH STANDARDS**

#### **CALCULATION OF Z-SCORES**

**Weight-for-Age (WFA):**
- Z-score = (Observed weight - Expected weight) / Standard deviation
- **Interpretation:**
```
-3 to -2 SD = Moderate underweight
-3 SD or below = Severe underweight
```

**Height-for-Age (HFA):**
- Z-score = (Observed height - Expected height) / Standard deviation
- **Interpretation:**
```
-2 SD or below = Stunting
```

**Weight-for-Height (WFH):**
- Z-score = (Observed weight - Expected weight for height) / Standard deviation
- **Interpretation:**
```
-2 SD or below = Wasting
```

#### **MUAC CATEGORIES**
```
RED ZONE: <11.5 cm = Severe acute malnutrition
YELLOW ZONE: 11.5-12.5 cm = Moderate acute malnutrition
GREEN ZONE: >12.5 cm = Normal
```

### **4. CLINICAL ASSESSMENT**

#### **GENERAL SIGNIFICANCE OF PHYSICAL SIGNS**

| **Sign** | **Significance** | **Commonly Seen In** |
|----------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Edema** | Hypoalbuminemia | Kwashiorkor, PEM |
| **Wasting** | Starvation | Marasmus, chronic poverty |
| **Flag sign hair** | Protein deficiency | Kwashiorkor |
| **Baggy pants** | Fat loss | Marasmus |
| **Xerosis** | Vitamin A deficiency | Severe malnutrition |
| **Koilonychia** | Iron deficiency | Anaemia |

#### **VITAMIN DEFICIENCY SIGNS**

- **Night Blindness:** Vitamin A deficiency
- **Angular Cheilitis:** Iron/Vitamin B12 deficiency
- **Goiter:** Iodine deficiency
- **Koilonychia:** Iron deficiency
- **Rickets:** Vitamin D deficiency
- **Scurvy:** Vitamin C deficiency

### **5. DIETARY ASSESSMENT**

#### **24-HOUR DIETARY RECALL**
```
Recall Procedure:
1. Yesterday morning foods?
2. Yesterday afternoon foods?
3. Yesterday evening foods?
4. What time was the last feed?

BE SPECIFIC: Include quantities, preparation methods
Note: These are estimates of usual intake
```

#### **FOOD FREQUENCY QUESTIONNAIRE**
```
Sample Questions:
- Was the child exclusively breastfed for 6 months? Yes/No
- How many times rice/cereals consumed yesterday?
- How many times milk/dairy consumed yesterday?
- How many times fruits/vegetables consumed yesterday?
- How many times pulses/beans consumed yesterday?
- How many times meat/eggs consumed yesterday?

Scoring: 0-2 points per food group, Total food consumption score
```

#### **HOUSEHOLD FOOD SECURITY QUESTIONS**
```
1. During the last 30 days, did you worry about food shortage? Yes/No
2. Did you skip meals or eat less than you wanted? Yes/No
3. Did you eat fewer types of food than you needed? Yes/No
4. Did you have to borrow or steal food? Yes/No
5. Did any family member go to bed hungry? Yes/No

Score: 0-5, Higher score = more food insecure
```

### **7. REFERRAL CRITERIA**

#### **URGENT REFERRAL (Immediately)**
- Severe acute malnutrition (MUAC <11.5 cm OR edema)
- Hypoglycemia (blood sugar <54 mg/dl)
- Severe dehydration or shock
- Complicated infections with malnutrition

#### **PRIORITY REFERRAL (< 3 days)**
- Moderate acute malnutrition with complications
- Failure to gain weight despite home management
- Severe anemia or other deficiency signs
- Child >3 years with severe malnutrition

#### **ROUTINE REFERRAL (< 1 week)**
- Moderate malnutrition without complications
- Prevention counseling for high-risk cases
- Nutritional support for vulnerable families

### **8. COMMONLY ASKED QUESTIONS**

#### **Q: Why repeat measurements?**
A: Some children are fearful, repeat measurements ensure accuracy.

#### **Q: What if the child won't stand still?**
A: For height, wait calmly. If very uncooperative, take length lying down (adjust recording).

#### **Q: Why measure MUAC on left arm?**
A: Standard practice for consistency. Use right arm only if left affected.

#### **Q: What if no growth chart available?**
A: Can use local MUAC criteria for screening, or smartphone apps with growth charts.

#### **Q: How to calculate age accurately?**
A: Use exact date of birth. For doubtful cases, ask for vaccination card.

---

### **9. QUALITY ASSURANCE**

#### **Measurement Standards:**
- Weighing scale calibrated weekly
- Stadiometers checked monthly
- MUAC tapes replaced when faded
- Growth charts updated annually

#### **Training Requirements:**
- Annual refresher training
- Inter-observer variation <5%
- Competency assessment quarterly
- Supervision during first 3 months

---

### **10. PRACTICAL TIPS FOR STUDENTS**

#### **Building Rapport:**
- Smile and talk to child in local language
- Involve mother/helper in the process
- Praise the child to reduce anxiety

#### **Measurement Accuracy:**
- Shirt removed for both weight and MUAC
- Child standing tall for height
- MUAC tape neither too loose nor too tight

#### **Data Reliability:**
- Double-check z-score calculations
- Verify age calculation
- Use consistent units throughout

#### **Family Counseling:**
- Start with positive findings
- Use local terminology
- End with concrete action plan

---

*Remember: Nutritional assessment is both a science and an art. Accurate measurements lead to correct diagnosis and appropriate management, ultimately saving lives.*

**Reference:** WHO Child Growth Standards (2006), IAP Manual on Nutrition
