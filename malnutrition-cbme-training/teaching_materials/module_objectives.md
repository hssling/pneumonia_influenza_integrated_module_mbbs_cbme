### **By the end of this 2-week module, MBBS students will be able to:**

## 🎯 **Patient Care Competencies (PC)**

### **PC1: Nutritional Assessment in Community Settings**
- **Competency Statement**: Conduct comprehensive nutritional assessment including anthropometric, clinical, and dietary evaluations
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Measure and interpret weight-for-age, height-for-age, and weight-for-height z-scores
  - Assess mid-upper arm circumference (MUAC) for acute malnutrition screening
  - Perform clinical examination for nutritional deficiency signs
  - Document nutritional assessment findings accurately using WHO growth charts

### **PC2: Patient and Family Counseling**
- **Competency Statement**: Provide culturally appropriate nutrition education and counseling to families of malnourished children
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Explain malnutrition causes and consequences in simple language
  - Demonstrate preparation of nutrient-dense, affordable local foods
  - Teach feeding practices for infants and young children
  - Counsel mothers on exclusive breastfeeding and complementary feeding

### **PC3: Multidisciplinary Malnutrition Management**
- **Competency Statement**: Coordinate comprehensive management of malnutrition involving multiple healthcare providers and community resources
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Identify cases requiring hospitalization vs community-based management
  - Collaborate with ASHA workers for follow-up and monitoring
  - Coordinate with local Anganwadi workers for nutritional support
  - Refer complicated cases to tertiary centers with proper documentation

### **PC4: Micronutrient Deficiency Management**
- **Competency Statement**: Screen for and manage common micronutrient deficiencies in community settings
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Perform clinical assessment for Vitamin A, Iron, Iodine, and Zinc deficiencies
  - Administer vitamin A supplementation as per national guidelines
  - Recognize and treat iron deficiency anemia
  - Educate on iodized salt usage and goiter prevention

---

## 🧠 **Medical Knowledge Competencies (MK)**

### **MK1: Malnutrition Pathophysiology**
- **Competency Statement**: Explain the pathophysiology and clinical classification of various forms of malnutrition
- **Knowledge Elements**:
  - Protein-energy malnutrition (PEM) mechanisms
  - Kwashiorkor vs Marasmus physiological differences
  - Micronutrient deficiency syndromes
  - Nutritional adaptation and metabolic changes in starvation

### **MK2: Epidemiology and Risk Factors**
- **Competency Statement**: Identify epidemiological patterns and risk factors for malnutrition in Indian context
- **Knowledge Elements**:
  - National and regional malnutrition prevalence data
  - Socioeconomic determinants of malnutrition
  - Seasonal patterns and climate change impacts
  - Vulnerable groups: infants, pregnant women, elderly, tribal populations

### **MK3: Malnutrition Management Protocols**
- **Competency Statement**: Apply evidence-based management protocols for different types of malnutrition
- **Knowledge Elements**:
  - WHO guidelines for severe acute malnutrition management
  - National guidelines for moderate malnutrition treatment
  - Micronutrient supplementation protocols
  - Feeding rehabilitation principles and practices

### **MK4: Complications Recognition**
- **Competency Statement**: Recognize complications of malnutrition and provide appropriate interventions
- **Knowledge Elements**:
  - Cardiac complications in severe malnutrition
  - Immune deficiency and infection vulnerability
  - Growth faltering and developmental delays
  - Refeeding syndrome pathophysiology and prevention

---

## 📚 **Practice-Based Learning Competencies (PBLI)**

### **PBLI1: Evidence-Based Nutritional Practice**
- **Competency Statement**: Apply evidence-based approaches to nutritional assessment and management decisions
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Search and critically appraise nutrition research literature
  - Apply WHO growth standards and malnutrition classification systems
  - Participate in community nutrition program evaluations
  - Implement quality improvement initiatives for malnutrition reduction

### **PBLI2: Self-Directed Learning**
- **Competency Statement**: Engage in continuous self-directed learning through community nutrition encounters
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Maintain case portfolio of malnutrition assessments
  - Set learning goals based on community experiences
  - Reflect on personal biases in nutritional counseling

---

## 💬 **Communication Skills Competencies (CS)**

### **CS1: Effective Patient Communication**
- **Competency Statement**: Communicate nutritional information effectively with patients and families from diverse backgrounds
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Use simple, non-technical language for nutritional education
  - Demonstrate feeding demonstrations using local foods
  - Employ teach-back method to verify understanding
  - Show empathy while discussing malnutrition consequences

### **CS2: Community and Stakeholder Engagement**
- **Competency Statement**: Engage communities and stakeholders in malnutrition prevention and control
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Conduct nutrition education sessions for community groups
  - Collaborate with local leaders for community nutrition programs
  - Work with school teachers for adolescent nutrition education
  - Organize community meetings to address malnutrition issues

### **CS3: Interdisciplinary Communication**
- **Competency Statement**: Communicate effectively with healthcare team members in malnutrition management
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Present malnutrition cases in pediatric and community medicine forums
  - Document referrals clearly using standardized formats
  - Collaborate with dietitians and pediatricians
  - Coordinate with ICDS program functionaries

---

## 👨‍⚕️ **Professionalism Competencies (PRO)**

### **PRO1: Ethical Practice in Nutrition**
- **Competency Statement**: Practice nutrition care ethically with attention to vulnerable populations
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Obtain informed consent for nutritional interventions
  - Respect cultural food practices while promoting nutritional health
  - Maintain patient confidentiality in community settings
  - Advocate for food security and nutritional rights

### **PRO2: Cultural Competence**
- **Competency Statement**: Provide culturally sensitive nutritional care to diverse populations
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Adapt nutritional education for local languages and literacy
  - Respect traditional food beliefs while promoting balanced nutrition
  - Recognize caste, religion, and socioeconomic factors affecting nutrition
  - Adapt care for tribal and rural populations

---

## 🏥 **Systems-Based Practice Competencies (SBP)**

### **SBP1: Healthcare System Navigation**
- **Competency Statement**: Navigate Indian health systems to optimize malnutrition management
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Access ICDS services for supplementary nutrition
  - Utilize government schemes for malnutrition treatment
  - Coordinate between PHC, CHC, and malnutrition treatment centers
  - Guide families through MDM (Mid-Day Meal) scheme benefits

### **SBP2: Resource Management**
- **Competency Statement**: Optimize resource utilization in malnutrition prevention and treatment
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Select cost-effective nutritional interventions
  - Prioritize severe cases in resource-limited settings
  - Monitor program effectiveness using community data
  - Advise on locally available food resources

### **SBP3: Community Nutrition Programs**
- **Competency Statement**: Participate in community-level malnutrition prevention programs
- **Observable Behaviors**:
  - Support village health nutrition days (VHND) activities
  - Conduct malnutrition screening camps
  - Monitor growth of children under ICDS program
  - Evaluate community nutrition intervention outcomes

---

## 📊 **Assessment Methods**

### **Formative Assessment:**
- **Portfolio Review**: Weekly documentation of malnutrition assessments
- **Skills Checklist**: Observed performance of nutritional assessment competencies
- **Case Presentations**: Presentation of complex malnutrition cases
- **Community Reports**: Documentation of community nutrition activities

### **Summative Assessment:**
- **OSCE Stations**: Testing nutritional assessment skills in simulated scenarios
- **Portfolio Defense**: Presentation of comprehensive malnutrition care experience
- **Written Examination**: Testing theoretical knowledge and clinical decision-making
- **Community Nutrition Project**: Planning and executing community intervention

---

## 🎯 **Exit Criteria - Mastery Level**

**Students must demonstrate competency at level 3 or 4 (shows/supervision required to does/autonomous performance) on the following scale:**

1. **Knowledge** (Aware of facts/principles)
2. **Comprehension** (Understands facts/relationships)
3. **Application** (Applies knowledge in familiar situations)
4. **Analysis** (Breaks information into components)
5. **Synthesis** (Integrates information into new understanding)
6. **Evaluation** (Makes judgments based on criteria)

## 📈 **Essential Learning Activities**

### **Community-Based Learning:**
- Growth monitoring and malnutrition screening at Anganwadi centers
- Household nutritional assessments in urban slums and rural villages
- Participation in village health nutrition days (VHND)
- Nutritional rehabilitation center visits

### **Clinical Learning:**
- Ward rounds and case discussions in nutrition rehabilitation units
- Outpatient department clinics for malnutrition cases
- Pediatric ward nutritional assessment experience
- Counseling sessions for malnourished children and mothers

### **Lab and Skill-Based Learning:**
- Anthropometric measurements practice sessions
- MUAC measurement and interpretation
- Dietary assessment and food frequency questionnaires
- Growth chart plotting and interpretation
