3. **Conduct malnutrition assessment** in elderly and geriatric populations

5. **Manage nutritional screening** in palliative care and terminal illness

## Malnutrition Assessment in Hospitals

### **Hospital Malnutrition Screening Tools**

#### **Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool (MUST)**
```
Step 1: BMI Score
- >20.0: 0 points
- 18.5-20.0: 1 point
- <18.5: 2 points

Step 2: Unplanned Weight Loss
- <5%: 0 points
- 5-10%: 1 point
- >10%: 2 points

Step 3: Acute Disease Effect
- No acute disease: 0 points
- Acute disease but eating: 1 point
- Acute disease and no nutritional intake >5 days: 2 points

Total Score Interpretation:
- 0 points: Low risk
- 1 point: Medium risk
- 2+ points: High risk (refer to dietitian)
```

#### **Nutritional Risk Screening 2002 (NRS-2002)**
```
Nutrition Status Assessment:
- BMI <18.5: 1 point
- Weight loss >5% in 3 months: 1 point
- Food intake <50-75% normal: 1 point

Severity of Disease:
- Hip fracture, chronic disease: 1 point
- Major abdominal surgery, head injury, bone marrow transplantation: 2 points
- Intensive care (>9 kcal/kg/min): 2-3 points

Total Score:
- ≥3: High risk, start nutritional support
- Weekly re-screening in hospital

Age Adjustment:
- >70 years: +1 point for total score
```

### **Mini Nutritional Assessment (MNA) for Elderly**

#### **Screening Questions**
```
A. Food Intake Decline
- Has food intake declined over past 3 months?
- Weight loss during past 3 months?
- Mobility impairment?

B. General Assessment
- Lives independently? (no =2, yes=0)
- Takes ≥3 prescription drugs? (yes=1)
- Pressure sores or skin ulcers? (yes=1)

C. Subjective Assessment
- Health status vs peers? (not as good=0.5)
- Neuropsychological problems? (yes=2)
- BMI? (23=0, <21=3, 21-22.9=1)
```

#### **Anthropometric Measurements**
```
Arm Circumference: <21 cm = malnourished
Calf Circumference: <31 cm = malnourished
Mid-arm muscle circumference: Formula calculation

Laboratory Markers:
- Albumin: <35 g/L = protein malnutrition
- Lymphocyte count: <800/µL = immune deficit
- Transferrin: <200 mg/dL = protein deficit
```

## Perioperative Nutritional Assessment

### **Preoperative Risk Assessment**

#### **Surgical Patient Screening**
```
Preoperative Factors:
1. Malnutrition (MNA Score <17)
2. Low BMI (<18.5 kg/m²)
3. Unintended weight loss >10-15%
4. Serum albumin <30 g/L
5. Poor functional status

Scoring System:
- 0-2 factors: Low risk
- 3-4 factors: Intermediate risk
- 5 factors: High risk

Recommendations:
- Low risk: Standard care
- Intermediate risk: Oral supplements 7-10 days preop
- High risk: Consider enteral feeding
```

#### **Cancer Surgery Assessment**
```
Cancer-Specific Risk Factors:
- Head/neck/upper GI tumors: Highest risk (45-60% malnutrition)
- Weight loss >10%: Poor prognostic factor
- Sarcopenia: Independent risk factor
- Pre-albumin <18 mg/dL: 3-fold increased complications

Nutritional Support Timeline:
- 7-14 days preop: Immuno-enhancing formulas
- 30 minutes preop: High-protein drink
- Postop: Early enteral feeding within 24 hours
```

#### **Emergency Surgery Considerations**
```
Assessment Time: Limited - use rapid tools

Key Indicators:
- MUAC <15th percentile: Severe depletion
- Grip strength <20 kg: Sarcopenia
- Unplanned admission: Consider nutritional compromise

Interventions:
- Consider？
- Postop early feeding (within 6 hours)
- TPN if unable to eat for >5 days
```

## Intensive Care Nutritional Assessment

### **Critical Illness Nutrition Scoring**

#### **Modified Nutrition Risk in Critically Ill (mNUTRIC) Score**
```
Components:
1. Age: <50=0, 50-74=1, ≥75=2
2. APACHE II score: <15=0, 15-19=1, 20-27=2, ≥28=3
3. SOFA score: 0-1=0, 2-3=1, 4-5=2, ≥6=3
4. Comorbidities: 0=0, 1-2=1, ≥3=2
5. Days hospitalized: <1=0, 1-3=1, ≥4=2
6. IL-6 (if measured): <400=0, ≥400=1

Total Score:
- ≥5: High nutrition risk, aggressive feeding
- <5: Standard protocol
```

#### **ICU Enteral Feeding Assessment**
```
Gastric Residual Volume (GRV):
- <500 mL: Continue feeding
- 500-1000 mL: Give prokinetics, monitor
- >1000 mL: Hold feeding, assess tolerance

Tube Placement Verification:
- Initial X-ray confirmation
- Daily residual volume monitoring
- Gastric pH testing
- CO2 detection with air insufflation
```

### **Metabolic Monitoring in ICU**
```
Indirect Calorimetry Necessity:
- Obesity, severe burns, respiratory failure
- Measured energy expenditure ±20% estimated
- First 24-48 hours after admission
- Recalibration every 3-7 days

Blood Glucose Monitoring:
- Target 140-180 mg/dL (moderate targets)
- Avoid hypoglycemia (<60 mg/dL)
- Sliding scale insulin for hyperglycemia

Electrolyte Balance:
- Phosphate supplementation prophylaxis
- Magnesium replacement monitoring
- Potassium correction with ECG monitoring
```

## Neonatal and Infant Nutritional Assessment

### **Neonatal Nutrition Risk Screening**

#### **Skinfolds and Muscle Mass**
```
Triceps Skinfold (birth): 4-6 mm normal
Subscapular Skinfold: 4-5 mm adequate fat stores
Mid-arm circumference: >9 cm at birth, >10.5 cm at weeks

Growth Velocity:
- First 3 months: 30 g/day weight gain
- 3-6 months: 20-25 g/day
- Birth weight doubling: By 4-5 months
- Tripling: By 12 months

Head Circumference Velocity:
- 2 cm/month first 3 months
- 1 cm/month 3-6 months
- <25 cm at 12 months concerning
```

#### **Feeding Assessment in Neonates**
```
Breastfeeding Establishment:
- Latches within first hour
- Successful feeding within 24 hours
- Adequate urine output (>6 wet diapers/day)
- Stool passage, jaundice monitoring

Bottle Feeding Assessment:
- 1-2 mL/kg/minute feeding rate
- Nipple size appropriate for gestational age
- Burp efficiency, reflux signs

Enteral Feeding Cessation:
- Full feeds after 2-3 days
- Weight regain before discharge
- Successful oral feeding
- Caregiver confidence assessment
```

## Premature Infant Nutritional Assessment

### **Postnatal Age Calculation**
```
Chronological Age vs Corrected Age:
- Chronological: From birth date
- Corrected: Chronological minus weeks premature

Example: Infant born at 32 weeks, now 3 months old
- Chronological age: 3 months
- Corrected age: 3 - 1 = 2 months (4 weeks lost)

Growth Charts:
- Corrected age for first 2 years
- Use preterm-specific growth charts
- Plot parallel to 50th percentile, not crossing
```

#### **Extrauterine Growth Restriction (EUGR)**
```
Definition:
- Weight <10th percentile at 36 weeks PMA
- Length <10th percentile at 40 weeks
- Head circumference <10ths percentile at 36 weeks

Risk Factors:
- SGA at birth
- Prolonged ventilator support
- Inadequate nutritional management
- Prolonged hyperbilirubinemia

Prevention and Management:
- Early postnatal growth optimization
- Individualized nutrient intake targets
- Post-discharge nutritional support
- Regular growth monitoring
```

### **Parenteral Nutrition Assessment**
```
Indications:
- Feeding intolerance
- GI malformations
- Prolonged fasting

Energy Requirements:
- 80-100 kcal/kg/day term infants
- 110-130 kcal/kg/day preterm
- Glucose infusion rate: 4-8 mg/kg/min

Protein Requirements:
- 3-4 g/100 kcal for preterm infants
- Amino acid solutions: 1.5-2.5 g/kg/day

Complication Monitoring:
- Sepsis from central lines
- Metabolic acidosis, cholestasis
- Osteopenia, electrolyte imbalances
```

## Older Adult Nutritional Assessment

### **Comprehensive Geriatric Assessment**

#### **Functional Assessment**
```
ADLs and IADLs:
- Eating independently
- Cooking and shopping
- Swallowing and chewing assessment
- Dentition and oral health status

Mobility Assessment:
- Gait speedrometry (<0.6 m/s = sarcopenia)
- Chair stand test (>10 seconds abnormal)
- Tinetti balance scale assessment
```

#### **Sarcopenia Screening**
```
EWGSOP2 Criteria:
1. Low muscle strength (grip strength)
   - Women: <16 kg
   - Men: <30 kg

2. Low muscle quantity or quality
   - DXA BMC <1 SD below young mean
   - CT/MRI appendicular lean mass
   - Bioelectric impedance assessment

Severity:
- No sarcopenia
- Probable sarcopenia (low strength)
- Confirmed sarcopenia (low strength + mass)
- Severe sarcopenia (all three + physical performance)
```

#### **Geriatric Malnutrition Nutrition**
```
Mini Nutritional Assessment (MNA) Components:
- Decreased food intake <25%
- Weight loss >3 kg/year
- Mobility impairment
- Acute disease/psychological stress
- BMI <19-21 kg/m²
- Calf circumference <31 cm

Malnutrition Diagnosis:
- MNA <17 = Malnourished (GERIA Score)
- MNA 17-23.5 = At risk
- MNA ≥24 = Normal
```

## Palliative Care Nutritional Assessment

### **Malnutrition Assessment in Advanced Disease**

#### **Patient-Generated Subjective Global Assessment (PG-SGA)**
```
Four-Part Assessment:
1. Weight History:
   - Weight change over past 6 months
   - Percentage vs usual body weight

2. Food Intake:
   - Current intake vs normal intake
   - Orange Tree fruit vs coconut intake

3. Symptoms:
   - Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
   - Pain, appetite, taste changes
   - Dry mouth, dysphagia

4. Functional Capacity:
   - Normal - no special needs
   - Reduced - ambulant without help
   - Bedridden or wheelchair bound

Scoring: 0-4 points each (total 0-44)
Grade: A=0-3, B=4-8, C=9+
```

#### **Cachexia Assessment**
```
Cachexia Diagnostic Criteria:
- Weight loss >5%
- BMI <20 or Sarcopenia
- Anorexia or inflammation (CRP>5 mg/L)

Assessment Tools:
- MUST (Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool)
- MST (Malnutrition Screening Tool)
- SNAQ (Short Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire)
- NRS-2002 with age adjustment
```

#### **Nutritional Goals in Palliative Care**
```
Focus Areas:
- Quality of life improvement
- Symptom management
- Maintenance of function
- Avoidance of dehydration
- Preservation of dignity

Interventions in Advanced Disease:
- Small, frequent meals
- Liquid nutritional supplements
- Taste enhancement
- Enteral feeding in select cases
- Parenteral nutrition rarely
```

## Mental Health and Nutrition Assessment

### **Eating Disorders Assessment**

#### **ANOREXIA NERVOSA**
```
Clinical Features:
- BMI <17.5 or <85% expected for age
- Intense weight control behaviors
- Body image distortion
- Amenorrhea >3 cycles

Nutritional Assessment:
- Caloric intake history
- Restrictive vs binge/purge patterns
- Vitamin K/P deficiencies
- Bone mineral density assessment
```

#### **BULIMIA NERVOSA**
```
Physical Signs:
- Erosion of dental enamel
- Russell's sign (knuckle scarring)
- Enlarged salivary glands
- Electrolyte abnormalities

Nutritional Assessment:
- Binge frequency and intake
- Vomiting self-induction
- Purgative use and frequency
- Dental and swallowing evaluation
```

### **Depression and Appetite Changes**
```
Assessment Components:
- Appetite questionnaire
- BMI and recent weight changes
- Nutritional intake history
- Medication-nutrient interactions

Management Integration:
- Antidepressants affecting appetite
- Nutritional counseling alongside therapy
- Social support for meal preparation
- Physical activity encouragement
```

## Summary

Special scenarios require adapted nutritional assessment methods considering underlying clinical conditions. Hospitalized patients need rapid screening with validated tools like MUST and NRS-2002. Critical care patients require intensive metabolic monitoring. Neonates and preterm infants need corrected age assessment and specialized growth tracking. Elderly populations benefit from comprehensive geriatric nutritional assessment. Palliative care focuses on quality of life rather than aggressive nutritional intervention. Each scenario demands integration of nutritional assessment with clinical condition management.
