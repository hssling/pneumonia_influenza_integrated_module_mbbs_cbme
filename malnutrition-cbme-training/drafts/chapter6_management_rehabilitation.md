## Principles of Malnutrition Management

### **WHO 10-Step Management Protocol**

The inpatient management of SAM follows a standardized 10-step protocol to ensure systematic care and prevent complications.

#### **Step 1: Treatment of Hypoglycaemia**
```
Emergency treatment:
- Hypoglycaemia = blood glucose <54 mg/dl
- Give 50 mL 10% glucose solution ORALLY immediately
- If unable to feed, give 5 mL/kg 10% glucose IV bolus
- If shock present, treat as sepsis (antibiotics first!)
```

#### **Step 2: Treatment of Hypothermia**
```
Core temperature <35.5°C = emergency
Immediate actions:
- Remove wet clothing
- Provide warm environment (28-32°C)
- Skin-to-skin contact with mother
- Warm blankets and radiant heaters
- NG feeding if unable to suck
```

#### **Step 3: Treatment of Dehydration**
```
Assess for dehydration:
- No dehydration: None/two skin folds
- Some dehydration: Irritable/restless, skin pinch slow
- Severe dehydration: Lethargic, unconscious, poor skin elasticity

Management:
- Oral rehydration for mild/moderate
- IV fluids for severe (do NOT use standard cholera plan!)
- Give 5 mL/kg/hour for first 2 hours
- F-75 feeds can be given simultaneously
```

#### **Step 4: Treatment of Electrolyte Imbalance**
- Usually corrects with nutritional recovery
- Monitor potassium levels (initial hyponatremia common)
- No routine electrolyte supplementation initially

#### **Step 5: Treatment of Infections**
```
Antibiotic Coverage (First 2 days):
- Ampicillin: 50 mg/kg IV/IM every 6 hours
- Gentamicin: 7.5 mg/kg IV/IM once daily
- Switch to oral when feeding well

Cover Malaria/Measles in endemic areas
Monitor for fungal infections in prolonged treatment
```

#### **Step 6: Treatment of Micronutrient Deficiencies**
```
Daily Vitamins and Minerals (beyond F-75):
- Vitamin A: 1,000 IU/day (or prophylactic 200,000 IU single dose)
- Folate: 160 µg/day
- Iron: 3 mg/kg/day (after stabilization)
- Vitamin C: 25 mg/day
- Zinc: 2 mg/kg/day
- Copper: 0.3 mg/kg/day
- Magnesium: 0.4 mg/kg/day
```

#### **Step 7: Initiating Feeding**
```
Start with F-75 milk formula:
- Caloric density: 75 kcal/100 mL
- Protein: 0.9 g/100 mL
- Preparation: 42 g powder in 170 mL water = 500 mL formula

Initiation:
- Start at 30 mL/kg/day (Session 1)
- Increase 30 mL/kg/day each session
- Target: 130 mL/kg/day by day 2
- Frequency: Every 2-4 hours around the clock
```

#### **Step 8: Achieving Catch-up Growth**

Transition to F-100 when:
- Appetite returns (full quota being eaten)
- No vomiting/nausea for 1-2 days
- Started stool becomes normal
- No pitting edema for 3 days

**F-100 characteristics:**
```
- Caloric density: 100 kcal/100 mL
- Protein: 2.9 g/100 mL
- Target: Weight gain ≥10 g/kg/day
- Specialized formula with whey protein caseinate ratio
```

#### **Step 9: Providing Sensory Stimulation**
```
Multidisciplinary approach:
- Emotional and neurological development
- Mother-child interaction encouraged
- Developmental milestones assessment
- Ongoing medical care for pneumonia/TB etc.
```

#### **Step 10: Preparing for Follow-up**
```
Discharge Criteria:
- Maintaining good appetite for 3 days
- Steady weight gain for 3 consecutive days
- No edema for 10 days
- Infection signs/regress for 7 days

Follow-up Plan:
- Weekly clinic visits for 4 weeks
- Monthly visits next 5 months
- Continued supplementation
- Immunization completion
```

## Nutrition Rehabilitation in Clinical Settings

### **Hospital-Based Management**

#### **Nutrition Rehabilitation Centers (NRC)**
```
India has 1,800+ NRCs with capacity for 800,000 cases/year
Located in: Medical colleges, district hospitals, dedicated centers

Components:
- Outpatient management for moderate malnutrition
- Inpatient care for severe cases
- Follow-up and prevention counseling
- Mother-child bonding and emotional support
```

#### **Individualized Care Plans**

**Case Example - 18-month-old with Kwashiorkor:**
```
Day 1-2: Stabilization
- F-75: 80 mL/kg/day (six feeds)
- Ampicillin + Gentamicin
- Treat comorbid pneumonia
- Monitor vital signs hourly

Day 3-5: Transition to F-100
- Gradually increase volume
- Achieve 15-20 g/kg/day weight gain
- Vitamin/mineral supplementation

Week 2-4: Rehabilitation
- Nutritional counseling for mother
- Complementary feeding education
- Growth monitoring
- Developmental stimulation

Discharge at 6 weeks:
- Catch-up growth achieved
- Family educated
- Follow-up arranged
```

### **Community-Based Management Program (CBMP)**

#### **Program Structure**
```
Target: Children 6-59 months
Coverage: 90% of villages
Personnel: ASHA, AWW, ANM work together

Monthly Pharma Kosh at Anganwadi:
- Weighing all children
- MUAC measurement
- Individual counseling
- Referral of SAM cases

RUTF Distribution Points:
- Stable children on outpatient treatment
- Weekly follow-up visits
- Family counseling sessions
```

#### **RUTF (Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food)**
```
Composition (per 92g sachet):
- Energy: 500 kcal
- Protein: 12-13 g
- Micronutrients: Vitamins A,C,D,E + Minerals
- Shelf life: 24 months without refrigeration

Dosage:
- Children 6-11 months: 260 kcal/day (half packet)
- Children 12-24 months: 500 kcal/day (one packet)
- Children 24-59 months: 1,000 kcal/day (two packets)

Administration: 2-3 times daily with family meals
```

#### **Success Indicators**
```
Recovery Rate: ≥75%
Default Rate: ≤15%
Mortality Rate: ≤3%
Weight Gain: ≥10 g/kg/day
```

## Management of Specific Conditions

### **Kwashiorkor Management**

#### **Edema-Specific Considerations**
```
Fluid Management:
- Restrict protein initially to prevent edema exacerbation
- Start feeding slowly to avoid sudden protein load
- Monitor serum albumin levels
- Handle fluids carefully - cardiac overload risk

Rehydration Protocol (Kwashiorkor):
- Half-strength oral rehydration solution
- 75 mL/kg over first 4 hours
- Supplement with potassium chloride
- Monitor for seizures (hypokalemia)
```

#### **Infection Prevention**
```
- Prolonged antibiotic course (7-10 days)
- Antifungal prophylaxis
- Infection control in severely edematous skin
- Nasogastric feeding if adequate oral intake impossible
```

### **Marasmic-Kwashiorkor Management**

#### **Highest Mortality Risk Group**
```
Exceptional Care Required:
- Intensive monitoring for first 72 hours
- Gradual protein introduction
- Multiple antibiotic coverage
- Emotional support essential
- Prolonged inpatient stay (6-8 weeks)
```

#### **Monitoring Parameters**
```
Hourly vital signs for first 48 hours
Daily weight checks (2x daily)
Strict input/output records
Biochemical monitoring (electrolytes, proteins)
Appetite testing before formula increments
```

## Complication Management

### **Refeeding Syndrome**

#### **Prevention**
```
- Start feeding at 30-50 kcal/kg/day (not maximum calories)
- Gradual increase over 5-7 days
- Supplement magnesium, phosphate, potassium from day 1
- Monitor serum electrolytes every 24 hours initially

Detect Signs:
- Lethargy, confusion, seizures
- Cardiac arrhythmias
- Respiratory failure
- Death in severe cases
```

#### **Treatment**
```
Electrolyte Supplementation:
- Phosphate: Intravenous if <1 mg/dL
- Magnesium: If symptomatic hypocalcemia
- Potassium: With ECG monitoring
- Vitamins: Multi-vitamin preparations

Feeding Adjustment:
- Temporary reduction in energy intake
- NG feeding if bulbar weakness
- Close cardiac and respiratory monitoring
```

### **Infections and Immune Deficiency**

#### **Bacterial Infections**
```
Common pathogens: Pneumococci, E. coli, Salmonella, Klebsiella

Management:
- Ampicillin + Gentamicin as first line
- Metronidazole for anaerobes
- Switch to oral when stabilized
- Prophylactic antibiotics not recommended
```

#### **Fungal Infections**
```
Increased risk in prolonged treatment
Prevention: Nystatin prophylaxis
Treatment: Systemic antifungals (fluconazole)
Monitoring: Daily inspection of mouth and intertriginous areas
```

### **Heart Failure**

#### **Detection**
```
Signs of cardiac overload:
- Tachycardia >120/min
- Hepatojugular reflux
- Gallop rhythm
- Peripheral edema

Prevention:
- Gradual rehydration
- Slow calorie advancement
- Daily cardiac monitoring
- Treat infections promptly
```

#### **Management**
```
- Restrict IV fluids
- Digitalis therapy
- Oxygen supplementation
- Adjust feeding volumes
- Consider transfer to higher center
```

## Follow-up and Monitoring

### **Short-Term Follow-up (4 weeks post-admission)**
```
Weekly visits to nearest health facility
- Weight monitoring and plotting
- Appetite assessment
- Any complications reporting
- Continued supplementation
- Counseling reinforcement
```

### **Medium-Term Follow-up (2-6 months)**
```
Monthly clinic visits
- Growth velocity assessment
- Developmental milestone evaluation
- Immunization completion
- Family nutrition improvement
- Behavior change reinforcement
```

### **Long-Term Care (6-24 months)**
```
Nutritional surveillance
- Growth monitoring (weight/height)
- Prevent relapse episodes
- Early intervention for any illness
- Community integration
- School readiness programs
```

## Summary

Malnutrition management requires a systematic approach following standardized protocols, with careful monitoring for complications. The WHO 10-step protocol provides a framework for inpatient care, while community-based management extends treatment to stable cases. Prevention of relapse and long-term nutritional support are essential components of comprehensive malnutrition management.
