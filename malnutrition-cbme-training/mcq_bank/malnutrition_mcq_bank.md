## Chapter 1: Introduction to Malnutrition and Epidemiology

### **Single Best Answer Questions**

#### **Question 1**
According to WHO, malnutrition refers to deficiencies or excesses in:
A) Energy intake only
B) Protein intake only
C) Nutrient intake, imbalance of essential nutrients or impaired nutrient utilization
D) Vitamin intake only

**Correct Answer:** C) Nutrient intake, imbalance of essential nutrients or impaired nutrient utilization

**Explanation:** Malnutrition encompasses undernutrition, overnutrition, and micronutrient deficiencies.

#### **Question 2**
Which of the following statements about global malnutrition burden is correct?
A) 50 million children have wasting worldwide
B) 200 million children worldwide are stunted
C) Malnutrition contributes to 45% of deaths in children under 5
D) Micronutrient deficiencies affect 1 billion people

**Correct Answer:** C) Malnutrition contributes to 45% of deaths in children under 5

**Explanation:** According to WHO, malnutrition contributes to 45% of deaths in children under 5, affecting 2 billion people globally.

#### **Question 3**
In India, according to NFHS-5, child stunting rate is:
A) 25.5%
B) 32.1%
C) 35.5%
D) 40.5%

**Correct Answer:** C) 35.5%

**Explanation:** NFHS-5 (2019-21) reports 35.5% stunting, 19.3% wasting, and 32.1% underweight among children under 5.

#### **Question 4**
Which of the following is NOT a consequence of malnutrition?
A) Reduced immunity
B) Impaired cognitive development
C) Increased longevity
D) Economic productivity loss

**Correct Answer:** C) Increased longevity

**Explanation:** Malnutrition leads to reduced immunity, cognitive impairment, and economic losses, not increased longevity.

## Chapter 2: Classification and Assessment

### **Clinical Scenario Based Questions**

#### **Question 5**
A child has height-for-age z-score of -2.8. This indicates:
A) Acute malnutrition
B) Severe acute malnutrition
C) Chronic malnutrition
D) Overnutrition

**Correct Answer:** C) Chronic malnutrition

**Explanation:** Height-for-age z-score assesses chronic/prior malnutrition, weight-for-age and weight-for-height assess acute malnutrition.

#### **Question 6**
Mid-upper arm circumference (MUAC) <11.5 cm indicates:
A) Moderate acute malnutrition
B) Severe acute malnutrition
C) Overweight
D) Normal nutrition

**Correct Answer:** B) Severe acute malnutrition

**Explanation:** MUAC <11.5 cm (red zone) indicates severe acute malnutrition in children 6-59 months.

#### **Question 7**
Which BMI category is defined as 25.0-29.9 kg/m²?
A) Underweight
B) Normal weight
C) Overweight
D) Obese

**Correct Answer:** C) Overweight

**Explanation:** Overweight is BMI 25.0-29.9, obesity ≥30.0, underweight <18.5.

#### **Question 8**
Serum albumin <3.5 g/dl in a child indicates:
A) Iron deficiency
B) Protein malnutrition
C) Vitamin C deficiency
D) Overnutrition

**Correct Answer:** B) Protein malnutrition

**Explanation:** Serum albumin <3.5 g/dl is indicative of protein malnutrition (PEM).

## Chapter 3: Etiology and Risk Factors

### **Application-Based Questions**

#### **Question 9**
Which of the following is an immediate cause of malnutrition?
A) Poverty
B) Inadequate dietary intake
C) Food insecurity
D) Poor maternal education

**Correct Answer:** B) Inadequate dietary intake

**Explanation:** Inadequate dietary intake is an immediate cause; poverty is a basic cause, food insecurity is underlying cause.

#### **Question 10**
The interaction between malnutrition and infection creates a:
A) Synergistic cycle
B) Independent process
C) One-way relationship
D) Temporary effect

**Correct Answer:** A) Synergistic cycle

**Explanation:** Infection reduces appetite and nutrient absorption, malnutrition reduces immunity - creating a vicious cycle.

#### **Question 11**
Which vulnerable group has the highest risk of malnutrition in India?
A) Children 6-24 months
B) School-aged children
C) Working adults
D) Elderly above 60 years

**Correct Answer:** A) Children 6-24 months

**Explanation:** The critical window of malnutrition is during complementary feeding period (6-24 months).

#### **Question 12**
Basic causes of malnutrition according to WHO framework include all except:
A) Political instability
B) Food availability in market
C) Limited resources
D) Cultural factors

**Correct Answer:** B) Food availability in market

**Explanation:** Food availability in market is an underlying cause; political/cultural factors are basic causes.

## Chapter 4: Protein-Energy Malnutrition

### **Case-Based Questions**

#### **Question 13**
A 12-month-old child presents with severe wasting, no edema, loose skin over buttocks, and depressed temples. This is most likely:
A) Marasmus
B) Kwashiorkor
C) Marasmic-kwashiorkor
D) Mild undernutrition

**Correct Answer:** A) Marasmus

**Explanation:** Severe wasting without edema is characteristic of marasmus.

#### **Question 14**
Flag sign (alternating bands of normal and dyspigmented hair) is characteristically seen in:
A) Marasmus
B) Kwashiorkor
C) Iron deficiency
D) Vitamin D deficiency

**Correct Answer:** B) Kwashiorkor

**Explanation:** Flag sign hair is a classic feature of kwashiorkor due to protein deficiency.

#### **Question 15**
In the management of severe acute malnutrition, the stabilization phase uses:
A) F-100 formula
B) F-75 formula
C) Ready-to-use therapeutic food
D) Supplementary nutrition programs

**Correct Answer:** B) F-75 formula

**Explanation:** F-75 (75 kcal/100ml) is used in stabilization phase; F-100 for rehabilitation.

#### **Question 16**
Refeeding syndrome is characterized by all except:
A) Phosphate depletion
B) Potassium depletion
C) Hyponatremia
D) Magnesium depletion

**Correct Answer:** C) Hyponatremia

**Explanation:** Refeeding syndrome involves phosphate, potassium, magnesium depletion; hyponatremia is common in kwashiorkor.

## Clinical Scenarios and Management

### **Scenario 1 - Marasmus Management**
A 10-month-old child weighing 5 kg (expected weight 9 kg) presents with severe wasting. No edema. Laboratory: Albumin 2.1 g/dl, glucose 45 mg/dl, hemoglobin 7.2 g/dl.

#### **Question 17**
Immediate management priority is:
A) Vitamin supplementation
B) Hypoglycemia correction
C) Iron therapy
D) Growth hormone therapy

**Correct Answer:** B) Hypoglycemia correction

**Explanation:** Hypoglycemia (<54 mg/dl) is a medical emergency and requires immediate treatment.

#### **Question 18**
Management approach follows which protocol?
A) WHO SAM protocol
B) IAP malnutrition management
C) CDC growth monitoring
D) UNICEF vitamin A supplementation

**Correct Answer:** A) WHO SAM protocol

**Explanation:** WHO Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) protocol is the standard for managing severe malnutrition cases.

### **Scenario 2 - Kwashiorkor Management**
An 18-month-old child presents with generalized edema, hypoalbuminemia (1.6 g/dl), and flaky dermatitis. No severe wasting.

#### **Question 19**
Characteristic feature supporting diagnosis is:
A) Moon facies with edema
B) Severe muscle wasting
C) Visible bony prominences
D) Orange hair color

**Correct Answer:** A) Moon facies with edema

**Explanation:** Edema with moon facies is characteristic of kwashiorkor; wasting and bony prominences suggest marasmus.

#### **Question 20**
Potential complication to watch for is:
A) Hypothermia
B) Cardiac failure during rehydration
C) Vitamin A toxicity
D) Iron overload

**Correct Answer:** B) Cardiac failure during rehydration

**Explanation:** Careless fluid administration can cause cardiac failure due to cardiac muscle weakening.

## Micronutrient Deficiencies

#### **Question 21**
Night blindness occurs due to deficiency of:
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin B complex
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin D

**Correct Answer:** A) Vitamin A

**Explanation:** Vitamin A deficiency causes night blindness, Bitot's spots, and corneal xerosis.

#### **Question 22**
Angular cheilitis, koilonychia, and atrophic glossitis are signs of:
A) Iron deficiency
B) Vitamin B12 deficiency
C) Folic acid deficiency
D) All of the above

**Correct Answer:** D) All of the above

**Explanation:** All these deficiencies can present with similar oral and dermal manifestations.

#### **Question 23**
Goiter is caused by deficiency of:
A) Iron
B) Iodine
C) Zinc
D) Vitamin C

**Correct Answer:** B) Iodine

**Explanation:** Iodine deficiency causes goiter, cretinism, and hypothyroidism.

#### **Question 24**
Which micronutrient deficiency increases susceptibility to infections?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Zinc

**Correct Answer:** D) Zinc

**Explanation:** Zinc deficiency particularly affects immune function and wound healing.

## Calculation-Based Questions

#### **Question 37**
A child weighing 8kg has height 75cm. BMI calculation is:
A) 14.2 kg/m²
B) 10.7 kg/m²
C) 18.3 kg/m²
D) 22.4 kg/m²

**Correct Answer:** A) 14.2 kg/m²

**Explanation:** BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)² = 8 / (0.75)² = 8 / 0.5625 = 14.2

#### **Question 38**
Weight deficit percentage for a child weighing 6kg when expected weight is 10kg is:
A) 40%
B) 60%
C) 50%
D) 30%

**Correct Answer:** A) 40%

**Explanation:** Weight deficit = [(Expected - Actual)/Expected] × 100 = [(10-6)/10] × 100 = 40%

## Case Study - Extended Matching Questions

#### **Question 41 - Case Study**
A 16-month-old child presents with generalized pitting edema, flaky skin lesions, sparse hair with flag sign, and hepatomegaly. Albumin is 1.5 g/dl.

Diagnosis is most likely:
A) Marasmus
B) Kwashiorkor
C) Marasmic-kwashiorkor
D) Moderate malnutrition

**Correct Answer:** B) Kwashiorkor

**Explanation:** Edema, flaky dermatitis, flag sign hair, hepatomegaly, hypoalbuminemia are all features of kwashiorkor.

#### **Question 42 - Management**
For the child in Q41, initial management includes all except:
A) F-75 formula
B) Careful rehydration
C) Broad spectrum antibiotics
D) High protein diet immediately

**Correct Answer:** D) High protein diet immediately

**Explanation:** Avoid high protein initially due to risk of refeeding complications; start with low protein-energy feeds.

#### **Question 43 - Prevention**
To prevent recurrence in similar cases, immediate action needed:
A) Increase family income
B) Improve maternal education
C) Promote optimal complementary feeding
D) Construct better housing

**Correct Answer:** C) Promote optimal complementary feeding

**Explanation:** Immediate prevention focuses on feeding practices; socioeconomic factors need longer-term interventions.
