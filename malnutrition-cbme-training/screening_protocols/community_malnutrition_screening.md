# Community Malnutrition Screening Protocols

## COMPETENCY-BASED MEDICAL EDUCATION

**Subject:** Community Medicine/Pediatrics  
**Module:** Malnutrition CBME Training  
**Target Personnel:** ASHA Workers, ANM, Medical Students

---

## **PROTOCOL 1: ROUTINE MALNUTRITION SCREENING AT ANGANWADI CENTERS**

### **Frequency & Coverage**
- **Monthly screening** for children aged 6-59 months
- **Weekly weighing** for high-risk children
- **Coverage target:** 100% registered children
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours per center

---

### **STEP 1: PREPARATION (15 minutes)**

#### **Equipment Check**
- [ ] Digital weighing scales (calibrated)
- [ ] Color-coded MUAC tapes (clean, functional)
- [ ] Growth charts posted on walls
- [ ] Register for weight recordings
- [ ] Health cards, referral slips ready
- [ ] Hand sanitizer, soap, clean water

#### **Documentation Preparation**
- Previous month's weight records
- High-risk children identified
- Missing children list prepared
- Reporting format ready

---

### **STEP 2: CHILD REGISTRATION (30 minutes)**

#### **Identification**
- Child's name and mother's name
- Age in months (verify with MCP card)
- Address, contact information
- Previous nutritional status

#### **Health Status**
- Current illness (diarrhea, cough, fever)
- Immunization status
- Recent hospital visits
- Family planning status

---

### **STEP 3: ANTHROPOMETRIC SCREENING (60 minutes)**

#### **WEIGHT MEASUREMENT PROCEDURE**
```
1. Greet mother and child warmly
2. Explain procedure in local language
3. Remove shoes, heavy clothing, diaper
4. Weigh to nearest 0.1 kg
5. Record weight in growth chart
📝 Weight: ______ kg | Date: ______
```

#### **MID-UPPER ARM CIRCUMFERENCE (MUAC)**
```
1. Explain to mother about nutritional status check
2. Expose child's left arm completely
3. Find midpoint between shoulder tip and elbow
4. Wrap tape around arm - NEITHER TIGHT NOR LOOSE
5. Read where tape crosses zero point
📝 MUAC: ______ cm | Color Zone: ____________
```

#### **INTERPRETING RESULTS**
```
RED ZONE (< 11.5 cm): SEVERE ACUTE MALNUTRITION
● IMMEDIATE REFERRAL TO NRC
● Start RUTF if available
● Inform supervisor immediately

YELLOW ZONE (11.5 - 12.5 cm): MODERATE ACUTE MALNUTRITION
● Supplementary feeding at Anganwadi
● Weekly follow-up
● Nutrition counseling to mother

GREEN ZONE (> 12.5 cm): NORMAL
● Continue routine care
● Health education
● Monthly monitoring
```

---

### **STEP 4: GROWTH MONITORING (30 minutes)**

#### **PLOTTING ON GROWTH CHART**
```
1. Find child's age in months on x-axis
2. Plot weight on y-axis
3. Draw line connecting previous weights
4. Compare with growth channel

Normal: Weight following red line
Warning: Approaching yellow line
Urgent: Weight dropped to yellow line
```

#### **GROWTH RATES**
- **Children <1 year:** Should gain 500-1000g/month
- **Children 1-2 years:** Should gain 200-300g/month
- **Children 2-5 years:** Should gain 100-200g/month

---

### **STEP 5: CLINICAL ASSESSMENT (30 minutes)**

#### **SIGNIFICANCE OF PHYSICAL SIGNS**
```
Edema (+ pitting): Signs of Kwashiorkor
● Pitting edema on feet, legs, face
● Generalised swelling
● IMMEDIATE REFERRAL

Muscle Wasting: Signs of Marasmus
● Visible ribs, prominent bones
● Loose skin over thighs
● IMMEDIATE REFERRAL

Vitamin Deficiency Signs:
● Night blindness: Vitamin A deficiency
● Angular stomatitis: Vitamin B deficiency
● Pale conjunctiva: Anaemia

Severe Signs Requiring Emergency Referral:
● Sunken eyes, no tears when crying
● Skin pinch slow to recover
● Child lethargic, unable to sit/stand
● Fast or slow breathing
```

---

### **STEP 6: REFERRAL AND MANAGEMENT (20 minutes)**

#### **REFERRAL CATEGORIES**

**URGENT REFERRAL (Same day):**
- Severe acute malnutrition (MUAC <11.5 cm)
- Generalised edema
- Complicated pneumonia, severe dehydration
- Hypoglycemia symptoms (fits, unconsciousness)

**ROUTINE REFERRAL (Within 1 week):**
- Moderate acute malnutrition
- Stunting detected for first time
- Persistent illness with weight loss

**LOCAL MANAGEMENT ACTIONS:**
- Nutrition counseling to mothers
- Exclusive breastfeeding advice
- Healthy complementary feeding guidance
- Hygiene and sanitation education
- Immunization status check

---

### **STEP 7: FAMILY COUNSELING (20 minutes)**

#### **ESSENTIAL MESSAGES**
```
Exclusive Breastfeeding (0-6 months):
● Only breast milk - no water, formula, or food
● Feed on demand - day and night
● Benefits: Protection from infections, optimal growth

Complementary Feeding (6-24 months):
● Start with semisolid food at 6 months
● Continue breastfeeding for 2+ years
● Family foods with extra nutrients
● Feed 4-5 times per day + 2 snacks

Feeding During Illness:
● Continue breastfeeding even during diarrhea
● Give frequent small feeds
● Homemade oral rehydration solution (ORS)
● Seek medical help early

Iron-Rich Foods:
● Green leafy vegetables (palak, methi)
● Cereals with jaggery
● Nuts, dry fruits, meat, fish
● Iron syrup supplementation

Recognizing Hunger Signs:
● Banging table, fussiness, thrusting tongue
● Time interval since last feed
● Child's sleep-wake patterns
● Mothers should recognize and respond
```

## **PROTOCOL 2: OUTBREAK INVESTIGATION**

### **STEP 1: DETECTING INCREASED CASES**
- Weekly SAM cases >3 per Anganwadi center
- Stunting rates >40% in area
- Cluster of similar symptoms in one area
- Multiple referrals from same area

### **STEP 2: RAPID ASSESSMENT**
- House-to-house survey in affected area
- Anthropometric screening of all children
- Food security assessment in households
- Environmental assessment (water, sanitation)

### **STEP 3: EPIDEMIOLOGICAL INVESTIGATION**
- Case definitions for malnutrition types
- Vulnerable groups identification
- Risk factors analysis using WHO framework
- Community meetings for local factors

### **STEP 4: IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION**
- Supplementary feeding centers
- RUTF distribution for SAM cases
- Health education campaigns
- Water and sanitation improvements

- **Services:** Growth monitoring, immunization, nutrition counseling

## **QUALITY ASSURANCE PROTOCOL**

### **EQUIPMENT STANDARDS**
```
Weighing Scales:
● Calibrated every month
● Battery charged, zero checked
● Clean surface, adequate light

MUAC Tapes:
● Color zones clear, tape intact
● Cleaned with alcohol daily
● Replaced when color fades

Growth Charts:
● Posted visibly on walls
● Adequate numbers, clean
● Updated regularly
```

### **STAFF TRAINING STANDARDS**
```
Monthly Training Requirements:
● 1 hour theory on malnutrition
● 30 min practical session
● 30 min case discussions
● Competency assessment quarterly

Supervisor Checks:
● 10% random verification
● Inter-observer variation <5%
● Accuracy against gold standard
● Feedback and correction
```
